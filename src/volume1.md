# Seven Short Stories about Gabu

## The Morning Sun

Gabu is a small Arabesque Greek tortoise. He is one year old. Every morning, he wakes up slowly. He does not run. He walks to a sunny spot in the garden. He sits under the warm sun for a long time. He blinks his dark eyes and feels happy. For Gabu, a good morning starts very slowly.

## The Big Green Leaf

Gabu sees a big green leaf in the grass. It looks delicious. But Gabu does not rush. He stops and looks at the leaf. Is it safe? Is there a bug on it? He waits for two minutes. Then, he takes one small step, and then another step. Finally, he eats the leaf very quietly.

## A Warm Puddle

It is afternoon. The sun is very hot, and it warms the garden. Gabu finds a small, shallow puddle of water near a big rock. The sun makes the water nice and warm. Gabu loves warm water! He walks into the puddle very slowly. He sits in the warm water and closes his eyes. He stays there for twenty minutes. The warm puddle makes his shell clean and his body happy.

## The Scary Red Ball

A red ball rolls near Gabu. Thump! Gabu stops immediately. He is a very careful boy. He pulls his head, his legs, and his tail inside his hard shell. He stays inside his shell like a quiet rock. He waits until the ball stops moving. Only then, he slowly peeks out.

## Step by Step

Gabu wants to go to the other side of the flowerbed. There are small stones on the path. Gabu looks at every stone. He puts his front foot down carefully. Then he moves his back foot. Step... step... step. He moves like a slow clock. He enjoys his peaceful walk.

## The Evening Nap

The sun goes down, and the garden becomes cool. Gabu knows it is time to sleep. He looks for a safe place. He finds a pile of soft, dry leaves under a big plant. He crawls under the leaves very slowly. He covers his shell and goes to sleep happily.

## The New Grass

Today, there is new, sweet grass near the porch. Gabu wants to try it. He walks toward the grass, but he hears a loud bird in the tree. Gabu stops. He waits and listens carefully. When the bird flies away, Gabu walks again. He is always safe and never in a hurry.
