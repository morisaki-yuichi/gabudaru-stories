Spring had arrived, and the garden was full of colour. The days were getting longer and warmer, and Gabu and Daru had both grown a little since the autumn. They were almost exactly the same size now, which Daru found very exciting and Gabu found perfectly fine.

After their frightening experience in the storm beyond the fence, Gabu had become even more thoughtful about new situations. Daru, on the other hand, was still always searching for something interesting to discover. But this time, they didn't have to go looking for adventure — adventure came running right into their garden.


## The Arrival of Dango


It was a quiet Tuesday morning, and Gabu and Daru were eating fresh dandelion leaves near the porch. Then, without any warning, the ground began to vibrate. A moment later, an explosion of happy barking echoed through the garden.

"Woof! Woof! Thump, thump, thump!"

Before either of them could pull their heads into their shells, a ball of orange and white fur came charging around the corner of the house. It was a puppy with a long body, very short legs, enormous ears, and a fluffy little tail that was spinning like a propeller.

It was Dango — the new Corgi who had just moved in next door.

Dango ran straight up to them, his tongue flopping out and his whole body wiggling with excitement.

"Hello! Oh wow! What are you two? Are you rocks? You look like rocks, but you're moving! Moving rocks!" Dango barked, jumping back and forth and sniffing their faces.

Gabu stepped back slowly. "I am not a rock," he said, with great patience. "My name is Gabu. Could you please calm down a little?"

But Daru was already stretching his neck out as far as it could go, trying to get a better look at this extraordinary creature.

"I'm Daru! What kind of animal are you? Your legs are so short!"

"I'm Dango! I love running and balls and sniffing things and — can you run? Let's run!"

"We prefer to walk," Daru said, grinning. "But not quickly."

For the next hour, Dango followed them everywhere they went. He seemed to find their slow, steady movements absolutely fascinating. Every time Gabu took a single step forward, Dango ran three full circles around him, then flopped down and waited — and then did it again. He was very noisy and completely unpredictable. But Gabu and Daru could both see that he had a kind heart and simply wanted company.


## A Terrible Loss


A few weeks later, Dango's boundless energy caused an unexpected problem.

During a long stretch of hot weather, Gabu and Daru had been spending their afternoons resting in the cool, damp earth near the old compost heap. The shade there was the best in the whole garden.

Dango came to visit, as he did every single day. Almost immediately, he began digging a hole in the earth nearby. Digging was his favourite activity, and he was very good at it. The dirt flew out behind him in a steady stream.

"Look at the size of this hole! It's getting deeper!" Dango barked happily.

"That's impressive, Dango," Gabu said, edging slightly away from the flying dirt. "But please try to be a little more careful."

The heavy heat of the afternoon made everyone feel drowsy. Gabu and Daru retreated into their shells for a long nap, and Dango curled up in the shade of a nearby bush.

When Daru woke up, the sun had moved far across the sky. He looked over at Gabu, who was still sleeping quietly. Then something made him look more carefully at Gabu's shell.

He stared. He moved closer. He looked again.

A few weeks earlier, Gabu had found a small, smooth blue stone near the water bowl. It had caught the light in a beautiful way, and Gabu had fixed it carefully to the top of his shell using sticky tree sap. He had been very proud of it.

But now — the stone was gone. Just an empty spot remained where it had been.

Gabu woke up slowly and saw Daru leaning over him with a strange expression.

"What are you doing? That tickles."

"Gabu... your blue stone. It isn't there anymore," Daru said carefully.

Gabu twisted and turned, trying to see his own back, but it was impossible.

"What? It can't be gone! I had it for nearly a month! Where did it go?"


## The Search Party


Dango had heard their voices and came bounding over from his bush.

"What's going on? Are we solving a mystery? I love mysteries!"

"It isn't a fun mystery, Dango," Gabu said, looking sad. "My blue stone fell off my shell while I was sleeping, and now I can't find it anywhere."

Dango's ears shot straight up. He looked serious for the first time in his life.

"Don't worry, Gabu. I haven't told many animals this, but I have a special talent." He tapped his wet black nose with one paw. "This nose can find anything. Anything at all."

The three of them began to search. Dango worked with his nose pressed to the ground, following invisible trails. Gabu and Daru moved slowly and carefully through the grass, examining every spot.

They searched near the compost heap, under every large leaf, around the water bowl, and along the fence. But as the sky started to turn orange and the light began to fade, they still had nothing. Gabu was very quiet, which was always a sign that he was upset.

"What if it's underground?" Daru said suddenly. He was looking at the hole Dango had dug earlier.

Dango froze.

"Oh no," he said, in a very small voice. "When I was digging... do you think I buried it by mistake?"

He ran to his hole without waiting for an answer. But this time, instead of digging fast, he worked slowly and gently, using his paws to move the dirt aside in careful layers. Gabu and Daru watched in silence.

After several minutes, Dango stopped. He lowered his nose to one particular spot and stayed very still. Then he nudged the earth softly. A tiny flash of blue caught what was left of the evening light.

"I found it! It's here!" Dango barked, and his tail became a blur.


## True Friendship


Daru carefully picked up the blue stone and carried it to Gabu. But when they looked at it, they could see that the old tree sap had worn away completely. The stone wouldn't stay on Gabu's shell anymore without being fixed again.

Gabu held the stone for a long moment. Then he looked at Dango, who was sitting very still with his ears flattened and his eyes full of apology.

"I'm so sorry, Gabu," Dango said. "I didn't mean to. I was just so excited about digging, and I wasn't paying attention."

"I know you didn't mean it," Gabu said, and he smiled. "And you found it again with your remarkable nose. That makes you a very good detective, Dango."

Then Gabu turned to look at Daru, and something shifted in his expression — as if he had just had an idea that felt exactly right.

"Actually, I've been thinking. I don't need to wear this stone on my shell. What if we placed it at the entrance to our home under the porch? Then all three of us could see it every morning."

Daru's eyes lit up immediately.

"Yes! That's perfect. It belongs to all of us."

They placed the blue stone carefully at the door of the small, cosy space beneath the porch where Gabu and Daru slept each night. When the moonlight found it later that evening, the stone shone like a tiny lamp in the dark.
