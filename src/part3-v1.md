# The Giant in the Garden and the Missing Shell
Spring had arrived in the garden, bringing warmer days and a colorful blanket of flowers. Gabu and Daru were now older, wiser, and a bit larger. Since their dangerous experience in the storm, Gabu had learned to respect Daru’s caution, while Daru had grown a little more curious about the world outside their immediate neighborhood.

However, they didn’t need to go looking for adventure this time, because adventure—in a very loud, energetic form—came directly to them.

## The Arrival of Dango
It happened on a quiet Tuesday morning. The brothers were enjoying a breakfast of fresh dandelion leaves when the ground suddenly began to vibrate. A loud, joyful barking echoed across the lawn, followed by the sound of rushing paws.

“Woof! Woof! Thump, thump, thump!”

Before the tortoises could even pull their heads into their shells, a blur of orange and white fur came charging around the corner of the house. It was a puppy—but a very strange-looking one. He had a long, sturdy body, incredibly short legs, large erect ears that looked like radar dishes, and absolutely no tail to speak of.

It was Dango, the neighbor’s new Pembroke Welsh Corgi.

Dango slid to a halt right in front of Gabu, his tongue hanging out of his mouth as he panted happily. His entire backside wiggled back and forth in excitement.

“Hello! Wow! What are you? Are you rocks? Moving rocks? That is amazing!” Dango barked rapidly, his voice full of energy. He bounced from side to side, sniffing Gabu’s nose.

Gabu, who usually considered himself the brave one, blinked in shock. “I am not a rock,” he said stiffly. “I am Gabu, a tortoise. And this is my brother, Daru.”

“Tortoise! Tor-toise!” Dango repeated, testing the word. “I’m Dango! I like running, I like balls, I like sticks, and I like you! Do you want to play fetch? Can you run?”

Daru carefully peeked out from his shell. “We can walk,” he offered gently. “But we are not very good at running.”

For the next hour, Dango followed the brothers everywhere. He was fascinated by their slow pace. Every time Gabu took a step, Dango would circle him three times, bark encouragement, and then lie down on his belly, waiting for them to catch up. Although he was loud and a bit overwhelming, the tortoises realized that Dango was incredibly friendly and had no intention of hurting them.

## A Terrible Loss
A few weeks later, Dango’s high energy became a real problem.

The weather had become quite hot, so Gabu and Daru decided to spend the afternoon near the cool, damp earth by the compost heap. It was their favorite spot to escape the heat.

Dango, who had become a daily visitor, was busy digging a hole nearby. Corgis love to dig, and Dango was particularly proud of his ability to move dirt at lightning speed.

“Look how deep this hole is!” Dango barked, showers of dirt flying behind him.

“That’s nice, Dango, but please be careful,” Daru warned, moving a few inches away to protect his eyes from the dust.

Eventually, the afternoon heat made everyone sleepy. The tortoises tucked themselves into their shells for a long nap, and Dango curled up in the shade of a nearby bush.

When Daru woke up a few hours later, the sun was setting. He stretched his neck out and looked around. Gabu was still asleep nearby. But as Daru looked closer at his brother, his heart skipped a beat.

Gabu’s shell was covered in beautiful, natural patterns that looked like small wooden shields. But right in the center of his upper shell, there was a smooth, empty space. His favorite decorative item—a beautiful, shiny blue glass stone that he had found weeks ago and stuck to his shell with hardened sap—was gone.

Gabu woke up as Daru was inspecting him. “What are you doing, Daru? That tickles.”

“Gabu... your blue stone. It’s missing,” Daru said with concern.

Gabu reached back as far as he could, but of course, he couldn’t see his own back. “What? No! I’ve had that stone for a month! It makes me look special! Where is it?”

## The Search Party
Hearing the commotion, Dango trotted over, yawning widely. “What’s wrong, friends? Are we playing a game?”

“It’s not a game, Dango,” Gabu said sadly. “My shiny blue stone fell off my shell while we were sleeping, and now it’s gone.”

Dango’s ears perked up instantly. “A mystery! A search mission! Don’t worry, Gabu. I have a secret weapon.” He tapped his wet, black nose proudly. “I can find anything with this!”

The three unlikely friends began their search. Dango kept his nose firmly attached to the ground, inhaling deeply, while Gabu and Daru checked the grass inch by inch.

They searched around the compost heap, under the big fern leaves, and near the water bowl. But the stone was nowhere to be found. The sky was getting darker, and Gabu was losing hope.

“Maybe it’s buried,” Daru suggested, looking toward the hole Dango had been digging earlier.

Dango gasped. “Oh no! I was kicking a lot of dirt behind me today. What if I accidentally buried it?”

Dango immediately ran to his hole and began digging again, but with much more care this time. He gently pushed the dirt away with his paws. Gabu and Daru watched anxiously.

After a few minutes, Dango stopped. He sniffed a specific spot, snorted, and then gently nudged something with his nose. A dull glint of blue appeared in the dark soil.

“I found it! I found it!” Dango cheered, doing a little dance.

## True Friendship
Daru carefully picked up the blue stone with his mouth and brought it over to Gabu. However, the old sap was gone, so the stone would no longer stick to Gabu’s shell.

Gabu looked at the stone, then at Dango, who was covered in dirt and looking very sorry.

“I’m sorry I buried your treasure, Gabu,” Dango said softly, his ears dropping flat against his head. “I was being too wild.”

Gabu looked at the energetic little dog and smiled. “It’s okay, Dango. You didn’t do it on purpose, and you used your amazing nose to find it. You are a very good detective.”

Gabu then turned to Daru. “You know, Daru, I don’t think I need to wear this stone anymore to be special. How about we use it to decorate the entrance of our home instead? That way, all three of us can see it every day.”

Daru smiled warmly. “I think that is an excellent idea.”

They placed the shiny blue stone right at the entrance of the small space under the porch where the tortoises slept. When the moonlight hit the stone, it glowed beautifully.

From that day on, the garden was never quiet. Dango continued to run in circles and dig holes, but he always made sure to watch where his paws were landing. And Gabu and Daru realized that having a loud, short-legged giant as a friend wasn’t a bad thing at all—especially when you need to find something hidden in the dirt.
