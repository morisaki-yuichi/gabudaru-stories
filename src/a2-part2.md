Since their great tomato adventure, Gabu and Daru had become an even stronger team. They both still lived in their beautiful garden, and they both still had the same habits — Daru was always looking for something new to explore, while Gabu preferred to stay close to familiar, safe places.

But lately, Daru had been spending a lot of time near the tall wooden fence at the back of the garden, wondering what was on the other side.


## The Strange Noise


One afternoon, while Gabu was napping under a large leaf, Daru was exploring near the fence when he heard something. Scritch, scratch, scritch.

He ran to the fence to investigate. At the very bottom, he found a small hole — just wide enough for a young tortoise to squeeze through. When he pressed his face close and looked through, his eyes grew wide.

On the other side was something extraordinary. There were tall purple flowers swaying gently, long green grass that moved like waves, and a fat bush covered in clusters of bright yellow berries. It looked like a secret world that nobody knew about.

"Gabu! You have to see this!" Daru called as loudly as he could.

Gabu opened his eyes slowly and walked over, step by careful step.

"What is it? Is it tomatoes?"

"Something much better! There's a hole in the fence, and there's a whole other garden out there — flowers, long grass, and these amazing yellow berries!"

Gabu looked through the hole for a long moment. Then he looked up at the sky, which had turned an uneasy grey while they were talking.

"I'm not sure about this, Daru. Our garden is safe, and I know every corner of it. Out there could be dangerous. And those clouds don't look good to me."

"Oh, come on! Just a quick look," Daru said. "We'll be back before any rain starts, I promise."

Gabu looked at the sky once more, then back at his friend's bright, eager eyes. He didn't want Daru to go alone.

"All right. But we must stay together and come back the moment it starts raining."


## Into the Wild


One after the other, they squeezed through the hole. The world outside was completely different from their garden. The grass here was much taller, and it was difficult to see more than a few steps ahead.

Daru made his way to the yellow berry bush and tasted one carefully. His face lit up immediately.

"These are incredible! They taste almost like honey!"

Gabu tried one too, and he had to admit it was very good. But while he was eating, he kept glancing up at the sky. The dark clouds were moving faster now, and the wind had started picking up. Leaves were spinning through the air around them.

"Daru, we really need to go back," Gabu said. "The storm is coming faster than I thought."

Before Daru could answer, a deep BOOM of thunder rolled through the sky and shook the ground beneath their feet. Then the rain started all at once — not light drops, but a heavy, cold downpour.


## Lost in the Rain


They turned around immediately to find the hole in the fence. But the tall grass was bending and twisting in the wind, and everything around them looked completely different from before.

"Which way is the fence?" Daru asked. His voice was smaller than usual, and for the first time, he was truly frightened.

"I can't tell — everything looks the same!" Gabu said.

The rain was falling so hard that it drummed loudly on their shells. The ground was turning into mud beneath them. Then the water began to run along the ground in fast little rivers, pushing right towards them.

Daru tried to walk against the current, but his feet slipped on the wet mud, and he began to slide towards a large, deep puddle.

"Gabu! The water is pulling me!"

Gabu looked around quickly. Near the puddle, he could see a big, thick tree root that rose out of the ground like a bridge. There was a small dry hollow underneath it — just big enough for two tortoises.

"Daru! Over here — get under that root!"

Daru scrambled to his feet and pushed through the mud. He reached the root and crawled underneath, then immediately turned around and stretched out his front legs. Gabu gripped the root with his claws while Daru pulled. After a few difficult seconds, Gabu made it under safely.


## Waiting it Out


The space under the root was small and damp, but the rain and wind couldn't reach them there. They were safe, at least for now.

Daru was shaking — not just from the cold.

"I'm really sorry, Gabu," he said quietly. "You were worried about the sky, and I didn't listen. Now we're lost because of me."

Gabu pressed close to his friend so they could share warmth.

"It's all right, Daru. Your curiosity isn't a bad thing — it's one of the things I like most about you. But next time, we check the sky first! Right now, we're safe. We just have to be patient and wait for the rain to stop."

They stayed under the root for a long time, listening to the rain drumming on the ground. To pass the time, they talked about their favourite foods, the warmest sunny spots in the garden, and all the things they were looking forward to doing when they got home.


## The Way Home


Eventually, the thunder grew quieter, and the rain slowed down until it stopped completely. When they crawled out from under the root, the sun was beginning to break through the clouds, and all the wet leaves around them were shining like glass.

With the grass flattened by the rain, they could now see the wooden fence clearly in the distance. They walked towards it together, choosing each step carefully over the muddy ground.

When they found the hole and crawled back into their own garden, both of them let out a long, slow breath. The familiar grass felt soft under their feet. The warm, gentle light felt like a welcome home.

"Home," Gabu said, lowering his head onto the cool grass.

"Yes," said Daru. Then — "Wait. Look at this."

"What?" Gabu asked, lifting his head.

Daru smiled and opened his mouth slowly. He had kept one last yellow berry tucked safely in his cheek the whole time.

"We have a story and a snack," he said.
