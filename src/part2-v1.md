# The Secret Garden and the Big Storm

Gabu and Daru, the two tortoise brothers, still live in their beautiful garden. Since their great tomato adventure, they have become an excellent team. Gabu is still adventurous and always looks for new things, while Daru is still calm and careful.

However, they are both a little bigger now, and they know their garden very well. Sometimes, Gabu gets bored. He wants to see what is on the other side of the tall wooden fence.

## The Strange Noise
One afternoon, while Daru was taking his usual nap under a large leaf, Gabu heard a strange sound near the edge of the garden. Scritch, scratch, scritch.

Gabu walked over to the wooden fence as fast as his legs could carry him. He noticed that the wind had blown away some dirt at the bottom of the fence. There was a small gap—just big enough for a young tortoise to squeeze through.

Gabu looked through the hole. On the other side, he saw something amazing. There were bright purple flowers, long green grass, and a huge bush full of ripe, yellow berries. It looked like a secret, wild garden.

"Daru! Wake up!" Gabu shouted excitedly. "You need to see this!"

Daru opened his eyes and blinked. "What is it, Gabu? Is it more tomatoes?"

"No, it is better! There is a hole under the fence. There is a whole new world out there!"

Daru walked over and looked through the gap. He shook his head. "I don’t know, Gabu. Our garden is safe. Out there looks dangerous, and the sky is turning grey. I think a storm is coming."

"Oh, come on, Daru! Just a quick look," Gabu begged. "We will be back before it rains."

Daru sighed. He did not want his brother to go alone. "Okay, but we must be careful."

## Into the Wild
One after the other, the two tortoises squeezed through the gap. The world outside was different. The grass was much taller, making it difficult to see where they were going.

Gabu immediately ran—in tortoise speed—towards the yellow berries. "Look at these! They taste like honey!" he said, eating a berry happily.

Daru ate one too. It was delicious, but he could not stop looking at the sky. The dark clouds were moving fast, and the wind was getting stronger. Leaves were flying through the air.

"Gabu, we need to go back now," Daru said loudly over the wind. "The storm is here."

Before Gabu could answer, a loud BOOM of thunder shook the ground. Then, the rain started. It was not a gentle shower; it was a heavy, cold downpour.

## Lost in the Rain
The tortoises turned around to go back to the hole in the fence, but the tall grass was blowing wildly in the wind, changing the way everything looked.

"Where is the fence?" Gabu asked, his voice shaking. For the first time, he was scared.

"I can't see it!" Daru replied. The rain was hitting their shells heavily. The ground was becoming muddy and slippery.

Suddenly, the water started to create a small river in the grass, rushing right toward them. Because they were small, the water was strong enough to push them away. Gabu tried to walk against the water, but his feet slipped, and he began to slide backward into a deep puddle.

"Daru! The water is too strong!" Gabu cried out.

Daru looked around quickly. He saw a large, old tree root near the puddle. It formed a small, dry cave underneath.

"Gabu! Don't try to walk back! Follow me to that root!" Daru shouted.

Daru used all his strength to walk through the mud. He reached the root and climbed underneath. He turned around and extended his head and front legs to help his brother. Gabu grabbed the root with his front claws, and Daru used his own heavy shell to block the rushing water so Gabu could climb up.

## Waiting it Out
Finally, both brothers were safe inside the small cave under the tree root. It was dark and damp, but the heavy rain and cold wind could not reach them.

Gabu was shivering. "I am sorry, Daru," he whispered. "You were right. I wanted an adventure, but now we are trapped and lost."

Daru sat close to his brother to keep him warm. "It is okay, Gabu. You want to see the world, and that is not a bad thing. But we must always check the weather first! We are safe here. We just have to wait."

They stayed inside the root for a long time. They listened to the sound of the rain hitting the ground. To pass the time, they talked about their favorite foods and the warm sun.

## The Way Home
After a few hours, the rain stopped. The sun came out from behind the clouds, making the wet leaves sparkle.

Gabu and Daru climbed out of their hiding place. The tall grass was flat now because of the rain, so they could easily see the wooden fence in the distance.

They walked back together, stepping carefully over the mud. They found the gap under the fence and squeezed back into their own familiar garden. The grass felt safer, and the sun felt warmer than ever before.

"Home sweet home," Gabu said, resting his head on the grass.

"Yes," Daru agreed, sitting next to him. "But look on the bright side."

"What's that?" Gabu asked.

Daru smiled and opened his mouth. He was holding one last yellow berry that he had saved from the storm. "We got a story and a snack."

The two brothers shared the berry and fell asleep under the clear blue sky, happy to be home together.
