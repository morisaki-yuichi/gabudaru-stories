The Last Warm Afternoon

It was autumn. The leaves turned yellow and orange and red. Every morning, more leaves fell to the ground.

Something was different about Gabu and Daru. Every day, they ate a lot — more than usual. They moved slowly and sat for a long time in the afternoon sun, very still, with their eyes half closed.

Dango watched them every day. He did not understand.

Something Is Different

One morning, Dango asked: "Gabu, why are you eating so much?"

"I am getting ready," Gabu said.
"Ready for what?"
"Come sit with us," Gabu said. "I want to tell all of you something important."

The four friends sat together by the blue stone.

"Daru and I will sleep through the winter," Gabu said. "We will go inside our home and not come out for about four months."

Dango's mouth fell open. "Four months?"

"Yes. It is the way tortoises are. Our bodies need to sleep in the cold."

Dango looked at Daru. Daru gave him a small, quiet nod. "Every year," he said.

Suke-san said nothing. His ears were a little lower than usual.

"Will you come back?" Dango asked.
"Yes. In spring."
"You promise?"

Gabu looked at him. His old eyes were calm and kind. "I promise," he said.

Dango looked at the blue stone. He believed Gabu. But four months was a very long time.

The Last Days

The days got colder and shorter. The leaves fell from the trees. The garden was mostly bare and brown.

Dango stayed with Gabu and Daru every day. He sat with them in the morning. He sat with them in the afternoon. He did not want to miss anything.

One afternoon, Suke-san sat down beside Gabu. "Are you afraid?" he asked. "To sleep for so long?"

"No," Gabu said. "It is natural for me. Like resting after a long, good day."

Suke-san was quiet. Then he said: "I am a little afraid. For you."

"That is because you care," Gabu said. "That is not a bad thing."

They sat together until the air turned cold. Suke-san walked home slowly and thought about that answer for a long time.

The Last Warm Afternoon

One Tuesday, the sun was warm for the last time that year. The garden was still and golden. Dry leaves drifted down one by one.

None of them said it out loud. But they all knew. Today was the day.

They sat together by the blue stone for a long time. Nobody hurried. The afternoon moved slowly around them.

Dango put his head down close to Gabu. "Will you remember everything?" he asked. "The stone. Us. All of this?"

"Everything," said Gabu.

Dango nodded. He pressed his nose gently against Gabu's shell. Then he sat back.

"I will take care of the stone," he said.
"I know you will," said Gabu.

Gabu stood up slowly. "It is time," he said.

"Goodbye," said Dango. His voice was very quiet.
"See you in spring," said Gabu.

Daru turned at the door one last time. "Don't let it get too quiet!" he said, his voice still bright. Then they were gone.

The door was still. The garden was silent.

Dango lay down by the blue stone. Suke-san sat down beside him, close. Neither spoke.

They stayed there until the sun went down and the garden turned blue with evening.

— ✦ —

Spring

Winter came. Snow fell on the garden, soft and white.

Every morning, Dango and Suke-san went to the blue stone. When snow covered it, they cleared it away together, carefully.

"I want it to be ready," Dango said one morning, his breath making small white clouds. "For when they come back."
"It will be," said Suke-san.

They did this every day, through the long, cold, quiet winter.

Then one morning, the air was soft and warm. Green things were beginning to grow. Something smelled different — alive.

Dango sat by the stone and waited. He was very still, which was unusual for him.

A slow sound. Small and soft. The door opened.

Daru came out first. He blinked slowly in the morning light. He looked at the sky. He looked at the new green grass. He looked at the stone. Then he saw Dango.

"I'm back!" he said.

"DARU!"

Dango ran to him and pressed close. He did not say anything for a moment. He just stayed there.

Then Gabu stepped out, slow and steady. He stood in the spring light and looked around at the garden — at the stone, at Dango, at Suke-san.

Dango walked to him slowly and put his nose gently against Gabu's shell.

"You came back," he said.
"I said I would," said Gabu.

Suke-san sat nearby. His tail moved — slowly, quietly — and he let it.

They sat together in the warm spring morning. The garden smelled of new earth and young leaves. The blue stone shone in the sun.

Dango looked at Gabu. At Daru. At Suke-san. He took a long, slow breath of the warm air.

Everything was the same. Everything was good.

Everything was exactly right.

Winter passed. Spring came.
And they were all together again. 🌱✨
