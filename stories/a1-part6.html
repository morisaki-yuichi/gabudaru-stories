When Dango Was Still

Every morning, Dango walked around the whole garden. He checked every corner. He knew every flower, every tree, every good spot of cool dirt. Walking the garden was his job, and he loved it.

One morning in early summer, he was running near the old fence when his paw caught on a root.

He stopped. He lifted his front paw. It hurt.

"Oh," he said.

Gabu came over slowly. He looked at the paw. "Rest for two or three days," he said. "Do not run on it."

Dango looked at the garden. All that space. All that good ground to cover. "Two or three days?"

"Yes," said Gabu.

This was going to be very hard.

The First Day

Dango sat by the blue stone. He did not run. He just sat and watched the garden from there.

At first, he felt restless. There was so much to check. The fat bird in the big tree. The bee on the yellow flower. The corner by the fence where something interesting always happened.

But he could not go. So he just sat.

After a while, something happened. A small black beetle walked across the ground near his paw. He had seen it many times before, but he had never really watched it. Today, he watched where it went.

It went under the blue stone. It came out the other side. It did this again. And again.

It always goes under the stone, Dango thought. I never knew that.

Daru came and sat beside him. He told old stories about the garden — things from before Dango arrived, things from long ago. Dango listened to all of it. He had never had time to listen like this before.

Gabu sat nearby without talking. Just there, warm and quiet. That felt good too.

— ✦ —

The Second Day

Suke-san came in the morning and sat down near Dango without being asked.

"You came early," Dango said.
"Yes," said Suke-san.

They sat together in the morning light.

Later, Dango asked Gabu: "What do you think about when you are still? Do you get bored?"

"No," Gabu said. "I watch. I listen. When I am still, things come to me that cannot come when I am moving."
"What kind of things?"
"Small things. The sound of the wind in the grass. The smell before rain. A shadow moving across the ground."

Dango tried it. He sat very still and closed his eyes.

He heard: a bird calling from the top of the big tree. The wind moving through dry grass. A bee somewhere far away.

He opened his eyes. "I never heard those before," he said.

"They were always there," Gabu said. "You were always moving."

— ✦ —

The Third Day

On the third morning, Suke-san was already there when Dango came out. He had arrived before Daru. Before Gabu. He was sitting by the stone alone.

"You are very early," Dango said.
Suke-san was quiet for a moment. Then: "If it were me who was hurt, you would just be here. I wanted to try to be like that."

Dango looked at him. He did not know what to say. So he sat down next to Suke-san, and they watched the morning together.

That afternoon, Dango tried his paw. It felt much better. He stood up. He walked a few steps. He walked a few more.

"Good," said Gabu. "Try running tomorrow."

Dango sat back down by the stone for one last afternoon, watching the beetle go under and come out the other side.

— ✦ —

After

The next morning, Dango ran around the garden. His paw was fine. He ran past the old fence, past the tall flowers, past the big tree.

It felt wonderful to move again.

But when he reached the middle of the garden, something made him stop. He was not tired. He did not need to stop. He just stopped.

He sat down, right there in the middle of the garden.

He listened.

A bird at the top of the big tree. The wind in the grass. A bee on the yellow flower. The blue stone in the sun, warm and quiet.

He sat there for a while. Not because he had to. Because he wanted to.

Then he stood up and ran again, fast and happy, all around the garden.

But that evening, he told Gabu about what he had heard.

Gabu listened to the whole thing. Then he said: "Good."

Just that. Just: good.

Dango felt that it was.

The garden was the same.
But now Dango knew how to listen to it. 🌿✨
