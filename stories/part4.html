Suke-san Does Not Need Anyone

The garden had a new neighbor.

He arrived on a warm spring morning. He was a Shiba Inu puppy — with soft red-brown fur, a white chest, and a small curly tail. His ears were still a little too big for his head. His paws were still a little too big for his body.

His name was Suke-san.

Suke-san walked along the top of the garden wall with his chin up and his tail high. He looked at the flowers. He looked at the big trees. He looked at the clouds.

He did not look for friends.

I am fine on my own, he thought. I do not need anyone.

A butterfly flew past his nose.

He watched it go. Just for a moment, his tail began to wag.

He stopped it.
He looked around. No one had seen.
Good.

The First Morning

Dango saw the new puppy from across the garden.

Dango was not a small puppy anymore. His body was big and strong now. His legs were still very short — that never changed — but he ran fast. Every morning, he walked around the garden and checked everything. He knew every bird. He knew every bee. He knew the best spots of cool dirt. He watched over the garden like it was his job.

And today, he saw something new.

"A new friend!" Dango said, and ran over as fast as his short legs could go.

Suke-san saw the orange-and-white dog running toward him. He sat up straight and tried to look very calm.

"Hello," said Suke-san, with a small, careful bow. "I am Suke-san."

"I'm Dango! Come with me! I want you to meet my friends!"

Suke-san looked at him. Then he looked at the garden. He followed. Just to see.

Near the old porch, two tortoises sat in the warm morning light. A small blue stone sat by the door of their home. One tortoise was looking very carefully at a leaf on the ground. The other was stretching his neck toward a flower, trying hard to smell it.

"Gabu! Daru! This is Suke-san! He is new here!" Dango barked.

The tortoise by the leaf looked up slowly. He had big, calm eyes. "Hello," he said. "I am Gabu. Welcome to the garden."

The other tortoise turned around very fast. "Hi! I am Daru! Are you new on this street? What do you like to eat? Have you seen the fat bird in the big tree? He is very round! Like a ball with wings!"

Suke-san looked at them. They were small. They were round. They moved very, very slowly.

"Hello," he said. "You are tortoises."

"Yes!" said Daru.
"You move very slowly."
"Yes," said Gabu.

Then Daru said: "Your ears are very big."

Suke-san stood up straight. "They are normal ears," he said.

"They are very cute," said Daru.

Suke-san opened his mouth. He closed it. He looked away.

Something about the quiet one — Gabu — made him stop. Gabu was not looking at him at all. He had gone back to his leaf, looking at it with great care. He was not trying to impress anyone. He was just there, doing his own thing.

Suke-san understood that.

"It was nice to meet you," he said. He turned and walked away.

On top of the wall, he sat down and looked at the garden below. The two tortoises. The short, happy dog. The blue stone by the door.

He did not need any of that.

He watched them for a little while anyway.

The Rainy Afternoon

Three days passed. Each morning, Suke-san walked the garden wall alone. He watched the birds. He watched the clouds. He did not go near the porch.

On the fourth day, the sky turned dark and grey.

Suke-san was on the wall when the rain started. Big, cold drops. He jumped down quickly and ran under a tree. The water came through the leaves and hit him anyway.

He moved to another tree. Still wet.

Then the thunder came.

A big, deep BOOM that shook the air.

Suke-san's legs went stiff. His ears went flat against his head. He looked around very fast.

He was fine. He was completely fine. He was not scared at all.

Another BOOM.

Suke-san ran.

He did not know where he was running. His big paws splashed through the wet grass. He ran past the tall flowers and past the old tree and — he stopped.

He was at the porch.

The small blue stone sat by the door. Even in the grey rain, it caught the light.

Daru's head came out. "Suke-san! Come in! It is dry here!"

Suke-san looked at the small space. He looked back at the dark, wet garden. He walked in.

He sat near the door and tried to breathe slowly. He was fine. Everything was fine.

"Was that scary?" Daru asked.
"No," said Suke-san.

"Oh," said Daru. "My legs shake a little when the thunder is very loud. Even now."

Suke-san looked at him. "Really?" he said.

"Yes. But it is okay. It always stops."

Outside, Dango was running through the rain in big circles, barking at the sky with great joy. His round body was soaking wet. He looked completely happy.

"He is not scared?" Suke-san asked.
"Dango is scared of only one thing," said Daru.
"What?"
"The vacuum cleaner."

Suke-san looked at Dango, who was now jumping into a puddle. Something in his chest moved. He made a sound — a small, short sound.

Almost a laugh.
He stopped it quickly.

Gabu had not said anything. He was sitting very still, watching the rain. Then, without looking up, he said: "The blue stone. Do you want to know about it?"

"...Yes," said Suke-san.

So Daru told the story. Gabu finding the stone. Putting it on his shell with tree sap. Dango burying it by mistake. The search. The moment Dango's nose found it in the dark dirt.

"After that," Daru said, "Gabu said: let's put it at the door. So all of us can see it every day."

Suke-san looked at the stone for a long time. "You three have many stories," he said.

"Yes," said Daru. He smiled. "You can have stories too, if you want."

Suke-san did not answer that.

The rain fell for a long time. Daru talked about the fat bird, a red berry, an interesting rock by the water bowl. Gabu said small things from time to time. Suke-san listened. He did not say much. But he did not leave.

When the rain stopped, he stood up and shook his fur dry. "Thank you," he said. "For the space."

"Come back any time," said Daru.

Suke-san walked to the door. He stopped and looked at the blue stone one more time. Then he walked out into the clean, wet garden.

Behind him, without thinking, his small curly tail moved once.

The Morning He Came Back

The next morning was clear and bright.

Daru woke up first, as he always did. He walked out and stretched his legs in the cool air. The flowers were bright from the rain. A small bird sang somewhere in the big tree.

Daru stopped.

Suke-san was sitting near the porch. He had not knocked. He had not called out. He was sitting very still with his big ears up, looking at the garden.

"Good morning," said Daru.
"Good morning," said Suke-san. "I was walking. I came this way."

Daru sat next to him. They looked at the garden together. The morning light moved slowly across the grass.

"It is beautiful," Suke-san said. Quietly.
"Yes," said Daru. "Every morning is a little different."

Gabu came out, one step at a time. He saw Suke-san. He nodded — once, slowly — the same nod as always. Then he walked to his favorite sunny spot.

Then Dango came running from across the garden. He saw Suke-san and his whole body became happy at once.

"You came back! I knew it! I knew it!"

Suke-san sat up straight. "I was just—"

But Dango was already jumping around him, and his big ears were bouncing, and before Suke-san could stop it —

— his tail began to wag.

He stopped it fast. But not fast enough.

"Your tail!" Daru said, smiling.
"It was the wind," said Suke-san.
"There is no wind today," said Daru.

Suke-san looked at the sky. He looked at the flowers. He looked at a very interesting spot on the ground.

"...Maybe a small wind," he said.

Dango sat down and looked at him with bright eyes. "Do you want to stay? We just sit here. Sometimes there is a rabbit. One time there was a frog — big like this—" he opened his mouth wide.

Suke-san was quiet.

He looked at Gabu, still and slow in the morning sun. He looked at Daru, already trying to smell the flower next to him. He looked at Dango, who had forgotten about the frog and was now watching a bee.

These were very strange friends.

"I am not good at sitting still," Suke-san said.
"That is okay," said Daru. "Gabu is not good at moving fast. I am not good at being quiet. Dango is not good at—"
"Hey!" said Dango.
"—many things," Daru finished.

Suke-san sat down next to Daru.

"I will stay," he said. "For a little while."

The morning sun moved across the garden. The fat yellow butterfly came by. Dango's tail wagged slowly on the ground. Gabu did not move, but the lines around his old eyes were soft.

No one said anything for a long time.

Suke-san sat very still and tried to look very calm.

But under his white chest, something warm was growing.

He did not have a name for it yet. He thought he would wait and see.

That evening, Suke-san walked home slowly.

He thought about the blue stone. The rain. The sound of Daru's voice. He thought about how Dango was scared of only one thing. He thought about Gabu's nod — you are welcome here.

Suke-san did not need anyone.

He was a Shiba Inu. He was fine alone. He was proud and calm and did not need —

A leaf blew past him in the evening air.

His eyes followed it.

His tail wagged.

He looked around. No one had seen.

He walked home with his chin up and his tail high.
But this time, he was looking forward to tomorrow.

The garden had a new neighbor.
And now — he was not alone. 🐾✨
