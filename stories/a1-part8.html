The Long Way Round

It was Gabu's idea to visit the town market. He had been there once, many years ago, and he wanted to see if it was the same.

"The fountain in the middle of the market square," he said. "I remember it was beautiful. Come."

So all four of them went. Out through the garden gate, down the long street, into the town.

The town was nothing like the garden. There were cars and bicycles and people walking in all directions at once. There were smells of food and metal and something warm coming from a bakery window. Dango's head moved left, right, up, down, barely able to keep up with it all.

They walked together through the market. Gabu and Daru were slow. Dango and Suke-san went a little ahead, then stopped, then went ahead again. It was a good morning.

Then a big delivery truck came down the narrow street, honking loudly. People stepped out of the way. There was a crowd, and then a surge, and then —

When Dango and Suke-san turned around, Gabu and Daru were not there.

Separated

Dango looked left and right. He looked behind them. He looked in the shop doorways. Nothing.

"Don't panic," Suke-san said immediately. He stood very straight. "We retrace our steps. We came from the left, so—"

They went left. The street looked the same as every other street. They went a little further. Still nothing familiar.

"Maybe right," said Suke-san. They tried right. Still wrong.

Suke-san stopped. He looked at the streets around them. Something in his expression — usually so certain — was less certain than usual.

Dango sat down on the pavement and looked up. On a window ledge above them, a large grey cat was watching them with the bored patience of someone who has lived in the same place for a long time.

"Excuse me," Dango said. "Do you know where the market fountain is?"

The cat looked at him for a moment. "Two streets down. Then left. Big stone fountain, you can't miss it."

"Thank you very much," Dango said.

He looked at Suke-san. Suke-san looked at the cat. Then back at Dango. "You want to trust a cat," Suke-san said.

"She has lived here a long time," Dango said. "She knows the streets."

Suke-san considered this. "Fine," he said.

Two streets down and left: there was the fountain. A large stone fountain in the middle of the square, with water catching the light.

Suke-san looked at it.
"The cat was right," he said.
"Yes," said Dango.

— ✦ —

Meanwhile

When the crowd had moved, Gabu looked around and saw that Dango and Suke-san were gone. He did not panic. He thought for a moment.

"Should we look for them?" Daru asked. He was worried — his voice had a different quality than usual.
"They will find their way," Gabu said calmly. "Let us go to the fountain. Suke-san is logical. He will think of a meeting point, and the fountain is the obvious one."
"How can you be so calm?" Daru asked.

Gabu began walking slowly toward the fountain. "I was lost once," he said. "A long time ago. In a place much bigger than this."

"What did you do?"
"I stopped. I thought about what I knew. Then I moved."
"Were you frightened?"
"Very much," Gabu said. "But I found something useful: you can be frightened and still think. They are not the same thing."

Daru was quiet for a moment, walking beside Gabu through the market crowds. "I always thought you were never frightened," he said.

"No," Gabu said. "I have just had more practice at thinking while frightened."

Daru thought about this for the rest of the walk to the fountain.

— ✦ —

Finding Each Other

Dango and Suke-san were sitting by the fountain when two small shapes appeared from the other side of the square — moving slowly but steadily, one behind the other.

"GABU! DARU!"

Dango ran to them. He pressed himself against Daru and stayed there for a moment. Daru said: "I knew you would come."

"I asked a cat," Dango said.
"Good thinking," said Daru.

Suke-san stood in front of Gabu. "I could not navigate," he said. He said it plainly, without making it smaller than it was. "I thought I could. I was wrong. Dango found the way."

Gabu looked at him for a moment. "You are here," he said. "That is what matters."

"We both guessed," Dango added. "But together, the guesses were good enough."

Gabu looked at the fountain. It was the same fountain he had seen many years ago. The water still caught the light in the same way.

"Is it the same?" Daru asked.

"Mostly," Gabu said. "The things that matter usually are."

On the walk home, they stayed together — not ahead and behind, but all four at the same pace. The town felt smaller than it had that morning.

Suke-san walked beside Dango instead of in front. "Thank you," he said. "For asking the cat."

"Thank you," said Dango, "for knowing the fountain would be the meeting point."
"I just thought about what Gabu would choose," Suke-san said.
"So did Gabu," said Dango.

Suke-san looked at him. "How do you know?"

"Because he was there," Dango said.

It was a very good answer, and neither of them said anything after that, all the way home.

They were lost for a little while.
And then they found each other. 🗺️✨
