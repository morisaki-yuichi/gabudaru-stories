December 12

Dango Makes Something

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

Dango had been working on something in secret.

It had been working on it for two weeks, a little each day, when the others were not looking.

On this day, it was done.

It brought it to the table at dinner.

It was a small woven circle — like a wreath. Made of dried vine and grass, with four small things worked into it: a tiny dried flower (from Daru's shelf), a piece of the blue-grey feather (from Dango's shelf), a loop of the braided rope (from Suke-san), and a piece of apple-tree bark (from the garden, which was Gabu's).

"What is it?" Daru said.

"For the house," Dango said. "For winter. I saw a picture in Gabu's book of a wreath. I made one."

Gabu looked at the four elements in it.

"One from each of us," he said.

"Yes," Dango said. It looked nervous. "Is it all right? I took the feather. I should have asked."

"It is all right," Daru said.

Gabu took the wreath. He held it up. The four small things were each different — different colors, textures, origins.

"Where should it go?" Dango said.

Gabu went to the door. He hung the wreath on the inside of the door.

They stood and looked at it.

"When we come home," Dango said, "we see it."

"Every time," said Gabu.

"All four of us," Dango said. "On the door."

It was not a large thing. But it was theirs.
