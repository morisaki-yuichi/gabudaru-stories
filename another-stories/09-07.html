September 7

Dango Adds to the Shelf

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

Dango found a new thing for its shelf.

It was a small pine cone. Not from the garden — there were no pine trees in the garden. It had blown over the wall, maybe, or been carried by a bird.

Dango held it in its hands and looked at it. It was perfect. Each scale fit tightly against the next. They opened in a spiral that went all the way around.

It added it to the shelf beside the feather and the stones.

Dango stood back and looked at the shelf.

"My shelf is getting full," it said.

Gabu looked. The shelf had grown. Feather. Round stone. Wave-bark. Stripe-stone. Clay turtle. Pine cone.

"Six things," Gabu said.

"Each one from a different day," Dango said.

Daru came and looked. He read the shelf like a story — each thing from a moment he remembered. The walk in August. The rainy day. The afternoon making clay.

"You make the things permanent," Daru said. "By keeping them."

Dango looked at the shelf. "Yes," it said. "Without the shelf, I might forget. But this way, when I see the pine cone, I remember the September morning I found it."

"Memory is in the things," Gabu said.

Dango nodded slowly. "Yes," it said. "My grandmother had things like this. Many things in a row. I did not understand then."

"And now?" Gabu said.

"Now I have my own row," Dango said. "Now I understand."
