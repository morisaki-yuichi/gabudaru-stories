September 22

The Empty Tree

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

The apple tree without apples looked different.

Its branches were mostly bare of leaves too now. Just the skeleton of the tree remained — the old twisted shape of it, the dark wood, the small scars from where branches had been trimmed over the years.

Dango looked at it for a long time.

"It looks smaller without apples," it said.

"It always does," said Gabu.

Daru stood beside Dango. He had seen this before — the transition from full tree to empty. "But the tree is still here," he said.

"Yes," Dango said. "Just... resting."

Gabu walked to the tree and put one hand on its trunk. The bark was rough and grey. He stood like that for a moment.

Dango went and put a hand on the trunk too.

Daru put his hand on it.

The three of them stood around the tree with their hands on it.

"Is this strange?" Dango said.

"Maybe," said Gabu. "But the tree has given us a lot this year. It seems right to acknowledge it."

Dango kept its hand on the rough bark. Under the bark, it knew, the tree was sleeping. Getting ready. Saving its energy for spring.

"See you in spring," Dango said to the tree. Very quietly.

Gabu smiled.

They took their hands away. The tree stood in the pale autumn light, bare and patient.

It would be back.
