March 4

Cherry Blossoms

🐢 Gabu

This morning Gabu looks up at the apple tree and gasps.

The tree is pink.

Not just a little pink. Really pink. The buds opened in the night. Dozens of pink flowers on every branch. The tree is a cloud of pink.

He stands and looks at it for a very long time.

He has seen this before, every year. But it always surprises him. Every year, when the apple tree blooms, it takes his breath away.

He walks under the tree. Pink petals are on the ground around it. He steps on them. They are soft.

He looks up. The pink flowers against the blue sky. Beautiful.

A bee comes. Then another bee. They go from flower to flower.

A bird comes and sits in the tree. It sits inside the pink clouds of flowers. It sings.

"Beautiful," says Gabu. He says it over and over. "Beautiful, beautiful."

He walks back and looks at the tree from far away.

He walks to the side and looks at it from the side.

He walks to the other side.

Every angle is beautiful.

He sits under the tree in the morning. Petals fall on his shell. He does not brush them off.

He eats his lunch under the tree. Petals fall on his food. He eats them too. They taste sweet and faint.

He stays under the tree all day.

When evening comes, the pink is orange in the sunset light. Then grey in the dusk.

He walks to his flat stone.

He thinks: the apple tree is the most beautiful thing in my garden. Today.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps with petals on his shell.
