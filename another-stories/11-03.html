November 3

Suke-san Mends Things

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Suke-san had been going around the house mending things.

A small basket that had a hole. A sleeping mat that was coming apart at the edge. A rope that Dango needed. The grass hat it had already fixed. Gabu's old chair that had a loose leg.

It did all of this without being asked.

One morning, Gabu found Suke-san working on the loose shutter of the window. It had rattled all autumn.

"You are fixing the shutter," Gabu said.

"It makes noise in the wind," Suke-san said. It had a small piece of wood wedged in at the corner.

Gabu watched. Suke-san worked carefully. Small adjustments.

The shutter stopped rattling.

"That has rattled for three years," Gabu said.

"I know," said Suke-san. "I heard it."

Gabu was quiet.

"You fix things because they bother you?" he said.

Suke-san thought. "Because a mended thing works better than a broken one," it said. "And it gives me something to do with my hands."

Gabu looked at the shutter. Three years of rattling, fixed in twenty minutes.

"I do not always see what needs mending," Gabu said.

"You see the big things," Suke-san said. "The garden. The pond. The structure. I see the small things."

Gabu thought about this. "We are useful to each other."

"Yes," said Suke-san. It picked up its tools and moved to the next thing on its list.

Gabu watched it go and felt the house settle around him, a little more whole.
