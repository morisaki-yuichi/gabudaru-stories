July 3

Too Many Insects

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

There was one thing about July that was not perfect.

The insects.

They were everywhere. Tiny biting things that hummed around Daru's ears. Flies that walked on his food. Midges that got in his eyes.

Daru waved his arms. He shook his head. He walked in circles trying to get away from them.

"There are too many," he said.

Gabu was sitting calmly. A fly landed on his nose. He did not move.

"How are you so calm?" Daru said.

"I am used to it," Gabu said.

Daru sat down beside him. A biting thing landed on his ear. He slapped at it.

"Tell me something useful," Daru said. "How do you stop being bothered?"

Gabu thought. "You cannot stop them from coming," he said. "So you stop letting them bother you. You say: they are here, and I am here. We are both in the garden. That is all."

Daru tried this. A fly landed on his arm. He looked at it. He said, inside his head: you are here, I am here. He did not slap.

The fly flew away on its own.

"Hm," Daru said.

"It is not perfect," Gabu said. "But it helps."

Daru tried again with the next one. And the one after that.

By afternoon, he was only a little bothered by the insects.

"I am getting better at this," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "A little every day."
