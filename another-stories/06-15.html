June 15

Midmonth

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

It was the middle of June.

Gabu noticed this in the morning, when he counted on his fingers. Fifteen days in. Fifteen days to go. The month was exactly half done.

He told Daru.

Daru sat with this information for a moment. "Half," he said. "What does that mean for the garden?"

"The roses will start blooming soon," Gabu said. "The pond plants will be their fullest. The days are almost as long as they will ever be."

Daru stood up and looked at the garden as if seeing it fresh.

The rose bush near the wall had fat buds on it. They were not open yet, but they were getting big. He could smell something sweet coming from them already.

The pond plants — the wide green leaves that floated on the surface — were very large now. They nearly covered one corner of the pond.

The sun was strong and high.

"It is a full garden," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "This is the high part of summer. Everything is growing as much as it can."

Daru walked slowly around the garden and looked at everything. The strawberry plants. The compost pile. The apple tree with its many small hard apples. The shelter he had built.

He had only been here since April. But all of this felt like his now.

"Gabu," he said.

"Yes?"

"Thank you for letting me stay."

Gabu looked at him. "Thank you for coming," he said.
