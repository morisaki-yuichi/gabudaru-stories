November 5

The First Story Circle

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

Suke-san proposed a new thing.

"A story circle," it said. "Each person tells a small story. Any story. About anything."

Dango immediately liked this.

"Yes," Dango said. "Now?"

"After dinner," said Suke-san.

After dinner, they sat around the fire. Gabu in his chair. Daru on the bench. Dango cross-legged on the floor. Suke-san with its back against the wall.

Suke-san started.

It told a story about a river it had once crossed in winter. The ice was thin. It went across very slowly. At the middle, it heard the ice crack. It stopped. It breathed. Then it kept going. It reached the other side.

"What was on the other side?" Dango said.

"Trees," said Suke-san. "A forest I needed to pass through."

"Was the forest worth the ice?" Dango said.

Suke-san thought. "Yes. The forest was beautiful."

Daru went next. He told a very short story about a leaf he had watched from green to yellow to brown to gone.

Dango told a long story with many sounds and exclamations about the day it first floated in a pond.

Gabu told the story of the first autumn he planted the apple tree. How small it was. How uncertain he was that it would grow.

"But it did," Dango said.

"Eventually," said Gabu. "After two bad years and one very good year. On the third year it gave five apples."

Four stories. Four voices.

They sat in the fire's light and felt how richly time had been spent.
