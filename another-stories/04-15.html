April 15

Two Turtles in the Garden

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Gabu notices things today that he never noticed before.

When he walks, there are two sets of footprints in the mud near the pond. His and Daru's.

When he eats, he is not the only one eating.

When he looks at the sky, there is someone to look at it with him.

When he goes to the gate and sniffs through the crack, Daru comes and sniffs too.

"What does it smell like?" asks Daru.

"Outside," says Gabu. "Grass and earth and things I don't know."

Daru sniffs. "And spring," says Daru. "It smells like spring out there too."

"Yes," says Gabu.

They walk away from the gate together.

In the afternoon, they rest under the big tree. They lie side by side. Not touching, but close.

Gabu looks up through the leaves.

He thinks: two months ago I was here alone. Now there are two of us.

He thinks: the garden is the same. Same tree, same pond, same wall. But it is different too. Completely different.

He thinks: presence changes things. Your presence is in a place even when you are not moving or making noise. Just being here changes it.

He says this to Daru.

Daru thinks about it. Then he says: "I think you are right. I can feel you in this garden. Even when you are far away, I know you are here."

Gabu is quiet.

Then he says: "I feel that too."

They lie under the tree in the afternoon sun.

Two turtles.

In the same garden.

At peace.

They sleep.
