April 27

Gabu and Daru Eat Together

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Gabu and Daru eat together near the south wall.

The flowers are very good this time of year. Not to eat. But to sit among.

They eat grass nearby. New, sweet spring grass. The best grass.

Gabu eats slowly. He chews each bite. He thinks about the taste.

Daru eats a little faster. But he stops and tastes too.

"Good," says Daru.

"New grass," says Gabu. "The best of the year."

Daru eats more. He tries different patches.

"This patch is sweeter," he says. "Near the wall. The sun warms it more."

Gabu tries that patch.

"Yes," he says. "You are right."

He thinks: Daru notices things I did not notice. He finds new things in my garden.

He says this to Daru.

Daru says: "You know the big things. I am finding the small things."

Gabu thinks: yes. Exactly.

He says: "Together we notice more."

They eat together in the warm April sun. The flowers around them. The bees visiting the flowers. The birds in the apple tree.

Gabu thinks: this is what I wanted. To eat with someone. To sit with someone and say: this is good. And have them say yes.

He looks at Daru eating next to him.

He is so glad.

He finishes his grass.

He sits in the warm sun.

He closes his eyes.

He is full and warm and happy.

He sleeps.
