August 29

The Last Fireflies

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango went to the pond alone that evening.

It sat on the flat stone and waited.

For a while, nothing. The pond was dark. A small breeze moved the floating leaves.

Then — one light. Faint. Near the edge of the water.

Dango did not move.

Another light. Two. Maybe three.

The fireflies were still here, but there were very few. Much fewer than in July and early August.

Dango watched them move. They were slower now, maybe. Or maybe that was the evening light playing tricks.

Gabu came and sat beside Dango.

"Are they leaving?" Dango asked.

"Slowly," said Gabu. "By September they will be gone."

Dango watched the small lights. Each one blinked on, drifted, blinked off. On. Off.

"I will miss them," Dango said.

"They come back," Gabu said. "Next June. You will see them again."

Dango thought about next June. It was far away. But it was real.

One firefly drifted close to them. It hovered just off the stone and blinked. Once. Twice. Three times. Then it rose slowly and was gone over the pond.

"I see you," Dango said to the empty air.

Gabu looked at Dango. "What did you say?"

"I learned it from Daru," Dango said. "When he saw them for the first time. That is what the fireflies are saying. I see you."

Gabu looked at the last few lights blinking over the pond.

"Yes," he said. "That is exactly right."
