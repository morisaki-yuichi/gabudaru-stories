February 23

The Wind Changes

🐢 Gabu

This morning the wind is different.

Gabu stands in the middle of the garden. He feels the wind.

It is coming from the south.

All winter, the wind came from the north. Cold and sharp. The north wind made him cold. He did not like it.

Today the wind comes from the south. It is still cool. But there is something in it. Something warmer. Something that smells different.

He closes his eyes. He breathes in the south wind.

He smells earth warming up somewhere far away. He smells green things, faint but real. He smells water in the sun.

Spring smells. Far away, but coming.

He opens his eyes.

He says: "The wind changed."

He walks around the garden. The south wind touches everything. The flowers on the south wall bend a little in it. The pond ripples.

He goes to the gate. He opens the crack and puts his nose to it. He sniffs.

The wind comes through the crack from outside. Yes. From the south.

He sniffs the outside wind. It smells like the garden wind but more. More wildness. More distance. More things he does not know.

He pulls back from the gate.

He thinks: the south wind brings spring from far away. It travels over fields and rivers and forests. It comes to my garden.

He thanks the wind.

"Thank you," he says.

He walks to his flat stone.

He sits in the south wind.

The wind moves his flowers. It moves the grass. It moves a strand of his neck skin.

He is warm.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps in the south wind.
