April 30

The End of April

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today is the last day of April.

Gabu walks with Daru around the garden. They go slowly together, as they often do now.

Gabu thinks about April.

He thinks about the morning Daru came through the gate. He remembers the gate opening, the small shape, the big eyes.

He remembers their first walk around the garden together. He remembers Daru rolling the blue stone. He remembers Daru afraid of the bee, and then not afraid.

He remembers sitting under the stars. And watching the cherry tree over the wall. And eating sweet grass together by the flowers.

He says to Daru: "One month."

Daru says: "One month since I came here."

"Yes."

Daru says: "It feels longer. In a good way. Like I have been here always."

Gabu says: "I feel that too."

They walk to the flat stone. They sit on it side by side. Two turtles on one stone.

Gabu looks at his collection. The flat stone. The grey stone. The blue stone. His feather.

He thinks: the garden is richer now. Not just with things. With Daru.

He says: "I am glad you came."

Daru says: "I am glad I came too."

They sit together.

The garden is warm and full and alive around them.

Gabu thinks: this is how spring should feel. Full, and warm, and not alone.

He closes his eyes.

He is happy.

He sleeps.
