June 16

The Spider's Web

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru
0:00

In the early morning, when the dew was still on the grass, Gabu found a spider's web.

It was stretched between two tall garden stakes. It was a perfect circle, with lines going out from the center like the spokes of a wheel. And every thread was covered in tiny drops of dew, so the whole web sparkled.

Gabu stood and looked at it for a long time. He did not call Daru. He just watched.

When Daru woke up and came outside, he stopped when he saw Gabu standing very still.

"What are you looking at?" he said softly.

Gabu pointed. Daru looked at the web.

"Oh," Daru said. Just that.

They stood together and looked at it. The morning sun came up over the wall and hit the web. Every dewdrop became a small rainbow.

In the center of the web, a small spider sat very still.

"Did the spider make all of that?" Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "In the night, while we were sleeping."

"How?"

"It pulls thread from inside itself," Gabu said. "Then it walks back and forth, very carefully, and builds."

Daru stared at the web. It was perfect. Every circle was even. Every line was straight. And the spider had done it all in the dark.

"It must have very good hands," Daru said.

Gabu smiled. "Eight of them," he said.

Daru laughed softly. They stood there until the sun dried the dew and the web became invisible again.
