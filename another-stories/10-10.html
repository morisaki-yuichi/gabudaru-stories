October 10

A Strange Sound Again

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango heard it.

It was in the evening, when they were sitting by the fire. A sound from outside. Thin and high, carried on the wind.

"What is that?" Dango said.

Gabu looked up from his book.

The sound came again. Not close. Far away — beyond the wall, from the direction of the path.

Daru had heard it too. He was very still.

"That is a voice," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

Dango jumped up. "Whose?"

"I don't know," Gabu said. He was calm.

They listened. The sound came and went with the wind. Sometimes clearer, sometimes gone.

It was not a worrying sound. It was thin but not frightened. It sounded, if anything, like someone talking to themselves while walking.

"Someone is on the path," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Going somewhere."

Dango went to the window and looked out. The garden was dark. The gate was shut. No one was there.

The sound came once more, then faded.

"They passed," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. He went back to his book.

Dango stayed at the window for a while.

"They were close to the gate," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Maybe they will come back," Dango said.

Gabu did not look up from the book. But something in the way he held himself was alert.

"Maybe," he said.
