August 27

Daru's Letter

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Daru wrote a letter.

He wrote it in the early morning while Gabu and Dango slept.

The letter was to no one in particular. He had learned the idea from a book Gabu kept on his shelf — a book about writing down thoughts.

He wrote:

"To the garden: Thank you. I came in April not knowing if I would stay. Now I cannot imagine leaving.

To Gabu: You taught me patience and stillness and how to float. You never asked me to be different.

To Dango: You came in July and the garden changed again. I did not know I wanted that.

To the pond: You hold me up when I float on you. You are always there in the morning.

To the apple tree: Thank you for the apple in August."

He stopped there. He read it over.

He felt a little embarrassed. It was a strange thing to write a letter to a garden.

But he kept it.

He folded it and put it in his sleeping spot, under the flat piece of bark.

When Gabu and Dango woke up, he said nothing about it.

But all through that day, he felt lighter. As if writing the things down had let him carry them differently.

Gabu noticed Daru seemed very calm and quiet that day.

"You seem peaceful," Gabu said.

"I feel good," Daru said.

"Good," said Gabu. And he asked no more.
