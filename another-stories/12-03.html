December 3

Ice on the Pond

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The pond was frozen.

Not just at the edges. The whole surface was solid ice.

Gabu checked it with a stick. He pressed hard. He listened. No cracking sound.

He crouched down and pressed both hands on the ice. Solid.

"How thick?" Daru said.

"Maybe six centimeters," Gabu said. "Perhaps more."

He stood up. He put one foot on the ice carefully. It held.

He put both feet on. He stood on the pond.

Dango's eyes went wide.

Gabu slid one foot forward. He was walking on the pond.

"Can I?" Dango said.

Gabu came back off. He tested once more.

"All right," he said. "Carefully."

Dango stepped onto the ice. It was slippery. Its foot slid. It caught itself.

"Oh," it said. "Slippery."

"Small steps," Gabu said. "Do not rush."

Dango moved slowly to the center of the pond. It was standing in the middle of the pond — the place where, in summer, it had learned to float.

"I am standing where I float," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

Daru came on the ice too, carefully. Suke-san waited at the edge, watching.

"Suke-san?" Dango said.

"I will watch," Suke-san said. "I have seen ice before."

They slid slowly on the frozen pond until their feet were cold. Then they came in for hot tea.

The pond held them up, even in winter.
