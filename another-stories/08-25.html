August 25

The Cooling Evening

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

The evenings were different now.

Not cold — not yet. But cool. A light jacket kind of cool, if turtles wore jackets.

The garden felt different in the cooling evening. The flowers were closing for the night. The bees were gone. The fireflies only came a little, for a short time.

But new things came with the cooler nights.

Owls.

Dango heard one for the first time. A low, soft call from the big tree beyond the wall.

"What is that?" it said.

"An owl," Gabu said.

"I thought owls only lived in stories," Dango said.

Gabu smiled. "They are very real."

They listened. The owl called again. It was a calm sound. Slow and deep.

Then, from another tree, another owl answered.

"There are two," Daru said.

"They talk to each other at night," Gabu said.

Dango stood perfectly still and listened to the two owls calling back and forth. One low note. A pause. Another low note from farther away. A pause. The first again.

It was like a slow conversation about the evening.

"They sound peaceful," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Owls are calm creatures."

They listened until the owls went quiet. The night was dark and cool and full of the smell of late summer.

"Autumn will bring new things too," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "It always does."

Dango thought about what was coming. And felt, to its own surprise, a small flicker of excitement.
