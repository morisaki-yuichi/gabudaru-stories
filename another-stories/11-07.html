November 7

Suke-san's Song

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Suke-san sang while it worked.

Dango had noticed this from the first day. But the songs were very quiet and not always complete — just fragments, phrases.

One day Dango asked: "What is that song you sing?"

"Which one?" said Suke-san.

"The slow one. The one that goes—" Dango hummed a few notes. It had been listening.

Suke-san recognized it. "That is a walking song," it said. "I learned it long ago. You sing it when a road is long and you need to keep going."

"Can you teach me?" Dango said.

"It has no words," Suke-san said. "Just melody."

"Then teach me the melody," Dango said.

Suke-san sang it slowly. It was a simple song — not complex. But it had a forward feeling. Like feet moving. Like distance being covered.

Dango learned it quickly.

Then Dango taught it to Daru. And Daru already half-knew Gabu's morning song, and he taught that to Suke-san.

By evening, they had two songs between four people.

At the fire, they sang both songs.

Gabu's song first — the morning/noon/evening song.

Then Suke-san's walking song.

Four voices together, some on melody, some humming underneath.

The fire listened.

Outside, November was cold and dark.

Inside, the songs moved through the room like something living, warm and going, going forward into the night.
