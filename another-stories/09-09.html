September 9

Playing with Leaves

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

The apple tree had more yellow leaves now.

A few had fallen. They lay on the ground under the tree — bright yellow against the dark soil.

Dango picked one up. It was still flexible, not yet dry. It held it up to the light. The veins were visible through the yellow: a thin network of lines, brighter than the rest.

Daru picked up a leaf and turned it over. The underside was paler.

Gabu sat under the tree and watched them play with the leaves.

Dango tried to float a leaf on the pond. It bobbed nicely, moving across the surface.

"Look, it is sailing," Dango said.

It added another leaf. Then another. Soon there were ten leaves sailing across the pond, moving with the small breeze.

"A fleet," Daru said.

"Yes," said Dango. "All sailing to the same place."

They watched the leaves drift. Some turned slowly. Some caught on the pond plants and stopped. Some made it all the way to the other side.

"The one with two colors won," Dango said. It was a leaf that was half yellow and half green — the last half turning.

Gabu looked at the winning leaf. "Half and half," he said. "Still changing."

Dango went around to the far side of the pond and picked up the leaves as they arrived. It was playing a game of its own — leaf dock attendant.

Daru watched and felt light and happy.

Autumn had arrived, and it was play.
