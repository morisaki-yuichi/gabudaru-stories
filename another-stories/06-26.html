June 26

The Map

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru found a piece of flat bark.

He brought it to Gabu. "Let us make a map of the garden," he said.

Gabu thought this was a good idea.

Daru took a stick and began to draw. He made a square for the outer wall. A circle for the pond. He drew the apple tree with a bump shape. He drew the rose bush near the wall. He drew the shelter with a little triangle roof.

"What else?" he said.

"The strawberry corner," Gabu said. "And the compost pile. And the flat stone by the pond."

Daru added them all. The map got more and more detailed.

"Here is where we always sit in the morning," Daru said, drawing an X. "And here is where the ants have their road."

When they were done, the map was full of things. The garden looked large and interesting, with so many features.

Daru held it up and compared it to the real garden.

"It is good," he said. "But the real one is better."

"Maps always are a little smaller than the real thing," Gabu said.

Daru put the map in Gabu's journal. It fit nicely between two pages.

"Now whoever reads the journal will know the garden," Daru said.

Gabu looked at the map. He had never drawn the garden before. He had always just known it.

"I see it differently now," he said. "When it is drawn like this."

"Me too," said Daru. "I see how much there is."
