June 13

The Ant Road

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

There was a road of ants going across the garden.

It went from under the big stone near the wall all the way to the compost pile at the other end. Hundreds of ants moved along it in two lines — one going, one coming back. They never bumped into each other.

Daru stood very still and watched.

"How do they know where to go?" he said.

"They follow a smell," Gabu said. "The ants who went first left a smell on the ground. The others follow it."

Daru bent down very close to look. The ants carried tiny things — pieces of seed, crumbs, bits of leaf. Each one carried something bigger than itself.

"They are very strong," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "For their size, they are the strongest things in the garden."

Daru watched one ant stop and turn around, confused. It moved left, then right. Then it found the smell again and kept going.

"What if the road washes away in rain?" Daru asked.

"Then they make it again," Gabu said. "They always find a way."

Daru stood up. He stepped carefully over the ant road so he did not break it.

"I like them," he said. "They work very hard. And they always know where they are going."

Gabu nodded. "Or they find their way again when they get lost," he said. "That is just as good."

Daru thought that was a good thing to know.
