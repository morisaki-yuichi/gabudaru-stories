August 9

Too Hot for Anything

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

The hottest day of summer.

The air was thick and heavy. Even breathing felt warm.

Nobody wanted to move.

Gabu sat in the shade of the apple tree and did not move.

Daru sat in the pond up to his ears and did not move.

Dango tried to do things. It picked up a stone. Put it down. Picked up a leaf. Put it down. Walked to the pond. Walked back. Walked to the pond again.

"Dango," Daru said from the water. "Come in and stay in."

Dango got in the pond. The water was warm at the top and cool below. It found the cool part and stayed very still.

"This is the only good place," Dango said.

"Yes," said Daru.

After a while, Gabu came and stood in the shallows.

Three turtles in the pond, not talking, not moving. Just cooling.

A dragonfly landed on Dango's shell and sat there. Dango did not move. The dragonfly stayed.

"Dango," Daru said quietly. "Look at your shell."

Dango turned its head slowly. The big dragonfly was sitting on its shell, wings spread, in the sun.

Dango made no sound. It went perfectly still.

The dragonfly sat on Dango's shell for five minutes. Then it launched into the air and was gone.

Dango let out its breath.

"It sat on me," it said.

"Yes," said Daru.

On the hottest day, in the still water, the dragonfly had chosen Dango. That was something.
