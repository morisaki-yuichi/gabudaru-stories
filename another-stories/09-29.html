September 29

The Smell of October Coming

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Gabu smelled it first, as he always did.

The end of September had a smell. Not autumn — they had smelled that for weeks. This was something new. Sharper. Almost like cold itself had a smell.

"October is coming," he said.

Daru sniffed. "Colder," he agreed.

Dango sniffed very hard.

"Like clean stone," Dango said. "Cold stone."

"Yes," said Gabu. "That is October."

Dango breathed it in. It was not unpleasant. It was interesting — the smell of something they had not experienced yet, coming from just ahead.

"What will October bring?" Dango said.

"More leaves falling," Gabu said. "Colder mornings. The last of the garden getting ready for winter."

"And the fire?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "In October we light the fire."

Dango smiled. It had been waiting for the fire.

"Anything else?" Dango said.

Gabu paused. He looked into the distance, as if he were thinking about something.

"Perhaps," he said. "Sometimes October brings something unexpected."

Dango looked at him. "What kind of unexpected?"

"The good kind," Gabu said. "Usually."

Dango looked at the gate. The vine on it had gone red — brilliant red, the leaves turning together, the whole gate framed in red leaves.

Something unexpected. The good kind.

Dango thought about that and felt a familiar feeling. The feeling from before Daru came. Before Dango itself came.

The feeling of something approaching.
