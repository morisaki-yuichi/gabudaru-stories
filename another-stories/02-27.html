February 27

Gabu Is Ready

🐢 Gabu

Gabu wakes up this morning with a clear feeling. He is ready.

Ready for what? He is not sure exactly. Ready for spring. Ready for whatever comes. Ready for something new.

He walks to his flat stone. He looks at his three stones. The flat one. The grey one. The blue one.

He thinks: my collection is ready.

He walks to the south wall. He looks at the flowers. Twelve now. More every day.

He thinks: the flowers are ready.

He walks to the big tree. The buds are swelling. Some of them are starting to show green at the tips.

He thinks: the tree is ready.

He walks to the pond. The frog is there. It looks at him.

He thinks: the frog is ready.

He walks to the gate. He stands in front of it.

He thinks: am I ready to go through?

He waits. He thinks carefully.

Not yet. But soon.

He walks away from the gate. He walks to the middle of the garden.

He looks at everything around him. His garden. His home.

He thinks: I am ready for whatever comes. Into my garden, out of my garden, wherever. I am ready.

This feeling is different from before. Before, he was waiting. Patient and still. Now he is ready. Active inside. Awake.

He stands tall. Well, as tall as a turtle can stand.

"I am ready," he says.

He does not know what it means. But it feels true.

He closes his eyes.

He takes a deep breath.

He sleeps, but lightly. Ready to wake.
