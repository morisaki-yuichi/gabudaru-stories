April 20

Daru Finds Something

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Daru finds something.

He is near the east wall, looking at the climbing plant, when he sees it.

A door. Small and wooden. In the wall. Very low down, half hidden by the climbing plant.

"Gabu!" he calls.

Gabu comes.

"Look," says Daru.

Gabu looks.

He has never seen this door before. Or maybe he has. Maybe he passed it a hundred times without seeing it.

He walks up to it. He looks at it closely.

It is a door. Small, grey with age, with a tiny iron ring.

He pulls the ring with his foot. It does not open.

He tries harder. The door is stuck. Or locked.

He and Daru look at it together.

"What is behind it?" asks Daru.

"I don't know," says Gabu.

"Shall we try to open it?"

Gabu thinks. "Not today."

Daru looks at him. "Why not?"

Gabu thinks. "Because some things are better discovered slowly. We know it is here. Let that be enough for now."

Daru looks at the little door. He thinks about this.

"All right," he says. "For now."

They look at the little door for a while.

Then they walk away together.

Gabu thinks about the door all afternoon. What is behind it? Another part of the garden? Something outside?

He does not know.

But he knows it is there.

He closes his eyes.

He thinks about all the doors in his garden. The gate. The little wooden door.

He sleeps.
