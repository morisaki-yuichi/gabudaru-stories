August 2

The Green Apple Test

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango had been patient.

For weeks it had watched the apple that Gabu had marked. Every few days it looked at it. It was getting bigger. Getting rounder.

On this morning, Dango looked up at the apple and said: "I think it might be ready."

Gabu looked up. The apple was big. Still mostly green, but with a yellow patch on the side that got the most sun.

"Not yet," Gabu said.

Dango bit its lip. "Are you sure?"

"Touch it," Gabu said. "Gently push on it."

Dango reached up and pressed very lightly on the apple. It was hard. Very hard.

"Still firm," Gabu said. "When it is ready, it will give a little under your finger. Like something soft inside."

Dango touched it again. Hard, hard, hard.

"How much longer?" it said.

"A month? Maybe more."

Dango looked at Gabu. "You have more patience than anyone."

Gabu smiled. "I have had a lot of practice."

Daru came and touched the apple too. He pressed it. Hard.

"We will know when it is ready," Daru said. "It will tell us."

"By letting go," Dango said. It remembered what Daru had told it in June, before Dango had even arrived: when something is ready, it lets go.

Dango nodded. "All right," it said. "I will wait."

And it stepped back from the apple and let it hang there, growing, in the August sun.
