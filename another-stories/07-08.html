July 8

Dango!

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

The small round shape stepped through the gate.

It was a turtle. Smaller than Daru. Rounder, like a little ball with feet. Its shell was pale brown with small spots. Its eyes were wide and looked in two directions at once, curious about everything.

It stopped when it saw Daru.

They looked at each other.

"Hello," Daru said. He kept his voice soft.

The small turtle did not run. It looked at Daru carefully. Then it took one more step forward.

"Hello," it said. Its voice was very small. Like the sound they had been hearing — thin and high, like a question.

Gabu came out from the house. He stopped when he saw the small turtle.

The small turtle looked at Gabu. Then back at Daru. Its eyes were very bright.

"I heard voices," it said. "I followed the sound. For many days."

Gabu walked over slowly and crouched down. "You are welcome here," he said. "What is your name?"

The small turtle was quiet for a moment. "Dango," it said. "My name is Dango."

Daru smiled. "I am Daru," he said. "And this is Gabu. We live here."

Dango looked around the garden. Its round eyes moved from the pond to the apple tree to the flowers to the shelter. It looked like someone seeing a picture in a book come to life.

"Can I come in?" it said.

"You are already in," Daru said gently.

Dango looked down at its feet. They were inside the garden gate. It had not even noticed.

"Oh," it said. And for the first time, it smiled.
