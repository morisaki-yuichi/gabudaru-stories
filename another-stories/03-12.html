March 12

Gabu Meets a Ladybug

🐢 Gabu
0:00

There is a small red and black spot on a leaf near the rose bush.

Gabu looks at it. The spot moves.

He looks more closely. It is a ladybug. Walking along the leaf.

He has not seen a ladybug since last autumn.

"Hello," says Gabu. "You are back."

The ladybug stops. It cleans its face with its front leg. Then it walks again.

It is red with black spots. Six black spots. Gabu counts them.

He looks at the ladybug from every angle. The red is very bright. The black is very black. Together, bright and strong.

The ladybug reaches the end of the leaf. It spreads its wings. It flies away. Gone in a moment.

Gabu looks at the empty leaf.

He thinks: it was here, and then it was not.

He looks around for other ladybugs. He finds three more on nearby plants.

He watches each one. They move quickly. They are busy. Each one eats small bugs on the leaves. They are working.

He thinks: the ladybug is small but useful. It helps the plants by eating pests. It helps the garden.

He thinks: small things can be very helpful.

He looks at himself. He is small. Is he helpful?

He walks to the south wall. He looks at his flowers. He has looked at them every day. He has said hello to them every day.

He thinks: maybe that is helpful too. Paying attention. Being there.

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He is glad for ladybugs.

He sleeps.
