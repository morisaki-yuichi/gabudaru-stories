December 20

The Solstice

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The shortest day.

Dango was up before dawn. It sat at the window and watched the darkness.

The sky lightened slowly. Grey, then pale blue, then a thin orange at the horizon. The sun came up very low — the lowest it would be all year.

Dango watched the light move across the garden. Slow. The shadows were very long.

By midday, the sun was low even at noon. The light was gold and slanted even at the middle of the day. Everything had long shadows.

Dango walked around the garden. Its shadow was longer than it had ever been.

"I am tall today," it said.

Suke-san, passing, looked at the shadow. "Winter shadows are the longest," it said.

The afternoon came quickly. By three, the gold light was already beginning.

Dango stood in the garden as the gold deepened.

Gabu came and stood beside it.

"Here it is," Dango said.

The gold light on the bare apple tree. On the frost. On the pond's ice. On the garden's brown stems.

Everything gold. Everything glowing.

Dango and Gabu stood together until the sun set — the earliest sunset of the year.

"The shortest day," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"And tomorrow?"

Gabu smiled. "One minute longer," he said.

Dango looked at the darkening sky.

"One minute," it said. "The year turns."

They went inside.

The year had turned. The long way back to summer had begun.
