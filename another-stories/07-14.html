July 14

The Smell of Bread

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

One morning, Gabu made bread.

He made it in the small clay oven at the back of the house. He had made bread many times. It took most of the morning.

The smell spread through the whole garden.

Daru and Dango were at the pond when they smelled it.

Dango lifted its nose. "What is that smell?"

"Bread," said Daru. "Gabu is making bread."

Dango breathed it in. "I have never smelled that before."

"It smells like warmth," Daru said. "Gabu says bread is the smell of home."

Dango kept sniffing. The smell was rich and good and came in warm waves when the breeze blew from the direction of the house.

They came into the house at noon, when Gabu called them. The bread was on the table, still warm. Gabu cut it into thick slices.

Dango picked up its piece and just held it for a moment, feeling the warmth.

Then it took a bite.

Its eyes closed.

Gabu and Daru watched.

After a moment, Dango opened its eyes. "It is the best thing," it said. "The best thing I have ever eaten."

"You say that about many things," Daru said, smiling.

"Yes," Dango said. "Because there are many best things."

Gabu laughed softly. He cut another slice for each of them.

They ate slowly at the table while the warm bread smell stayed in the room.
