March 16

Three Frogs

🐢 Gabu

There are three frogs at the pond today.

Gabu counts them.

One is on the stone at the far edge. One is on the mud near the grass. One is in the water, with just its eyes showing above the surface.

Three frogs.

He walks around the pond slowly. He looks at each frog.

The first frog: green, fat, sitting very still on its stone. Its eyes blink slowly.

The second frog: smaller, browner. Moving a little. Eating something small.

The third frog: in the water. Very still. Watching.

Gabu sits near the second frog, the one he knows.

"Hello," he says.

The frog says: "Rrr."

He looks at the other two frogs.

"Are they your friends?" he asks.

The frog says: "Rrr, rrr."

Gabu thinks that might mean yes.

He watches the three frogs for a long time. They are all different. Different sizes. Different colors. Different personalities, maybe.

He thinks: they are together at the pond. All three, today.

He thinks about having friends. Not one, but several. Three. All different.

He smiles.

He watches the frogs until the sun starts to go down.

One by one, the frogs go into the water.

The pond is quiet again.

He thinks: they will be back tomorrow.

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He is warm.

He sleeps.
