March 14

Gabu Climbs the Rock

🐢 Gabu

Gabu goes to the old stone in the corner today.

He wants to climb it.

He has climbed it before, but not lately. Not since winter.

He walks to it. He looks at it. The stone is tall and a little bit steep on one side. But not too steep.

He puts his front feet on it. He pushes. He goes up a little.

He keeps going. Slowly, step by step.

He gets to the top.

He looks out at the garden.

It is very different from up here. He can see everything. The whole garden from above.

He sees: the pond is shining. The apple tree is still pink and white, petals falling. The big tree is covered in small new leaves. The south wall flowers are a bright patch of color. The gate stands dark in the wall.

He turns slowly. He looks in all four directions.

He can see over the wall from here. Just a little. He can see the top of a tree outside. He can see the sky above the outside world.

He looks at the outside tree. It has leaves too, the same as his tree. Spring is out there too.

"Hello, outside tree," he says.

He stays on the big stone for a long time.

He feels very good up here. Like he can see everything at once. Like the whole world is his garden.

He climbs back down carefully. Step by step.

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He thinks about the view from the top of the stone.

He sleeps.
