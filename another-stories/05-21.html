May 21

Gabu Looks for Daru

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today the same thing happens again. Daru is not in his spot.

But this time Gabu is not worried. He looks more carefully.

He goes to the apple tree first. Not there.

He goes to the far corner. Not there.

He goes to the pond.

There. Daru is in the water. He went in early this morning, before Gabu woke up.

Gabu goes to the edge of the pond.

"Good morning," says Daru. He is floating on his back. Well, trying to. Turtles do not float well on their backs. He is partly on his side.

"You went swimming early," says Gabu.

"I wanted to see the pond in the morning," says Daru. "It is different in the morning."

Gabu looks at the pond. The early light on the water. The mist above it. The pond is indeed different.

He says: "How is it different?"

Daru says: "Quieter. And the light is lower. The reflections are longer."

Gabu looks at the reflections. The trees and sky stretched long across the water.

"I have never been here this early before," says Gabu.

Daru says: "Come in."

Gabu hesitates. Then he walks in. The water is cool. Cool from the night.

He swims to Daru.

They float together in the early morning pond.

The mist rises.

The frogs are quiet.

Everything is still.

Gabu thinks: Daru finds new things even in places I know well.

He is glad.

He closes his eyes.

He floats.

He sleeps, floating on the water.
