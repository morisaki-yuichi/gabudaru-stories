July 19

Gabu's Hat

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Gabu had a hat.

It was old and wide-brimmed. He wore it in summer to keep the sun off his head. He put it on in the morning and took it off at night.

Dango saw it for the first time on a hot morning.

"What is that?" Dango said.

"A hat," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the hat with great interest. "Can I try it?"

Gabu took it off and put it on Dango's head. It was too big. It fell down over Dango's eyes.

Dango lifted the brim and peered out. "I cannot see," it said.

Daru laughed.

Dango took it off and looked at it. It was straw-colored, with a dark band. There was a small feather tucked into the band.

"Where did the feather come from?" Dango said.

"A bird left it," Gabu said. "A long time ago."

Dango put the feather back carefully. Then it handed the hat back to Gabu.

"I want a hat," Dango said.

"Then we will make you one," Gabu said.

In the afternoon, they wove a small hat out of long grass. It was not as good as Gabu's hat. It was lumpy and a little lopsided. But it stayed on Dango's head.

Dango put it on and walked around the garden looking very pleased.

"I have a hat," it said.

"You do," said Daru.

Dango wore the grass hat all day, even at dinner.
