March 20

The Longest Day of Spring

🐢 Gabu

Today is the equinox. Gabu does not know that word. But he feels it.

The day and the night are equal today. Gabu has noticed the days getting longer since January. But today they are exactly balanced. Equal.

The sun rises at the same time it sets. Twelve hours of day. Twelve hours of night.

Gabu walks out early. He watches the sun come up.

The sun comes up over the east wall. Slow and golden. It lights up the garden.

He watches the sun all day. He cannot watch it directly, of course. But he watches where its light falls.

In the morning: the west side of the garden.

In the middle of the day: everywhere.

In the afternoon: the east side of the garden.

He watches the shadows move. Like a slow clock.

When the sun goes down behind the west wall, the garden is golden. Then orange. Then pink.

He watches until the last light is gone.

Then the stars come out.

He looks at the stars. He cannot count them. Too many.

He thinks: today the sun and the stars had equal time.

He thinks: everything has a balance. Day and night. Sun and rain. Warm and cold.

He thinks about his garden. Sunny spots and shady spots. Warm and cool. Wet and dry. All in balance.

He thinks: I live in a balanced place.

He goes to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He is peaceful.

He sleeps.
