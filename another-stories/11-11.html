November 11

A Day of Stories

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

It rained all day.

A cold, steady November rain. They stayed inside.

By midmorning, Dango said: "Tell me stories."

So they told stories. All day.

Suke-san told about a mountain pass it had crossed in summer and how the view at the top had been so vast that it had stayed still for an hour, unable to move.

Gabu told about a young turtle who had planted an apple seed and waited fifteen years for it to give fruit.

Daru told about a pond he had lived near before coming here — a pond that was beautiful in summer but froze solid every winter, so the fish had nowhere to go. "What happened to the fish?" Dango said. "I never knew," Daru said. "I think they swam very deep."

Dango told about the time it had tried to follow a butterfly and walked so far from where it started that it could not find its way back until the next morning.

They told old stories and new ones. Real ones and ones they made up.

At dinner, Dango said: "I think we have enough stories between us for the whole winter."

"Stories are not like wood," Gabu said. "They do not run out."

"They get better," Suke-san said. "The more you tell them."

Dango thought: next year there will be new stories. And the stories of this year will be old, and old means trusted, and trusted means good.

It looked forward to next year's stories already.
