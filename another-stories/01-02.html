January 2

Gabu's Shell

🐢 Gabu

It is the second day of January. The sky is white. There is no wind. The garden is very still.

Gabu wakes up and looks at his shell. It is round and smooth. It has a pattern of green and brown and yellow. Each part is different. Some parts are dark. Some parts are light.

"I like my shell," says Gabu.

He touches it with one foot. It is hard. He taps it gently. It makes a small sound.

He walks to the big tree. He walks under it and looks up. The branches are bare. No leaves at this time of year. But the tree is still beautiful. It is very big and very old.

Gabu bumps his shell against the tree trunk. It makes a soft thump. Gabu is not hurt. His shell keeps him safe.

"Good tree," says Gabu.

He finds some old leaves on the ground near the tree. They are brown and dry. He pushes them with his nose. They make a rustling sound. He likes that sound.

He eats a little grass near the wall. It is not very tasty in winter. But it is something.

In the afternoon, Gabu sits on his flat stone. He looks at the grey sky. Clouds move slowly. He watches them for a long time.

One cloud looks like a bird. One cloud looks like a leaf.

"That one looks like me," says Gabu. There is a round, fat cloud just above the tree.

He laughs a little.

When evening comes, Gabu is warm under his shell. He curls up. He closes his eyes. He sleeps.
