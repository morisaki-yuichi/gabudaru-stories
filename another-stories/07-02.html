July 2

The Pond in July

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru
0:00

The pond in July was different from the pond in June.

The water was warmer. The green plants on the surface had spread until they covered nearly half the water. Under the surface, Daru could see small fish — he had not noticed them before.

"There are fish in here," he said.

Gabu looked in. "Three," he said. "There have always been three fish."

Daru watched them. They were small and silver. They moved together, all in the same direction, as if they were one thing.

"Why do they move like that?" Daru said.

"Fish feel safe when they are together," Gabu said. "When one turns, the others follow."

Daru watched the fish turn and turn again. They never bumped into each other. They never lost each other.

"They are good at being together," Daru said.

He watched for a long time. The fish circled the pond. They went under the floating leaves and came out the other side. They rose to the surface and then dove back down.

After a while, Daru got into the water with them.

The fish scattered at first. But then they came back. They circled him, curious. One came very close to his foot and then darted away.

Daru held very still.

The fish came back again. They moved around him as if he were just another part of the pond.

"They accept me," Daru said quietly.

"They are used to turtles," Gabu said from the edge. "They have lived here a long time."
