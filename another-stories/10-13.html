October 13

Waiting with Patience

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Three days had passed since the singing.

The gate was still open. Gabu had left it open every morning and closed it at night.

Dango was finding it hard.

On the third day, it said to Daru: "What if we imagined it? The singing?"

"We did not imagine it," Daru said. "All three of us heard it."

"Then where are they?"

Daru thought. He remembered his own walk. "When I came here," he said, "I walked back and forth along the path many times. I heard Gabu and Gabu's garden from far away. But I was not sure I should come in."

Dango looked at him. "Why not?"

"I was not sure I would be welcome," Daru said. "Even though I wanted to be."

Dango was quiet.

"So they might be close," it said. "Just... uncertain."

"Maybe," said Daru.

Dango went to the gate. It stood there and looked out at the path.

Then it did something Gabu had not done and Daru had not done.

It sat down in the open gate. Half in, half out. Visible from the path.

"I will sit here," Dango said. "So if someone comes near, they will see that there is someone inside. That it is a real place."

Daru watched Dango sit in the gate, small and round, visible from the path.

"That is a good idea," he said.

"I know what it is like to be uncertain," Dango said. "I want them to see that it is safe here."
