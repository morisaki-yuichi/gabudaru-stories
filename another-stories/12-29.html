December 29

Winter Stars, One More Time

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

On a clear cold night, they went out to look at the stars again.

This had become something they did when the nights were clear.

They lay on the flat stone by the pond — all four of them. Their breath made clouds. The stars were sharp and cold and many.

Suke-san named the winter stars one more time. Dango and Daru repeated them.

"Now I know them," Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san.

Dango found the three in a line. Found the bright blue-white one. Found the red-orange one.

"I will remember these next winter," Dango said. "And the winter after."

"Yes," said Suke-san.

"Will you teach me more?" Dango said. "The spring stars and summer stars?"

"Yes," said Suke-san. "When they appear."

Dango thought about a whole year of star-learning.

The stone was cold under them but the cold was bearable with the company.

Gabu was looking at a cluster — many stars close together.

"That one," he said. "I have been looking at it all my life. I never learned its name."

He looked at Suke-san.

Suke-san looked at the cluster. "The Seven Sisters," it said. "Six are easy to see. The seventh is faint."

Gabu found the six. Counted them.

"I have been looking at them for thirty years," he said. "Now I know."

"Now you know," Suke-san said.

They looked at the Seven Sisters.

It was never too late to learn a name.
