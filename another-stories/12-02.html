December 2

The Gold Light

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Daru had been right.

At three-thirty in the afternoon, the sun came low through the bare apple tree.

Dango was at the window as it had promised.

The light came in golden-orange, through the bare branches, making long shadows on the frost. Everything it touched turned warm-colored — the dark tree trunks glowed with orange. The dry grass was pale gold. Even the grey pond had a golden strip across it.

Dango pressed its face to the cold glass.

"Oh," it said.

Daru came to the window.

"There it is," he said quietly.

They watched together.

The light lasted about twenty minutes. The sun moved lower. The gold deepened to orange, then to deep red at the very edge of the garden wall. The shadows got longer and longer.

Then the sun was gone. In seconds, the gold went out.

The garden was grey again.

But the image stayed.

Dango turned from the window. Its eyes were bright.

"I understand," it said.

"What?" Daru said.

"Why you remembered this," Dango said. "From last December. Why you told me."

Daru nodded.

"It is like a gift," Dango said. "At the end of the grey day. Just before dark. A gift."

"Every day," said Daru. "In December."

Dango turned back to the window. The garden was dark now. But tomorrow it would come again.

"I will not miss it," Dango said.
