July 21

Dango Finds the Journal

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango found Gabu's journal.

It was sitting on the bench outside, where Gabu had left it in the morning.

Dango picked it up and turned it over. It was old and soft. It smelled of paper and time.

Dango brought it to Gabu. "What is this?"

"My garden journal," Gabu said. "I write things I notice."

Dango's eyes lit up. "Can I read it?"

Gabu thought. Then he said: "Yes. But carefully."

Dango sat with the journal for a long time. It turned the pages slowly. It read every entry. Its eyes moved back and forth.

After a while, it looked up. "Daru wrote something in here," it said.

"Yes," Daru said. "In June. Gabu let me write one thing."

Dango looked back at the page. It read Daru's words: "The water is warm now. Floating is easy." And beside it, Daru's name in Gabu's handwriting.

"Can I write something too?" Dango said.

Gabu got the pencil.

Dango thought for a long time. Then it wrote, very carefully: "Everything is new here and that is good."

It handed the journal back to Gabu. Gabu read the words. He wrote Dango's name beside them.

"Now both of you are in the book," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the two entries side by side. Daru's from June. Its own from July.

"The book is about all of us now," Dango said.

"It was always going to be," Gabu said.
