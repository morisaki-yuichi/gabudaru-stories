February 28

The Last Day of February

🐢 Gabu
0:00

Today is the last day of February.

Gabu walks around his garden slowly. He wants to see everything before the month ends.

He goes to the south wall first. He counts the flowers. Fifteen flowers. More than he has ever had this early in the year.

He goes to the big tree. The buds are green at the tips. Really green. Tomorrow will be March. In March, leaves will come.

He goes to the pond. The frog is there. It is more awake now. It moves around more. It even catches a small bug while Gabu is watching.

He goes to the gate. He puts his nose to the crack. The outside world smells warm today. Alive and open.

He walks to the old stone in the corner. He climbs up on it. He looks at the whole garden.

It looks different from January. The green is coming. The flowers are here. The tree is starting to open. The pond is alive.

He remembers January. Grey and cold. Quiet. Just him.

He thinks: February was better than January. Not because January was bad. But because things grew and changed.

He climbs down.

He walks to his flat stone.

He sits next to his three stones.

"Thank you, February," he says. "You had the south wind. And the blue stone. And the butterfly. And the fog. And the frog. And the flowers."

He thinks: February had many good things.

He closes his eyes.

He thinks about tomorrow. March. Spring.

He is ready.

He smiles.

He sleeps.
