December 19

The Night Before the Solstice

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Tomorrow would be the shortest day.

They sat by the fire as always. But it felt different tonight — significant. Like the edge of something.

Dango said: "What do we do for the solstice?"

Gabu thought. "I usually sit with it," he said. "Notice it."

"That is all?" Dango said.

"That is enough," said Gabu.

Suke-san said: "I have seen the solstice in many places. Always there is a feeling. Like a held breath."

"The year holding its breath," Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san.

Daru said: "And then it breathes out. And begins again."

Dango thought about that. The year breathing out. All the months — January to December, the whole long exhale — and then: the solstice. The breath in. The beginning.

"I want to be awake for it," Dango said. "For the actual moment."

"The solstice is not one moment," Gabu said. "It is the whole day. The shortest one."

"Then I want to be awake for the whole day," Dango said.

"That you can do," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the fire.

"Next solstice," it said, "we will have been here a whole year. All of us."

Gabu looked at Dango. "Not Suke-san," he said. "Not quite. Suke-san came in October."

Suke-san said: "Close enough."

"Close enough," Gabu agreed.

The fire crackled. Tomorrow was the turning point.

They went to sleep warm, and the year held its breath.
