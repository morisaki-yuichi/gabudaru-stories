November 8

Digging Up the Letters

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Wait — they had agreed to wait until spring.

But one morning Dango said: "Can we look at the letters?"

"We said spring," Daru said.

"I know," Dango said. "But I want to read mine now. I forgot what I wrote."

Gabu thought. "We agreed to open them in spring," he said. "Together. To see what came true."

Dango looked at the garden, at the small stone marker by the rose bush.

"All right," it said.

But it stood at the window looking at the stone for a while.

Suke-san came and stood beside it. "What did you write?" it said.

"I cannot remember exactly," Dango said. "Something about wanting to still be here."

"And you are," Suke-san said.

Dango turned. "Yes. So it already came true."

"Most of the year's hopes come true before you open the letter," Suke-san said. "That is why you open it in spring — to see how much more came true than you expected."

Dango looked at the buried spot again. "So I hope for things and then the year gives them before I can even read back what I hoped?"

"Often," said Suke-san.

Dango thought about this. It thought about the hopes it had had in September. To still be here. To have friends. To see winter through.

"I should have written bigger hopes," it said.

"Write them now," Suke-san said. "In the journal. For next autumn."

Dango went and found a pencil. It wrote for a long time.

Bigger hopes for next year.
