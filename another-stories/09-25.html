September 25

The Wind Picks Up

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

In late September, the wind changed.

It came from a new direction — from the north now, not the south. It was stronger and colder. When it blew, the remaining leaves came down in yellow showers.

Dango stood in the garden and let the leaves fall on it.

"Leaf rain," it said.

Daru watched from the shelter.

Gabu was in the house. He looked out the window and saw Dango standing in the falling leaves with arms outstretched.

He smiled.

Then he went out.

"Come in," he said. "It is cold."

"One more minute," Dango said.

Gabu stood and watched.

Dango stood with its arms out and its face up. Leaves fell on it. Yellow and brown and one red one. They landed on Dango's shell, on its head, on its outstretched arms.

Then Dango lowered its arms. It looked at the leaves gathered on its shell and in the fold of its arms.

"They chose me," it said.

"Random," Gabu said.

"Maybe," said Dango. "But I was there."

It shook the leaves off gently. They fell to the ground.

"All right," Dango said. "Inside now."

Gabu made tea and they sat at the table. The wind made sounds around the house. The leaves came down outside the window in long, slow streams.

"I love September wind," Dango said.

"I thought you would," said Gabu.
