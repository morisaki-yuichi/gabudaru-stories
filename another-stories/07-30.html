July 30

The End of July Is Near

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

Two days left in July.

Gabu sat with the journal at his usual morning spot. He wrote:

"July. The month Dango came. Three of us now. The garden is noisier and fuller. Dango floats. Dango has a grass hat. Dango writes in this journal now."

He looked at what he had written. Then he added: "I did not know I was waiting for Dango. But I think I was."

Outside, Daru and Dango were arguing about something — not a bad argument, the cheerful kind. About which fish in the pond was fastest. Dango was sure it was the one with the most silver on its side. Daru was not sure.

Gabu listened to them from inside.

Two voices instead of one. And his own quiet one, which had been here the longest.

He thought: in April, Daru came. In July, Dango came. The garden grew bigger both times. Not the land, but the life in it.

He closed the journal.

Outside, the arguing had stopped. Now Daru and Dango were both looking at the fish. Watching.

Gabu went out.

"Which one is fastest?" he said.

They both turned to him.

"We cannot decide," Dango said.

"Then we will have to watch more," said Gabu.

They sat together at the edge of the pond. The three fish moved in their synchronized circle. Silver in the green water.

July was almost over. August was coming, full and warm.
