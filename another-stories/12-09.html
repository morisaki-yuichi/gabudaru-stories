December 9

Four Winter Mornings

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Every morning in December had its own quality.

The ninth morning was sharp and clear. The sun came up orange. The frost sparkled.

Gabu had noticed that each of them had a relationship with winter mornings.

Gabu went out first, always. He needed to feel the morning alone, just for a minute.

Daru came out second. He stood quietly beside Gabu, if Gabu was still there. He did not need to talk.

Suke-san came third. It moved slowly, took everything in with its careful eyes.

Dango came out last. It usually came out with great energy, made a sound at the cold, then looked around with great interest.

This pattern had been there since September. None of them had arranged it. It had simply happened.

On this morning, Gabu was at the garden's edge. Daru came and stood beside him. They looked at the frost together.

Suke-san came out and went to the pond to look at the ice.

Dango came out. "Good morning!" it said to the cold air. It looked around. "Oh. Everyone is out already."

"Yes," said Gabu.

Dango came and stood beside Gabu and Daru.

Four of them in the cold December morning.

"Good morning," Dango said again, more quietly this time.

"Good morning," said the garden back, in the way gardens do — with light on frost and the smell of cold and the small sound of distant birds.
