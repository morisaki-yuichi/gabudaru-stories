April 3

The Garden Changes

🐢 Gabu

Gabu looks at his garden today and feels it is different.

Not different in a bad way. Different in a good way. Like something is being prepared.

The flowers are more than ever. He stopped counting. There are too many to count now.

The grass is tall and green and thick near the walls.

The big tree is completely full of leaves now. A full canopy of green.

The climbing plant on the east wall has reached the top and is going along the top of the wall.

The pond is alive with frog calls every morning and evening.

Butterflies visit every sunny afternoon.

The garden is full, Gabu thinks. Full and ready.

He walks to the middle of the garden and turns around slowly. He looks at everything.

He thinks: my garden is beautiful right now.

He is proud of it. He did not make it himself. It is just what it is, what it grows to be. But he is part of it. He is here. He tends it with his presence and his attention.

He walks to the gate.

He puts his hand on the gate.

He thinks: if someone came through here, what would they see?

They would see this. The green and the flowers and the pond and the tree.

He thinks: they would like it.

He smiles.

He walks to his flat stone.

He looks at his three stones. The flat one. The grey one. The blue one. And his feather.

He is happy.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
