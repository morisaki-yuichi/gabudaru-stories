December 22

Daru Makes Dinner

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Daru made dinner.

He did not make it often — that was usually Gabu. But this evening, Gabu was tired and Daru said: "Sit. I will make it."

Gabu sat.

Daru made a simple soup — what was in the root vegetable store, with dried herbs and a little of the bone broth Gabu had been keeping.

Dango came to help. Daru let it stir.

Suke-san chopped the vegetables when Daru asked.

By the time it was done, everyone had put something in.

Dango tasted it. "This is good," it said.

Daru brought it to the table. Four bowls.

Gabu sat with them and ate.

"You made this," he said to Daru.

"We all did, a little," Daru said.

Gabu tasted his soup. It was different from his own — different proportions, a slightly different choice of herbs. But very good.

"You cook like someone who has been watching for a long time," Gabu said.

"I have," said Daru. "I watched every meal you made."

Gabu held his bowl. He thought about all the meals he had made alone, and the meals he had made with Daru watching, and the meals he had made while explaining to Dango, and the meals Suke-san had helped with.

All of it had led to this: Daru making soup for all of them.

"Good," said Gabu. "Keep watching. And keep cooking."

"I will," said Daru.
