February 16

Mud

🐢 Gabu
0:00

It rained last night. A lot. This morning the garden is very wet.

Gabu walks out. His feet sink into the mud.

He does not like mud very much. It is cold and sticky and it gets between his toes.

But he walks.

The pond has more water in it. It is higher. It almost reaches the grass at the edge.

He walks to the south wall. The ground there is muddy. He slips a little.

"Careful," he says to himself.

He finds the mud near the apple tree is very deep. He takes a step and sinks in up to his knee. He pulls his foot out. It makes a sucking sound.

He decides to stay on the higher, drier ground.

He walks along the path where the flat stones are. These stay drier.

He finds a good muddy patch near the big tree. Here the mud is the right kind. Not too wet. Not too deep. He puts one foot in.

Nice. Cool and smooth. He puts another foot in.

He walks through this mud slowly. He leaves deep footprints.

He looks at his footprints.

"I was here," he says.

He thinks about how his footprints will dry. Later, when the sun comes and dries the mud, his footprints will be there. Hard. A record of where he walked today.

He makes a few more footprints on purpose.

He goes back to his flat stone.

He cleans his feet on the grass.

He is a little dirty. He does not mind.

He thinks: mud is a record of where you have been.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
