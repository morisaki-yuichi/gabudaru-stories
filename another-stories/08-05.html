August 5

Dango's Shelf

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Gabu cleared a space on the low shelf near the window.

"This part is yours," he said to Dango.

Dango looked at the empty shelf. Then it laid out its things carefully. The blue-grey feather. The round stone. The bark with wave patterns. The stone with a white stripe.

It stepped back and looked.

"It looks like a beginning," Dango said.

"All collections begin somewhere," said Gabu.

Daru came and looked at the shelf too. He had a few things on the higher shelf — a dried flower from spring, a smooth piece of river glass that he had found on his walk in June.

"What is that piece of glass?" Dango asked.

"I found it," Daru said. "Long ago. Before I came here. I carried it the whole way."

Dango picked it up carefully. It was smooth and green, with rounded edges. Sea-worn.

"It has been many places," Dango said.

"Yes," said Daru.

Dango put it back carefully.

They stood together and looked at both shelves. Daru's things on the top. Dango's on the bottom. Gabu's journal on the middle shelf, where it had always been.

Three people, three sets of small things.

"The house feels more full," Dango said.

Gabu looked at the shelves. He had always lived alone. He had always had one shelf.

"Yes," he said. "It does."
