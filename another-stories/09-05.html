September 5

The Smell of Woodsmoke

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

From somewhere beyond the wall, the smell of woodsmoke came.

Someone in a nearby garden had lit a fire.

Dango lifted its nose.

"What is that?" it said.

"Woodsmoke," said Gabu.

"It smells warm," Dango said.

"Yes. In autumn, people light fires."

Dango breathed in the smell. It was not unpleasant. It was the smell of warmth that came from work — from cutting wood and laying it and lighting it and tending it.

"Will we have a fire?" Dango said.

Gabu nodded. "When it gets colder. I have a small fireplace inside. On cold evenings, we will light it."

Dango thought about this. A fire inside. Warmth. The three of them around it.

"I want that," Dango said.

"Not yet," Gabu said. "September evenings are still warm enough. Wait for October."

Daru was listening. "I remember last autumn," he said. "The fire on cold nights was very good."

"You were here last autumn?" Dango said.

"Yes," Daru said. "I arrived in April. I was here for autumn and winter."

Dango looked at Daru. "What was winter like?"

Daru thought. "Quiet," he said. "The garden was white sometimes. We stayed inside more. But it was not bad."

"Because of Gabu," Gabu said, pretending to be modest.

Daru laughed. "Yes," he said. "Because of Gabu."
