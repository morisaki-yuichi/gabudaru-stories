June 25

The Night Garden

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

They stayed outside after dark to watch the fireflies again.

The garden at night was very different. The colors were gone — everything was silver and grey and black. But the shapes were still there. The apple tree. The rose bush. The shelter. The pond.

Daru walked slowly through the garden. The grass was cool and wet with dew. The air smelled different at night — sweeter, somehow. The flowers let out more smell in the dark.

"The garden is two gardens," Daru said.

"What do you mean?" said Gabu.

"A day garden and a night garden," Daru said. "The same place. But different."

Gabu thought about this. "You are right," he said. "I have lived here many years, but I think you see things I stopped seeing."

Daru looked at him. "Maybe because it is all still new to me," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "That is a gift."

They stood by the pond. The fireflies were still going. The moon was up now, nearly full, and it put a white path across the water.

Mido the frog was sitting at the edge. It sat very still, watching the moon path on the water.

The three of them — Gabu, Daru, and Mido — were quiet together.

After a long time, Daru said, "I am glad I came here."

Gabu put a hand on Daru's shell. Just briefly. He did not say anything.

He did not need to.
