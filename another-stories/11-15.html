November 15

Four Things to Be Grateful For

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

It was Gabu's custom.

On the middle day of November, he wrote in the journal: four things he was grateful for this year.

He had done this alone for many years.

This year, he asked the others to join.

They sat at the table. Each one thought.

Dango went first. It said: "One: the first time I floated. Two: the night with the fireflies. Three: the day Suke-san arrived. Four: the sound of everyone sleeping."

Daru went next. "One: the long evenings in June. Two: teaching Dango to be still. Three: the apple on August 20th. Four: four cups on the table every morning."

Suke-san spoke slowly. "One: the open gate. Two: Dango sitting in the gate. Three: the first fire. Four: being known."

They all looked at Suke-san. Being known. That was the most important kind of welcome.

Gabu went last. He wrote as he spoke. "One: Daru's steadiness. Two: Dango's wonder. Three: Suke-san's presence. Four: this table."

He looked at what he had written.

"You made us the grateful things," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Is that allowed?" Dango said.

"It is your list," said Gabu. "You can put anything on it."

Dango looked at Gabu's four things. Then at the table.

"A table," it said. "I would not have thought to put that."

"A table is where everything happens," Gabu said. "Meals. Stories. Letters. The journal. Plans. Gratitude."

Dango looked at the old wooden table with new eyes.
