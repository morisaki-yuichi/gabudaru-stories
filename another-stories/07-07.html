July 7

Waiting at the Gate

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

In the morning, Gabu went to the gate.

He opened it wide and left it open.

Daru came and stood beside him. They looked out at the path. It was green and quiet in the morning. The trees were still. The air smelled of wet leaves and warm earth.

Nothing.

They waited.

A butterfly flew past the open gate. It went in, circled the garden, and went out again.

Still nothing.

Daru sat down just inside the gate. He watched the path.

After a while, Gabu went to make breakfast. "Stay here if you want," he said. "Call me if anything comes."

Daru stayed. He sat very still, like Gabu had taught him for watching birds.

A leaf fell on the path. A small wind came and went. The shadow of the gate moved slowly as the sun rose.

Then Daru heard it.

Very faint. From the direction of the clearing. The small high sound, like a question.

Daru did not shout. He sat perfectly still.

The sound came again. Closer.

Then he heard footsteps on the path. Small ones.

Daru's heart beat fast. He breathed slowly. He waited.

The footsteps got closer. And closer.

Around the bend came a small, round shape, moving carefully through the leaf shadows.

Daru said nothing. He just watched.

The shape came toward the open gate.

Tomorrow it would arrive.
