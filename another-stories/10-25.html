October 25

October Passes

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Three quarters of October had passed.

The garden was bare. The trees near the wall were skeleton-shapes against the pale sky. The ground was covered in brown leaves. The pond was still as glass.

Gabu walked around the garden alone in the early morning. This was something he needed to do sometimes — to see the garden by himself, to think his own thoughts.

The others understood this. They stayed inside.

Gabu walked slowly. He noticed everything.

The rose bush with its last red hips, shrinking now as the cold took them. The compost pile, quiet and working. The shelter that Dango had built, now part of the garden as if it had always been there.

He thought: this garden has always changed.

When he was young, it was small and wild. As he worked, it became more itself. And each year it changed again, a little.

This year it had changed the most.

Daru. Dango. Suke-san.

He had not planned any of them. They had come like the vine on the gate — finding the place on their own.

He stood under the bare apple tree and looked up through its branches at the pale October sky.

He thought: next spring there will be apples again.

And mushrooms. And fireflies. And the swallows will return.

And they will all be here to see it.

He walked back to the house.

Inside, someone had made tea. Four cups on the table.

He sat down and put his hands around the warm cup.
