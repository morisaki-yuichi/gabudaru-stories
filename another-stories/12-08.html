December 8

After the Snow

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The snow did not last long.

Two days later, a warmer wind came and most of it melted. The garden was brown and wet again.

Dango was sad about this.

"It went too fast," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "The first snow often does."

"Will it come again?"

"Yes," said Gabu. "Most Decembers there is more."

Dango stood in the wet garden. The footprints were gone. The white was gone.

But it had been there. Dango knew that.

"I need to draw it," Dango said.

It went inside and drew the garden in snow, from memory. The white apple tree. The white path. The footprints.

The drawing was not perfect. But it held the memory.

Dango put the drawing in the journal.

Gabu found it there later and read Dango's small note beside the drawing: First snow. Dec 7.

He wrote beside it in his own hand: A real December at last.

Dango found this note and showed it to Daru.

"A real December," Dango said.

"Because all of us are here," Daru said.

Dango thought about Gabu's other Decembers, alone, with snow. He had survived them. He had been all right.

But this was different.

This was a real December.

Dango closed the journal carefully and put it back on the shelf, between its present and its past.
