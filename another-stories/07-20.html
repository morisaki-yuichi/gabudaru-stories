July 20

The Long Hot Week

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

A long hot week arrived.

Every day was the same: blue sky, bright sun, thick warm air. The bees moved slower. The flowers drooped a little by noon.

But Gabu and Daru and Dango made the most of it.

In the mornings, they went in the pond before it got too warm.

In the afternoons, they rested in the shade of the apple tree. Sometimes they talked. Sometimes they napped. Sometimes Dango told long rambling stories that went nowhere but were somehow very interesting.

In the evenings, they sat outside until it was dark, watching the fireflies and feeling the air cool down.

On the fourth hot day, Dango said: "What do you want most right now?"

"Cold water," said Daru.

"A cloud," said Gabu.

Dango thought. "I want some of that bread from last week," it said.

Gabu laughed. He went inside and made cold barley tea instead, which was almost as good.

They drank it by the pond. The water was warm now all the way down.

"Tell me," Gabu said to Dango. "Before you came here — where did you sleep?"

"Anywhere soft," Dango said. "Under trees. In tall grass. Once in a pile of leaves."

"Was it comfortable?" Daru said.

"Sometimes," Dango said. "But now I have my spot here." It looked toward the corner of the garden where it had made a sleeping place. "Now it is comfortable every night."

Gabu looked at the pond and felt something quiet and full.
