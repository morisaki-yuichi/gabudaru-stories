March 17

Gabu's Name

🐢 Gabu

Gabu is sitting by the pond when he starts to think about his name.

Gabu. That is his name. He has always been Gabu. He does not know where the name came from. It is just his name.

He says it out loud: "Gabu."

He says it again: "Gabu."

It is a short name. Two sounds. Ga-bu.

He thinks about what it means. Does it mean anything?

He does not know.

He walks to the big tree. He says his name to the tree: "Gabu."

The tree does not respond. But it feels right to say his name here.

He walks to the pond. He looks at his reflection in the water.

"Gabu," he says.

His reflection says nothing. But it looks like Gabu.

He thinks about the things that are his. His garden. His flat stone. His three stones. His tree. His pond. His wall. His gate.

And his name.

Gabu.

He thinks: these things make me who I am. My name. My place. My home.

He is glad to have a name. A name means you are someone. You have a place in the world.

He thinks: if a friend came to the garden, he would tell them his name. Gabu. I live here.

He smiles.

He walks to his flat stone.

He looks at his three stones.

He says: "I am Gabu. This is my garden. I am glad to be here."

He closes his eyes.

He is warm and content.

He sleeps.
