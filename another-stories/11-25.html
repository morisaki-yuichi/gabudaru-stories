November 25

The Ice Gets Thicker

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The ice on the pond got thicker.

Gabu checked it every morning — he pressed the edge with a stick to test.

On this morning, the ice was solid three centimeters deep.

"It is getting there," he said.

Dango stood behind him. "Getting to what?"

"Thick enough to walk on," Gabu said. "Maybe by December."

Dango looked at the ice. It was clear and flat, like a grey window. Beneath it, deep water.

Daru came and tested it too with a careful foot. It held.

"Not yet," said Gabu.

"How thick does it need to be?" Daru asked.

"For a turtle? Maybe five, six centimeters. Then it is safe."

Dango crouched and looked through the ice. The water below was dark. The three fish were somewhere deeper, invisible.

"Hello down there," Dango said.

Nothing.

"They cannot hear you," Daru said.

"They might feel the sound," Suke-san said from behind them.

Dango looked up. "Through the ice?"

"Vibrations," Suke-san said. "Sounds travel through ice."

Dango pressed its ear to the ice. Cold. It could hear its own heartbeat, amplified by the ice.

"What do you hear?" Suke-san asked.

"My heart," Dango said.

"That is enough," said Suke-san.

Dango stayed with its ear on the ice for a while, listening to itself.
