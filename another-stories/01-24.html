January 24

A Worm

🐢 Gabu

It rains today. Soft, quiet rain. The kind that falls straight down with no wind.

Gabu walks in the rain. He does not mind rain. His shell keeps most of him dry. His feet get wet, but feet can be wet.

He walks near the wall. The ground is soft. He sees a worm come up out of the earth. Then another. When it rains, worms come up.

He watches a worm on the path. It is long and pink-brown. It moves by stretching and contracting, over and over.

"Hello, worm," says Gabu.

The worm does not stop. It moves across the wet path.

Gabu walks beside it. Slowly. The worm moves quite fast for something with no legs.

"You have no shell," says Gabu. "Are you cold?"

The worm is not cold, probably. It is a worm. It does not care about cold in the same way.

But Gabu thinks it would be nice to have a shell if you were a worm. Round and warm. Safe inside.

He watches the worm until it disappears into the grass.

He stands in the rain. He looks up. The rain falls on his face. He blinks. The rain tastes clean.

He thinks: the worm came up because of rain. The rain is a gift for worms. The sun is a gift for me. Different creatures like different things.

He walks back toward his flat stone. The rain is getting heavier. He goes under the big tree. The tree keeps off some of the rain.

He sits there and watches the rain fall on the pond. It makes a thousand tiny circles.

Beautiful.

He watches until the rain stops.

He is happy.

He sleeps.
