October 2

The First Fire

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

That evening, Gabu built the fire.

Dango had been waiting for this since August, when Gabu had first mentioned it.

Gabu carried in three pieces of dry wood and laid them in the small clay fireplace near the wall inside the house.

He laid tinder underneath — dry grass and shredded bark. He struck the flint twice. A spark fell. The tinder caught.

Small flames appeared.

Dango watched from two feet away, eyes wide.

The fire grew. Gabu added a small piece of wood. It caught. He added another.

The room changed. It got warm. It got orange. The walls had moving light on them.

Dango sat down on the floor in front of the fire.

"Oh," it said.

Gabu sat in his chair. Daru sat on the bench beside the fire.

The fire crackled and shifted. A small log popped. The warmth spread.

Dango held its hands toward the fire. "It is warm on this side," it said. "And still cool behind."

"The fire makes its own weather," Gabu said.

Dango sat very close to the warmth. Its shadow moved on the wall behind it.

"I want to sit here all winter," Dango said.

"You can," said Gabu.

Dango looked at the fire. The flames were orange and yellow and white at the base. They moved constantly.

"No two moments are the same," Dango said.

"No," said Gabu. "That is what fire is."
