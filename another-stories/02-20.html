February 20

The Frog Goes Away

🐢 Gabu

Today the frog is not at the pond.

Gabu goes to the pond in the morning. He looks. No frog.

He waits. He watches the muddy edge of the pond. No frog comes up.

He walks around the whole pond. He looks in all the places the frog usually is. No frog.

He sits and waits.

In the afternoon, still no frog.

He calls softly: "Hello? Frog?"

Nothing.

He thinks: where did it go?

He is not very worried. Frogs come and go. They hide under mud. They sleep. They go away and come back.

But he misses it.

He sits by the empty pond. The pond is the same. The water. The mud. The stones at the edge. But without the frog, it is different.

He thinks about this. He thinks: when someone is there, a place is different. When they go, the place feels different. Not bad, just different.

He thinks about his garden without himself in it. Would it be different? Would the tree feel different? Would the pond feel different?

He thinks: yes. Probably.

He thinks: I am part of my garden. The garden is different because I am in it.

He feels a little better.

"I am here," he says to the pond. "Even if the frog is not."

He sits by the pond all afternoon.

In the evening, just as the light is fading, he hears a small sound from the mud. He looks.

The frog is there. Eyes above the mud. Looking at him.

Gabu is so happy.

"You came back," he says.

He goes to his flat stone.

He smiles all the way.

He sleeps.
