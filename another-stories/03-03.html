March 3

The Bee

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu sees the first bee.

He is near the south wall, looking at the flowers, when he hears it. A buzzing sound. He looks around. There.

A bee. Flying from flower to flower. Yellow and black, round and busy.

Gabu watches.

The bee goes to a yellow flower. It goes in, moves around, and comes out. Then it goes to the next flower. And the next.

Gabu has always liked bees. They are very busy. They always know what they are doing.

He walks closer to watch.

The bee does not mind him. It just keeps working.

"Hello," says Gabu.

The bee flies past his nose. He blinks. He stands still.

The bee goes to a flower right next to Gabu. He can see it very closely now. Yellow legs. Fuzzy body. Bright eyes. Wings moving very fast.

The bee leaves the flower and flies away. Up and over the wall.

"Where are you going?" asks Gabu.

He goes to the gate. He looks through the crack. He cannot see the bee. It is gone into the wider world.

He thinks: the bee goes out of my garden and comes back. It goes to many flowers in many places.

He thinks: the bee knows places I have never been.

He walks away from the gate. He goes to the flowers on the south wall. He looks at them.

The flowers are brighter now. The bee has visited them.

He thinks: the bee helped the flowers. And the flowers helped the bee.

He is glad the bee came to his garden.

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He listens for the buzz.

He sleeps.
