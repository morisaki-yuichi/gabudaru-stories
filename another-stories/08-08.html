August 8

Making Something

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Gabu had old clay in a pot at the back of the house.

He brought it out one morning and set it on the table.

"What is this?" Dango said.

"Clay," said Gabu. "For making things."

Dango and Daru both leaned over to look. The clay was reddish-grey. It was soft and cool.

Gabu took a piece and began to work it with his hands. He pressed it and rolled it and shaped it.

After a while, he had made a small bowl.

"It looks like a bowl," Dango said.

"It is a bowl," Gabu said. "When it dries, it will be hard."

Dango and Daru each took a piece of clay.

Daru worked slowly and carefully. He made a small flat square, then curved the edges up. A tray, maybe.

Dango worked fast. Its fingers moved quickly. It pressed and pulled and pinched. It was not sure what it was making. It let its hands decide.

After a while, it held up what it had made.

It was a small round shape with two bumps on the top.

"What is that?" Daru said.

Dango looked at it. "A turtle," it said. "Sort of."

Gabu looked at it. The round body, the two bumps that were legs. "Yes," he said. "I can see it."

They put their three things in the sun to dry.

When they were hard, they put them on the shelf inside — beside the feather and the stone and the smooth green glass.
