July 12

Three in the Rain

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

Rain came in the afternoon.

Not a storm this time. Just steady, warm summer rain. The kind that falls gently and makes the whole garden smell good.

Gabu went under the porch.

Daru went to his shelter. He had made it bigger after the storm in June. It was big enough for two now.

Dango stood in the middle of the garden and looked at the rain falling.

"Come in!" Daru called.

Dango walked to the shelter. It fit, just barely. The three of them were very close together — Gabu on the porch, Daru and Dango in the shelter.

Dango watched the rain. Its eyes were reflecting the grey sky.

"Rain is so interesting," Dango said.

"How so?" Daru asked.

"It falls from so high up," Dango said. "Those drops came from clouds. The clouds came from the sea. So really, the sea is falling on us."

Daru and Daru both thought about this.

"I never thought of it that way," Daru said.

They watched the rain together. The pond made rings where the drops fell. The apple tree dripped. The shelter creaked softly in a small breeze.

Gabu called from the porch: "Tea!"

Daru and Dango went under the porch. Gabu had made warm herb tea in three small cups. They sat together and drank it while the rain fell.

It was warm. It was quiet. It was very good.

"Thank you," Dango said. It held its cup in both hands.

Gabu smiled. "Tea is better with more," he said.
