October 28

A Walk with Suke-san

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu and Suke-san walked outside the garden together.

They walked slowly. Suke-san walked at Gabu's pace, which was slower than Daru and much slower than Dango.

They walked down the path to the clearing.

In October, the clearing was different. The tall grass had fallen and was flat. The seed heads were empty. The trees around it were bare.

They stood in the middle.

Gabu said: "Do you miss walking?"

Suke-san thought. "Sometimes," it said. "But I missed staying more."

"Staying is not easy," Gabu said.

"No," said Suke-san. "Walking is easy. You always know what to do — put one foot in front of the other. Staying is harder. You have to decide to be where you are."

Gabu understood this. He had made this decision once, many years ago — to stay in this garden, to tend it, to make it his.

"But you are sure?" Gabu said.

"Yes," said Suke-san. They had not known each other long. But Gabu trusted Suke-san's few words.

They walked back through the October forest. The leaves were deep on the ground. Their footsteps were quiet.

At the gate, they stopped.

The garden was inside. The path was behind.

Suke-san stepped through the gate.

Gabu followed.

"Home," Gabu said.

Suke-san looked at the word. It was a short word. But it was not an easy word to say without meaning it.

"Home," Suke-san agreed.
