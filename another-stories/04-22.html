April 22

Daru's Favorite Spot

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Every turtle has a favorite spot.

Gabu's favorite spot is his flat stone near the big tree.

Daru is still finding his favorite spot.

Gabu watches as Daru tries different places.

Daru tries the stone at the edge of the pond. He sits there all morning. "The water is too loud," he decides.

He tries the far corner with the soft moss. He sits there all morning. "Too damp. My feet get cold."

He tries near the rose bush on the east wall. "Too thorny."

He tries under the apple tree. "Better."

He sits under the apple tree for several days.

Then he finds it. His spot.

It is a patch of warm ground near the south wall, sheltered from the wind, where two walls meet at an angle. The stones hold the warmth. The angle stops the wind. The flowers are nearby.

Daru sits there one afternoon and does not get up.

Gabu comes to check on him.

"Is everything all right?" asks Gabu.

"Yes," says Daru. He looks very content. "I think I found my spot."

Gabu looks at it. Warm, sheltered, near the flowers.

"It is a good spot," says Gabu.

Daru smiles.

Gabu walks to his flat stone. He sits on it.

His spot.

Daru's spot is there. His spot is here.

Two spots. Two turtles. One garden.

Gabu thinks: the garden now has two centers.

He closes his eyes.

He is very happy.

He sleeps.
