October 1

October Arrives

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

October arrived overnight.

The air in the morning was the coldest yet. Gabu's breath made a small cloud.

Dango came outside and immediately went back in.

"It is very cold," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu from inside, already making tea.

Daru was already outside, standing calmly in the cold morning.

Dango came back out wrapped in an extra dry leaf it had found. It held it around its shoulders.

"That is not a coat," Daru said.

"It is today," said Dango.

Gabu brought the tea outside and they drank it standing in the cold air.

The garden in early October was nearly bare. Leaves on the ground everywhere. The pond was still and grey. The sky was pale and wide.

But the vine on the gate was still brilliant red.

"It is very beautiful," Dango said.

"October is the most colorful," Gabu said.

And it was true. The cool air had brought all the colors out of the leaves — golds and reds and deep oranges, still on the few trees near the wall and on the ground everywhere.

Dango let the leaf-coat fall. It was not that cold. Not really. Just different.

"New month," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"What happens in October?"

"Many things," Gabu said. "You will see."

Dango looked at the gate with its red vine. It believed him.
