June 21

Daru Tells a Story

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

That evening, Daru told Gabu a story.

He said: "Once there was a small turtle who lived by a river. The river was fast and cold. The turtle could not swim in it. So she watched from the shore.

Every day, leaves fell into the river and sailed away. The turtle wanted to know where they went. But she could not follow.

One day, a leaf landed on her shell. She stood very still. And slowly, slowly, the river carried her too. She went around the bend. She saw new trees. New banks. New sky.

At the end, the river reached the sea. The leaf and the turtle floated on the warm sea for one whole afternoon. Then the turtle swam back. She knew the way now.

She lived by the river for the rest of her life. But she always knew that the sea was there, just around the bend."

Daru was quiet when he finished.

Gabu did not say anything for a moment.

"Did someone tell you that story?" Gabu asked.

"No," Daru said. "I just made it up. I don't know where it came from."

Gabu looked at the pond. The moon was on it. "It is a good story," he said. "The turtle was brave."

"I think she was just curious," Daru said.

"That is often how bravery starts," said Gabu.
