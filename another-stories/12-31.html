December 31

The Last Day of the Year

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The last day of the year.

Gabu made something special for dinner — a proper feast. Not elaborate. But the best of what they had: the last of the stored root vegetables, roasted. Good bread. Herb broth. The last jam, which they spread on everything.

They ate slowly.

"Last meal of the year," Dango said.

"First meal of next year will be tomorrow," said Daru.

"With Gabu cooking," Dango said. It looked at Gabu. "Porridge."

Gabu smiled. "Yes. Porridge."

After dinner, they sat by the fire.

Gabu got down the fourteenth journal. He opened it to the last page.

He wrote: "December 31. The last day. Four friends. One house. One fire."

He looked at what he had written.

Then he added: "I did not plan this year. I only kept the gate open."

He closed the journal for the last time. He set it on the shelf.

The new journal was waiting. Empty. Ready.

Dango put its head on its arms and looked at the fire.

"Next year," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Next year I will have been here for a whole year," Dango said. "From the very beginning."

"Yes."

"And the year after that too," Dango said. "If I stay."

Gabu looked at Dango. "If?"

Dango looked back. "When," it said. "When I stay."

Gabu nodded.

The fire burned low. The year was ending. Outside, December dark, December cold, December stars.

Inside: four friends, one fire, one home.

The gate was closed for the night.

But it would open again in the morning.

It always did.
