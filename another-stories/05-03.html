May 3

The Frog Returns

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today there are five frogs at the pond.

Gabu and Daru count them together.

"One, two, three, four, five," says Daru.

"More than last week," says Gabu.

The frogs are calling back and forth. It is quite loud now. A chorus.

Gabu's old frog is there. The one he has known since February. He can tell it from the others.

"That one is Frog," he tells Daru.

Daru looks. "How can you tell?"

"He sits on the same stone every time," says Gabu. "And his voice is a little lower than the others."

Daru listens carefully.

"I hear it," says Daru. "Lower. Like this." He makes a low sound in his throat.

The frogs look at him.

Gabu laughs. A small turtle laugh.

Daru makes the sound again. One of the frogs calls back.

"It answered!" says Daru.

Gabu says: "What did you say?"

"I don't know," says Daru. "But it answered."

They sit by the pond and listen to the frogs. The chorus is warm and alive. Full of spring feeling.

Gabu thinks: in winter this pond was silent. Now it is full of voices.

He thinks about what he said to himself in January. We are all here. We just don't always see each other.

He says this to Daru.

Daru says: "Like us. We were both here. We just had not met yet."

Gabu is quiet.

He says: "Yes. Like us."

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
