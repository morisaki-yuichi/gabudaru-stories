July 28

Dango's First Month

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

It was July 28.

Dango counted on its fingers. "I have been here twenty days," it said.

Gabu nodded. "Almost three weeks."

Dango thought about twenty days. It seemed both very short and very long.

In twenty days: it had learned to float. It had helped in the garden. It had eaten Gabu's bread. It had night-swum with fireflies. It had seen the stars in the pond. It had made a hat from grass. It had heard about the ant road and the spider's web and the three fish.

It had sat under the apple tree in the rain. It had eaten bread by the pond. It had held Gabu's old journal.

"I have done many things," Dango said.

"Yes," said Daru. "You fit in quickly."

Dango smiled. Fitting in. That was what it had always wanted. And it had happened so naturally here. Not because everyone was the same. Gabu was slow and quiet and careful. Daru was patient and steady. Dango was fast and loud and curious. But they fit together.

"Do you think you will stay?" Daru asked.

Dango did not hesitate. "Yes," it said.

Gabu looked at Dango. "Good," he said. Just that.

Dango sat back and looked at the garden. The pond. The apple tree. The shelter. The house.

Twenty days had made this place a home.

It could not imagine leaving now.
