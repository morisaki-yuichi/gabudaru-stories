November 30

The Last Day of November

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The last day of November.

Cold and grey and still.

They spent it simply — breakfast, tending the fire, a short walk, lunch, reading, the fire again, dinner.

In the evening, Dango said: "November is over."

"Yes," said Gabu.

"I survived November," Dango said.

"You did more than survive," Daru said.

Dango thought about November. The grey sky. The porridge. The jam. The ice on the pond. The clear bright day. The long evenings of stories and star-learning and fire.

"It was good," Dango said. "I did not think it would be. But it was."

"That is November's gift," Gabu said. "It is hard to like on the outside. But inside — if you have warmth and people — it is one of the best months."

Dango thought: inside is the important word.

"December next," Dango said.

"December," said Gabu.

"The shortest day," Daru said.

"And then the days start getting longer," Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san.

Dango looked at the fire. "So December is the turning point."

"Yes," said Gabu. "The year turns toward the light in December."

Outside, the last November wind came through the bare garden.

Inside, four friends sat by the fire, warm and whole, turning toward the light.

November was over.

December was waiting, with its cold and its dark and its promise of the sun coming back.
