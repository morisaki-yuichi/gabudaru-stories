November 27

Thinking Ahead to Spring

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu had been thinking about spring.

Not because he was impatient with winter. But because spring was when the year began again, and there were things to plan.

He talked to them at dinner.

"In spring," he said, "we will dig up the buried letters. And then we will plant again."

"Suke-san's mystery seeds will come up," Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san.

"And the swallows will come back," Daru said.

"And Mido will wake up," Dango said.

"And the fireflies will come in late spring," Daru said.

"Not until June," Gabu said.

Dango thought about June. It seemed very far away. But it was real — it would come.

"Next summer," Dango said, "we will all be here. All four of us. For the whole summer."

"Yes," said Gabu.

Dango thought about next summer. Night swimming with four. Picking apples with four. Four cups of cold tea on a hot day.

"I want to float with all four of us in the pond," it said.

"The pond may not be large enough," Daru said.

"We will make it work," Dango said.

Gabu smiled. He was glad Dango was thinking about next summer. It meant Dango knew it would be here.

"We will plan in spring," Gabu said. "When the ground thaws."

Outside, November wind moved through the bare trees.

Inside, four friends planned the summer over dinner.
