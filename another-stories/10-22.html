October 22

Dango Shows Suke-san Around

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Dango had appointed itself guide.

"I will show you everything," it told Suke-san. "Every corner."

Suke-san agreed calmly.

Dango led Suke-san on a full tour. The pond: "The fish are deep now. You cannot see them. They will come back in spring." The shelter: "I built this. In June. It leaned but we fixed it." The compost: "Everything goes in. Everything comes back." The apple tree: "The last apple was the best one. We ate it on September 21st."

Suke-san walked beside Dango, listening.

They came to the corner near the rose bush. Dango stopped. "Under here," it said, "we buried letters."

Suke-san looked at the soil. The small stone marker was still there.

"Letters to spring," Dango said. "We wrote things we hoped for and buried them."

Suke-san was quiet for a moment.

"May I write one?" it said.

"We will dig them up in spring and read them," Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san. "I would like to write one."

Dango led Suke-san to Gabu's journal. Gabu found a piece of paper. Suke-san sat with the pencil for a long time.

Then it wrote. Not much — Suke-san's words were always few. But when it folded the paper and Gabu added it to the bundle under the rose bush, Suke-san put one hand on the soil.

"Hello, spring," it said, very quietly.

Dango watched. It felt the shape of the year becoming something whole.
