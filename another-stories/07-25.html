July 25

Three Shells

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

Daru had a thought one afternoon.

He said: "All three of us have shells."

Gabu looked up from his book.

"But our shells are all different," Daru said.

They looked at each other's shells.

Gabu's shell was large and dark, with deep ridges worn smooth over many years.

Daru's shell was medium — darker than when he had arrived, with a rounder shape.

Dango's shell was small and spotted, pale brown with yellow-gold edges. It was very round, like a dome.

Dango touched its own shell. "Mine has spots," it said.

"Yes," said Daru. "I have lines."

"And mine has history," Gabu said. He ran a hand along one of the old ridges. "This scratch — a rock in a stream, long ago. This worn part — years of sitting on the flat stone."

Dango came closer and looked at Gabu's shell. It was like a map. Every part of it told a story.

"Will mine have stories someday?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Every scratch and worn spot means something. You will not always remember them. But your shell will."

Dango looked at its small spotted shell. It was smooth and new. But it was its. And someday it would have scratches and worn places too.

"I want good stories on mine," Dango said.

Gabu smiled. "You are already making them," he said.
