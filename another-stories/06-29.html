June 29

The Walk Outside

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

In the morning, they went outside the garden gate.

Gabu opened the gate. The path beyond was narrow and green. Trees grew on both sides. The ground was soft with old leaves.

Daru had not been outside since he first arrived. He stopped at the gate and looked at the path.

"It is bigger out here," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "But you know the way back."

They walked along the path slowly. Gabu knew it well. He had walked it many times. He pointed things out as they went: a hollow log where beetles lived, a mossy stone, a small muddy pool where frogs laid eggs in spring.

Daru looked at everything. His eyes were wide.

"Where does the path go?" he said.

"It goes many places," Gabu said. "To the forest. To the stream. To the meadow."

Daru looked down the path. It went around a bend and out of sight.

They listened for the sound from yesterday. But today the path was quiet. Only birds. Only wind.

"It is not here today," Daru said.

"No," said Gabu. "But it was real. We both heard it."

They walked a little farther. Around the bend, the path opened up into a small clearing. Sunlight came through the trees and fell in warm patches on the ground.

Gabu and Daru stood in the clearing for a while. The world felt big and open.

"There is a lot out here," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "And the garden is still home."

They turned and walked back together.
