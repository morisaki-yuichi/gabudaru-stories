January 28

The Old Stone

🐢 Gabu
0:00

Near the far corner of the garden, under the oldest part of the wall, there is a very large stone. Gabu knows this stone. He has known it all his life.

It is bigger than his flat stone. It is rough and covered with lichen. Orange lichen and grey lichen. Some of the lichen is very old. Older than Gabu.

He goes to this stone today.

He puts his foot on it. The stone is rough under his foot. He steps up on it. From the top of this stone, he can see the whole garden. It is just high enough.

He turns slowly. He sees the big tree. The pond. The gate. The south wall where the yellow flower grows. His flat stone.

He can see everything from here.

"Hello, garden," he says.

He stays on the stone for a long time. The wind is cold but he does not mind. From up here, the garden looks beautiful.

He thinks about the stone. How long has it been here? Before the wall. Before the tree, maybe. This stone was here when the garden was new.

He touches the lichen. It is soft and rough at the same time.

He thinks: things that stay in one place for a long time grow beautiful. The lichen takes years to grow. The tree takes years to grow. The wall takes years to become part of the garden.

He thinks: I have stayed in one place for a long time too.

He looks at his reflection in a tiny puddle on top of the stone.

"Hello," he says.

He is old for his size. And he is part of this garden.

He climbs down slowly.

He goes to his flat stone and sleeps.
