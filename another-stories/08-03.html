August 3

Dango Counts the Stars

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

One night, Dango decided to count the stars.

It lay on the flat stone by the pond. It chose a patch of sky and started counting.

One. Two. Three. Seven. Twelve...

But then more appeared as its eyes adjusted. Fainter ones, between the bright ones.

It counted forty, then lost track. It started again.

Gabu came out and saw Dango lying flat on the stone, eyes wide, lips moving.

"What are you doing?" Gabu said.

"Counting stars," Dango said without moving.

Gabu sat beside it. He looked up. "How many?"

"I cannot count them," Dango said. "Every time I think I have them all, more appear."

"There are too many to count," Gabu said.

Dango lowered its eyes slowly. "How many are there?"

Gabu thought. "More than every grain of sand on a beach," he said. "More than everything in the garden times every garden in the world."

Dango stared at him. "That is not possible."

"It is," said Gabu. "The sky is very, very large."

Dango looked back up. The stars seemed brighter now that it knew how many there were.

Daru came out and lay down too.

Three turtles on the stone, looking up.

"We are very small," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"But we are here," said Daru.

"That seems important," Dango said.

Gabu smiled in the dark. "It is the most important thing," he said.
