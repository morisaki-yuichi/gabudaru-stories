January 10

A Robin

🐢 Gabu

It is a clear January morning. Cold and bright. The sun makes the frost on the grass sparkle.

Gabu sees a bird on the ground near the pond. It is a small bird with an orange-red chest. It hops on the frozen ground. It looks for food.

Gabu walks toward it slowly. He does not want to scare it.

The bird looks up. It sees Gabu. It does not fly away. It hops to one side and looks at him.

"Hello," says Gabu.

The bird tilts its head. Then it hops a little closer. It looks at Gabu with one bright eye.

"I live here," says Gabu. "In this garden. Do you live here too?"

The bird opens its beak. It sings three short notes. Then it closes its beak.

Gabu is not sure what that means. But he thinks it sounds friendly.

The bird hops near the pond edge. It finds something small in the frozen mud. It eats it quickly.

Gabu watches. He thinks this bird is very good at finding food. Better than him.

The bird hops around the pond. It goes all the way around and comes back near Gabu.

"You are very fast," says Gabu.

The bird looks at him again. Then it flies up. It goes to the big tree. It sits on a branch and sings a longer song. The song is bright and clear in the cold air.

Gabu listens to the whole song.

When the bird is done, Gabu says: "That was beautiful."

He walks back to his flat stone. He feels warm inside, even though it is cold.

He closes his eyes. He still hears the bird's song in his head.
