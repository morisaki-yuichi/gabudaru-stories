December 16

Gabu Thinks About the Year

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu went to his window seat alone.

He sat and thought about the year.

January: the garden alone, as it had been for many years. The pond, the tree, the winter quiet.

April: Daru appeared. The gate opened. Everything changed.

July: Dango arrived, following voices. The garden became loud and full of questions.

October: Suke-san, finally, after all the singing and listening and open gates.

And now December.

He thought: I did not plan any of this. I only opened the gate and kept it open.

He thought about what Suke-san had said — that some places you pass through, and some you stay. He had stayed here for a very long time.

And now he was glad he had.

Daru brought him tea. He set it beside Gabu and went away. He could tell Gabu was thinking.

Gabu picked up the tea and looked at the winter garden.

He thought: this garden has held many seasons. But this was the best year.

He picked up the journal and opened to the back pages. He wrote:

"Year's end approaches. I am thinking about what came. Not what I made — what arrived. Three friends. A gate kept open."

He looked at the words.

Then he added: "I would not change a single day."

He closed the journal.

Outside, the afternoon gold was starting on the bare branches. December light, low and true.
