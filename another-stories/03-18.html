March 18

A Long Walk

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu walks all the way around his garden without stopping.

He starts at his flat stone. He walks to the south wall. He walks along the south wall to the southeast corner. He walks along the east wall to the northeast corner. He walks along the north wall to the northwest corner. He walks along the west wall to the southwest corner. Then back along the south wall to his flat stone.

The whole way around.

It takes a long time. Gabu is not fast.

But he does it.

He stops at each interesting place. The flowers on the south wall. The climbing plant on the east wall. The far corner with its soft moss. The gate. The old stone.

He says hello to each place.

When he gets back to his flat stone, he is tired.

He sits down.

He thinks: my garden is not so small. It took a long time to walk all the way around.

He thinks about each part of the wall he walked along. Each part is different. Different stones. Different plants in the cracks. Different things growing at the base.

He knows every part.

He is glad he walked the whole way today. He has not done this in a while.

He looks at the garden around him.

He thinks: I know this place very well. All of it. Every corner.

He is proud of that.

He looks at the sky. Bright blue. Spring sky.

He closes his eyes.

He is a little sore in his legs. But a good sore.

He sleeps.
