January 3

The Big Tree

🐢 Gabu

It is January. The air is cold and sharp. Gabu can see his breath like a small cloud.

He goes to the big tree. He does this every morning. The tree is very important to Gabu. It has always been there.

Gabu walks slowly around the tree. The trunk is wide. He cannot reach all the way around it. He puts his feet on the roots. The roots come up out of the ground in big curves. Gabu walks along the top of one root like a path.

A small hole is at the base of the tree. Something lives in there. Gabu does not know what. He sniffs at the hole. He smells something warm and earthy.

"Hello?" he says quietly.

Nothing comes out. But that is all right.

He looks up into the branches. In summer, this tree is full of leaves. Green and bright. Now it is bare. But Gabu can see the sky very well through the branches. He likes that too.

A tiny spider has made a web between two low branches. It is very careful work. Gabu looks at it closely. He does not touch it.

"Good spider," he says.

In the afternoon, a wind comes. The branches of the big tree move. They make a soft creaking sound. Gabu listens. He has heard this sound many times. It is a comfortable sound.

He sits at the base of the tree. He leans his shell against it. The tree feels solid and strong behind him.

Gabu is happy here. He closes his eyes.

The tree creaks softly above him. The garden is peaceful. Gabu rests.
