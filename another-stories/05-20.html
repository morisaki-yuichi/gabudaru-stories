May 20

Where Is Daru?

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

In the morning, Gabu wakes up and Daru is not there.

He is not at his spot near the south wall. He is not at the pond. He is not at the big tree.

Gabu walks around the garden.

"Daru?" he calls softly.

No answer.

He goes to the far corner. No Daru.

He goes to the apple tree. No Daru.

He goes to the gate. The gate is closed.

He looks at the pond. He looks under the bushes. He looks in the leaf pile.

No Daru.

He stands in the middle of the garden.

He thinks: where could he be?

He is not afraid. Daru is a turtle. Turtles can take care of themselves.

But he wants to find him.

He calls again: "Daru?"

Nothing.

He goes to the old stone in the corner. He climbs up.

He looks out over the garden.

There.

Under the apple tree, in a shadow he missed. Daru, sleeping. So still he looked like a stone.

Gabu climbs down.

He walks to the apple tree.

He sits next to Daru.

He waits.

After a while, Daru wakes up.

"Oh," says Daru. "Good morning."

"I was looking for you," says Gabu.

Daru says: "I moved last night. It was cooler under the tree."

"I see," says Gabu.

He does not say: I was worried. He was, a little. But Daru is fine.

He says: "Let me know if you move. So I can find you."

Daru says: "Yes. Sorry."

They eat breakfast together.

Gabu closes his eyes.

He smiles.

He sleeps.
