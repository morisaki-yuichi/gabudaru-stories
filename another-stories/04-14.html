April 14

Daru Likes the Pond

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru loves the pond.

The first morning, he went to look at it. The second morning, he went in. Now it is the third day and Daru spends hours at the pond.

He swims. He goes to the shallow end and stands with the warm water up to his neck. He goes to the deeper part and paddles with all four feet.

He catches a water bug on purpose and then lets it go.

He watches the frogs. He talks to them. The frogs look at him without answering, but he does not mind.

Gabu sits at the edge and watches.

"Is it good?" asks Gabu.

"It is wonderful!" says Daru.

He does a circle in the water. He goes under and comes back up. His shell gleams when he comes out.

Gabu thinks: I like swimming too, but not as much as Daru.

Daru comes to the edge. He sits next to Gabu, dripping.

"Why are you not swimming?" asks Daru.

"I am watching you," says Gabu.

Daru looks at him. "Watching is not as good as swimming."

Gabu thinks about this. He gets into the water. He wades in slowly. The water comes up to his belly. Cool and fresh.

He paddles out to the middle.

He looks at Daru from the middle of the pond. Daru is at the edge, watching him.

"How is it?" calls Daru.

"Good," calls Gabu.

They swim together.

Gabu thinks: this is much better than swimming alone.

He closes his eyes in the water.

He floats for a moment.

He is happy.

He sleeps that evening with damp feet and a warm heart.
