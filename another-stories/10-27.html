October 27

The Last Red Leaf

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

The vine on the gate had lost most of its leaves.

But one leaf remained. The last one, at the very top of the vine.

It was still red — deep red, like something that did not want to go.

Dango noticed it one morning. "The last vine leaf," it said.

They all came to look.

"It has been holding on for weeks," Daru said.

"All the others are gone," Dango said. "Why is this one still here?"

"Maybe it is stubborn," Suke-san said.

Dango looked at the leaf. "Or maybe it just likes it here," it said.

They stood by the gate and looked at the last red leaf.

The wind came. The leaf shook. But it held.

"I am glad it stayed," Dango said.

They went inside and had breakfast. In the late morning, the wind came again, stronger.

Dango went to the window and looked at the vine.

The last red leaf was gone.

Dango pressed its face to the glass and looked at the bare vine. The gate had no more color on it. Just the dark twisted branches.

"It went," Dango said.

Suke-san came to the window and looked.

"It was here until today," Suke-san said.

Dango nodded. "That is enough," it said.

But it stayed at the window a little longer, looking at the bare gate, thinking about the summer and the autumn and the red that was now gone.

Everything has its last day. And then it is only remembered.
