March 9

The Big Rain

🐢 Gabu

It rains today. Not soft February rain. A proper March rain. Big drops. Loud on the leaves. Loud on the pond.

Gabu stands in it. His shell is loud with the rain.

He walks through the puddles. The garden is very wet. Worms are everywhere.

He goes to the pond. The rain is making the water jump. A thousand little splashes, all the time.

He watches.

He thinks: the pond is getting bigger. The rain is filling it.

He looks up at the sky. Grey clouds everywhere. Water falling from them.

He thinks about where rain comes from. The sea? The river? The clouds? He does not really know. But it comes, and it waters his garden.

He says: "Thank you, rain."

He is wet. His feet are soaked. Even inside his shell it is a bit damp.

He goes to the big tree. Under the tree it is drier. The leaves catch some of the rain.

He stands under the tree and watches the rain in the garden.

The flowers on the south wall are bending under the drops. But they are not broken. They bend and then come back up.

He thinks about bending and coming back. Like the flowers.

The frog comes out in the rain. Of course. Frogs love rain. It hops across the wet ground. Happy.

Gabu watches the happy frog.

"You like this," says Gabu.

"Rrr," says the frog.

In the afternoon, the rain gets softer. Then it stops.

The garden is dripping and clean. Everything is brighter.

Gabu walks out into the wet garden.

He is happy.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
