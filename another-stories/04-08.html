April 8

Everything Feels Different

🐢 Gabu

This morning everything feels different.

Gabu walks out and the air is different. The light is different. The smell is different.

Or maybe he is different. Maybe he is paying so much attention that everything feels more vivid.

He walks to the gate. He puts his ear against it.

He hears: footsteps on the path outside. Coming closer.

He pulls back.

He waits.

The footsteps stop at the gate.

He hears a sound. A gentle sound from outside. A small sound, like a greeting.

His heart is beating fast. For a turtle, fast.

He puts his nose to the crack.

He smells the living smell, very close now. Very clear.

He can see through the crack. The path. The outside grass. And a small shape. A small shape on the path, right in front of the gate.

He looks.

He cannot see clearly through the crack.

He puts his foot on the gate.

He stops.

He thinks: tomorrow.

He does not know why he thinks that. Tomorrow.

The footsteps move away. Slowly. Hesitating. Then moving again.

He listens until he cannot hear them.

He walks away from the gate. He walks to his flat stone.

He sits.

He breathes.

He thinks: tomorrow.

He closes his eyes. He cannot sit still. He gets up again.

He walks to the pond.

He looks at the water.

He is very awake.

He waits for tomorrow.

He sleeps, finally, when the stars come out. A deep and ready sleep.
