June 4

Daru Learns to Float

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Gabu was floating in the pond.

He lay on his back with his legs out. His shell kept him up. The sky above was very blue. He was perfectly still.

Daru watched from the edge.

"How do you do that?" Daru said.

"I just let go," Gabu said. "Stop trying to swim. Just... be still. The water holds you."

Daru stepped into the pond slowly. He walked out until the water was up to his chin. He stopped there.

"Now what?" he said.

"Lean back slowly," Gabu said. "Very slowly. Do not be afraid."

Daru leaned back. He put his legs up. For one moment he was floating.

Then he got scared and grabbed at the water. He went under. He came up sputtering.

"I went down," Daru said.

"You got scared," Gabu said. "That is why. When you are scared, you tighten up. Try again."

Daru tried again. This time he leaned back more slowly. He told himself: the water will hold me. He felt his shell on the surface. He let his legs rise.

He was floating.

"Oh," he said. His voice was very quiet. "Oh, I am floating."

"Yes," said Gabu. He was floating beside him.

The two turtles floated side by side in the pond. The sky above was blue and wide. A white cloud moved slowly across it.

"This is how birds must feel," Daru said.

Gabu smiled. "Maybe," he said.

They floated for a long, quiet time.
