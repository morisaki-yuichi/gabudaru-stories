June 17

The Garden at Noon

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

At noon in June, the garden was very quiet.

The sun was right overhead. The shadows were small and dark under the plants. The air was hot and still. The bees had stopped buzzing. The butterflies were resting.

Gabu and Daru sat under the apple tree.

"Tell me something," Daru said.

"What kind of something?" said Gabu.

"Something about before," Daru said. "About when you were young. About the garden then."

Gabu thought. He was quiet for a while.

"When I was very young," he said, "this garden was much smaller. The apple tree was only as tall as I am now. The pond was just a hole with muddy water."

"Really?" Daru said.

"Yes. I dug it bigger myself, over many years. I planted the apple tree. I brought the rose bush from far away, in a little pot, and put it by the wall."

Daru looked at the big rose bush. It was taller than both of them.

"You made all this?" he said.

"I grew it," Gabu said. "Like the garden grows things. Slowly."

Daru was quiet for a moment. "Did you do it alone?"

"For a long time, yes," Gabu said. "That is why it is even nicer now. With you here to see it."

The noon light fell warm and gold through the apple leaves above them.

Daru leaned against Gabu's shell gently. Gabu did not move. They sat like that together, in the quiet noon, under the tree that Gabu had planted long ago.
