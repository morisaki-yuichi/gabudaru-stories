March 29

Gabu Wonders

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu wonders about many things.

He walks around the garden, wondering.

He wonders: how old is the big tree? He does not know. He has lived here for a long time. The tree was already big when he came.

He wonders: where does the pond water come from? It does not dry up, even in summer. Water comes from somewhere.

He wonders: how do birds know when to come back in spring? What tells them?

He wonders: what is on the other side of the wall? He has some ideas from the crack in the gate. But the whole picture? He does not know.

He wonders: will anything new come to the garden this year?

He wonders: will he ever have a friend?

He sits by the pond and wonders.

The frog comes up and looks at him.

"Rrr," says the frog.

Gabu says: "I am wondering things."

The frog blinks.

Gabu says: "Do you wonder things? Or do you just know things?"

The frog blinks again. It hops into the water.

Gabu thinks: the frog just knows things. It does not wonder about them.

He thinks: maybe that is easier. To just know.

But he thinks: wondering is also nice. When you wonder about something, you pay attention to it. You notice more.

He likes noticing things.

He walks to his flat stone.

He is still wondering.

He closes his eyes.

He wonders if he will dream.

He sleeps.
