April 2

A New Smell

🐢 Gabu

Today there is a new smell in the garden.

Gabu walks out in the morning and notices it right away. Something he has not smelled before. Or not recently. Something from outside the wall.

He sniffs the air.

It is a warm, living smell. Something animal. Not threatening. Just present.

He walks around the garden, sniffing.

The smell is strongest near the gate.

He goes to the gate. He puts his nose to the crack.

Yes. The smell comes from outside. Something is on the path outside the gate. Or was recently.

He waits.

He listens.

He hears footsteps outside. Slow and careful. Moving past the gate.

He presses his ear to the wood.

He can almost hear breathing. Very soft. On the other side of the gate.

He thinks: someone is there.

He thinks: I should open the gate.

He waits.

The footsteps stop right outside the gate.

There is a moment of quiet.

Then the footsteps move away. Slow, careful, quiet.

He listens until he cannot hear them.

He stands at the gate for a long time after.

He thinks: someone was right there. Right on the other side.

He feels a warm feeling in his chest.

He thinks: they will come back.

He does not know why he thinks that. But he does.

He walks to his flat stone.

He looks at the gate from far away.

He closes his eyes.

He hopes.

He sleeps.
