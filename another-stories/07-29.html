July 29

Something Dango Cannot Do

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango could not sit still.

This was not a small problem. It was a real one.

Gabu sat still for long stretches. Daru had learned it. Even Mido the frog sat on its stone for hours.

But Dango shifted and fidgeted. It moved after thirty seconds. It got up and walked in a circle. It came back. It shifted again.

One morning, Daru sat beside Dango and said: "I want to show you something."

He brought Dango to the edge of the garden, near the big stones.

"Sit here," he said. "And try to be still for as long as you can."

Dango sat. And shifted. And sat.

"Keep trying," Daru said. He sat beside Dango.

Dango focused. It watched the stone in front of it. It breathed slowly.

One minute. Two minutes.

A beetle walked across the stone. If Dango had been moving, it would have missed it.

Dango watched the beetle. It was small and green, with tiny moving mouth-parts. It carried a piece of seed.

"Oh," Dango breathed.

"See?" Daru said softly.

Dango kept watching. The beetle went around a small pebble and disappeared under a leaf.

When it was gone, Dango let out a long breath.

"I was still for that long," it said.

"You were," said Daru.

"Because of the beetle," Dango said.

"Yes. Stillness has things in it. That is why it is worth it."
