July 5

Daru's Shell Grows

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Gabu noticed something one morning.

He looked at Daru carefully. "You are bigger," he said.

Daru looked down at himself. "Am I?"

Gabu stood beside him and compared. Yes — Daru's shell was larger than in April. His legs were longer. He stood higher.

"You grew," Gabu said. He sounded happy.

Daru felt strange about this. He had not noticed. How did it happen without him seeing it?

"When did I grow?" he said.

"Every day, a little," Gabu said. "That is how growing works. You cannot see it happening. Then one morning you notice."

Daru looked at his reflection in the pond. He tried to compare it to the Daru he remembered in April. He had been smaller, he thought. Softer-looking.

"My shell used to be lighter," he said. "Not as dark."

"Yes," said Gabu. "It will keep getting darker and stronger as you grow."

Daru touched his own shell. It was smooth and firm. He had had it his whole life. He had never thought much about it.

"What does it feel like from the inside?" he said.

"Home," said Gabu. "It always feels like home."

Daru thought about this. He pulled his head in for a moment — he did this sometimes when he was thinking. Inside the shell, it was warm and dark and his.

He came back out.

"Yes," he said. "Home."
