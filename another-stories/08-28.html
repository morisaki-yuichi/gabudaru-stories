August 28

Preparing for Autumn

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Gabu began some small preparations.

He checked the house for gaps where cold air might come in. He put extra dry leaves in each sleeping spot — the leaves would keep warmth in. He made sure the clay pot repairs were all solid.

Daru and Dango watched him work.

"Is it getting cold already?" Dango said.

"Not yet," Gabu said. "But September comes soon. I like to be ready early."

Dango went to its sleeping spot and looked at it. "Mine could be warmer," it said. "I will find more leaves."

Daru helped Dango collect dry leaves from under the big tree. They carried armfuls back.

Meanwhile, Gabu was at the compost pile. He turned it over with a stick. Underneath, the old leaves and food scraps had become dark, rich soil.

"This is ready to put in the garden beds," he said.

"Now?" said Daru.

"In autumn," said Gabu. "We will dig it into the soil before winter. To feed next year's plants."

Dango stood by the compost pile and looked in. "It has been working all summer," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Quietly."

Dango thought about all the things in the garden that worked quietly without anyone noticing. The compost. The roots of the plants. The worms turning the soil. The rain building up in the pond.

"Many things work without being seen," Dango said.

"Many of the most important things," Gabu said.
