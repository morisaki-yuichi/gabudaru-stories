June 6

The Long Evening

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

In June, the days were long. The sun did not set until very late.

Gabu remembered this from other years. But Daru had never seen it before.

One evening, Daru walked to Gabu. He looked worried.

"Is the sun broken?" Daru said.

Gabu looked up. The sun was still in the sky, but it was already past dinnertime.

"No," Gabu said. He smiled a little. "In summer, the days are longer. The sun stays up later."

Daru stared at the sun. "Why?"

Gabu thought of how to explain. "The world tilts toward the sun in summer," he said. "So we get more light. More warmth. More time."

Daru was quiet for a moment. "More time," he repeated.

"Yes. Long evenings like this one. Look — we can still eat dinner and walk around the garden and still see the sky turn pink."

They walked around the garden together. The light was golden and warm. Everything had long shadows. The grass was soft under their feet.

Daru kept looking at the sky. He watched it go from gold to orange to a deep pink. Then, slowly, it went purple.

"Now it is setting," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

They watched the sun go down behind the trees. It took a long time. The colors changed slowly.

When it was finally dark, the first star appeared.

"That was the longest sunset I ever saw," Daru said.

"There will be more," said Gabu. "All through June."

Daru smiled. He liked long evenings.
