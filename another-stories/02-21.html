February 21

One More Flower

🐢 Gabu

Gabu goes to the south wall this morning. He counts the flowers.

Nine.

Yesterday there were eight. A new one opened overnight.

He looks at all nine flowers. They are a mix now. Yellow ones, white ones, a few small purple ones. They are not very tall. They are low and close to the ground. But they are bright.

He thinks: every day, one more.

He thinks about that. Every day, one more flower. And every day, a little more sun. And every day, the buds on the tree are a little bigger.

One more. Every day.

He thinks: big things happen in small steps.

He looks at the flowers again. In autumn, there are roses on the east wall. Big, heavy roses. Pink and white. They are very beautiful. But they started as buds smaller than his toe.

One more. Every day.

He walks to the big tree. He looks at the buds. He picks the same bud he looked at yesterday. He looks at it very carefully.

Yes. A little more open. He can see a tiny bit of green inside.

One more. Every day.

He walks to the pond. The water plant at the bottom has grown. He can see it from the bank.

One more. Every day.

He sits on his flat stone.

He thinks about himself. Is he growing too? One more. Every day.

He looks at his feet. He looks at his shell. He looks at his reflection in a puddle.

He thinks: maybe.

He closes his eyes.

He is happy to be growing.

He sleeps.
