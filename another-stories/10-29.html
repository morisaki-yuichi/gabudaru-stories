October 29

Dango's Big Question

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Dango had been thinking about something.

It waited until evening, when they were all together by the fire.

Then it said: "Why do we all live together?"

Everyone looked at Dango.

"I mean," Dango said, "we did not know each other before. We are not family. We are very different. So why does it work?"

Gabu was quiet for a moment.

"Why do you ask?" he said.

"Because I want to understand it," Dango said. "So I can hold onto it."

Daru thought. "It works because Gabu opened the gate," he said. "If the gate had been closed, none of us would be here."

"The gate was open," Suke-san said. "But you had to walk through it."

"We all had to choose it," Dango said. "The gate and the choice."

They thought about this.

"And then?" Dango said.

Gabu looked at the fire. "And then you are in," he said. "And when you are in, you are responsible. For the garden. For each other. You tend both."

Dango looked around at the others. Gabu, tending everything. Daru, patient and steady. Suke-san, adding strength and stillness.

"What am I?" Dango said. "What do I tend?"

Gabu smiled. "You remind us to be surprised," he said. "You see things we have stopped seeing."

Dango was quiet. It was not what it expected.

"Oh," it said.

"Do not stop being surprised," Gabu said. "We need it."

Dango nodded slowly. That was a thing it could do.
