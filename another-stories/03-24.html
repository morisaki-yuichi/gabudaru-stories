March 24

Gabu Sleeps in the Sun

🐢 Gabu

Today is a warm day. Warm like summer, almost. The sun is strong and the air is mild.

Gabu walks to a sunny patch in the middle of the garden. He lies down in the sun. He stretches all four legs out. He puts his head down on the warm ground.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps in the sun.

He sleeps for a long time. Longer than usual. An hour. Maybe more.

The sun moves slowly across the sky. The warm patch moves. His tail gets cold. He moves a little to follow the sun.

He sleeps more.

Birds come near him. They see he is sleeping. They hop around him.

A bee lands on his shell and then leaves.

He sleeps.

When he wakes up, the sun is much lower. The afternoon is getting on.

He lifts his head. He blinks.

He stretches. He feels wonderful. Warm through and through. Deep and slow and relaxed.

He has not slept like that in many months.

He walks to his flat stone. He sits on it.

He looks at the spot where he was sleeping. The grass is a little pressed down where he lay.

He thinks: I slept in the sun.

He thinks: this is what summer will be like. Warm and slow and good.

He looks at the blue sky.

"Thank you, sun," he says.

He closes his eyes again.

He is deeply, completely warm.

He sleeps once more, happy.

He sleeps.
