August 20

The First Apple

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

It happened on the twentieth of August.

Gabu was in the garden in the morning. He walked to the apple tree as he often did. He looked at the marked apple.

It was red.

Not a little red, not a blush — fully red, the color of a held breath, the color of a sunset.

"Daru! Dango!" he called.

They came running. They saw the apple.

Dango put its hands on its cheeks.

"It is ready?" it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Look."

He reached up and touched the apple. It gave a little under his fingers.

"Touch it," he said to Dango.

Dango reached up and pressed the apple very gently. It gave — soft, just a little, beneath the firm skin.

"Oh," Dango said. "It is soft inside."

"Yes," said Gabu. "It is ready. It will let go."

He put his hand around the apple. He lifted up gently. The apple came off the branch without any pulling.

He held it in both hands.

It was red and round and smelled like something warm and sharp and sweet.

Gabu brought it inside. He cut it into three pieces on the table.

They each took a piece.

Dango bit in.

Its eyes closed. When it opened them, it looked like it had heard a piece of music for the first time.

"This," Dango said, "is what waiting tastes like."

Gabu and Daru ate their pieces. The apple was perfect.

The best apple of the year, as always.
