May 17

Playing Together

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Gabu and Daru play a game.

Gabu is not sure it is a game. He has not played many games in his life. But it feels like one.

They are near the big tree. Daru picks up a leaf with his foot. He carries it a few steps. He puts it down.

Gabu picks it up. He carries it a few more steps. He puts it down.

Daru picks it up.

Back and forth, with the leaf.

It does not go anywhere in particular. There is no goal. They just pass the leaf.

Gabu thinks: this is silly. And then he thinks: no, it is not. It is fun.

After the leaf, they find a stick. Daru pushes it with his nose. Gabu pushes it. They push it around the garden, back and forth.

The stick goes to the pond, around it, to the tree, back to the flat stone.

They stop at the flat stone. They look at the stick.

"We pushed it all the way around," says Daru.

"Yes," says Gabu.

They are both a little out of breath. For turtles.

Gabu says: "I have not played in a long time."

"When did you play last?" asks Daru.

Gabu thinks. He cannot remember. Maybe never? Not like this.

He says: "I don't remember."

Daru says: "Then it was too long ago."

They look at the stick.

Gabu pushes it again. Toward Daru.

Daru pushes it back.

They play until the sun goes down.

Gabu closes his eyes.

He is happy.

He sleeps.
