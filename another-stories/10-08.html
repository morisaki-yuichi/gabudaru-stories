October 8

The Wood Pile

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

They spent the morning gathering wood.

Outside the garden gate, Gabu knew where the fallen branches were. He led them to the spots — a big branch down from a storm in summer, smaller branches along the path, dry wood from under the big tree.

Daru carried the longest pieces. Dango carried armloads of smaller sticks. Gabu chose what was dry enough to burn.

They made many trips from outside the garden to the woodpile inside.

By midmorning, the woodpile was large.

Dango stood back and looked at it. "That is a lot of warmth," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Enough for winter."

Daru stacked the pieces carefully. He had learned from Gabu that a good woodpile was stacked so air could get through — the wood dried better that way.

Dango handed Daru pieces and Daru stacked them.

When it was done, the woodpile was neat and high.

Gabu touched the top piece. Good dry wood. It would burn well.

"We did this together," Dango said. "It would have taken you all day alone."

"Two days," Gabu said.

Dango thought about that. "So we gave you two days back," it said.

Gabu looked at Dango. "Yes," he said. "You did."

Dango smiled. It liked the idea. The woodpile was not just wood. It was time — their time, all together, turned into warmth for winter.
