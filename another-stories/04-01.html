April 1

April Fool

🐢 Gabu

It is the first of April. The garden is warm and bright.

Gabu goes for his morning walk. He goes to the pond. He looks. He blinks. He looks again.

There is something in the pond. Something that was not there yesterday. Something red and round. Floating.

He walks to the edge. He looks closely.

It is an apple. A bright red apple, floating in the pond.

He tilts his head. Where did it come from? There are no red apples in his garden. The apple tree has small green apples.

He walks around the pond. The apple floats slowly.

He says: "Hello, apple. How did you get here?"

The apple bobs gently.

He thinks: maybe a bird brought it. Maybe it blew in from outside the wall.

He reaches out with one foot.

The apple is real. He touches it. It is smooth and cold and real.

He pulls it to the edge. He takes a bite.

Sweet. Very sweet. Much sweeter than his garden apples.

He eats the whole apple slowly.

He thinks: this was a gift. A strange gift. From somewhere.

He thinks about where it came from. He does not find an answer.

He walks around the garden. He looks for anything else unusual. He does not find anything.

He goes back to the pond. Just water. The apple is gone. He ate it.

He smiles.

He thinks: some things just happen. You do not need to know why. A sweet apple on the first day of April.

He goes to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps happily.
