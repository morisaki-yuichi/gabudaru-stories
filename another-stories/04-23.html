April 23

Gabu and Daru Watch the Stars

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Tonight Gabu takes Daru to look at the stars.

They go out after dark. They walk to the middle of the garden.

Gabu says: "Look up."

Daru looks up.

He is quiet for a long time.

Then he says: "Oh."

Just that. Oh.

Gabu looks up too.

The stars are very bright tonight. Many, many stars.

"I have seen stars before," says Daru. "But not like this. Not so many."

"The garden is dark," says Gabu. "Darker than other places. So you can see more stars."

Daru looks at the stars. He is quiet.

Gabu says: "I came out to look at them last month. Alone. I sat here for a long time."

"And now?" says Daru.

"Now I am here with you," says Gabu.

Daru looks at him. Then at the stars.

He says: "Which star is your favorite?"

Gabu points his nose at the bright one near the wall. "That one. It is always there, even when the others are not visible."

Daru looks. "Yes. It is very bright."

He picks his own star. A smaller one. Part of a curve.

"That one," says Daru. "It is part of a family of stars. They stay together."

Gabu looks at the curve of stars.

He thinks: a family.

He thinks: maybe he and Daru are a family now. Small, but real.

They stay under the stars for a long time.

Then they walk back to their spots.

Gabu closes his eyes.

He thinks of the stars.

He sleeps.
