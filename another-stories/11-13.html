November 13

Suke-san Teaches Braiding

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Suke-san was braiding a new length of rope.

Dango watched its hands move. Over, under, over, under.

"Can I learn?" Dango said.

Suke-san looked at Dango's hands. "Yes," it said.

It gave Dango three strands of plant fiber — long and dry and flexible. It showed Dango slowly: take the left strand over the middle. Now the right over the middle. Now the left again.

Dango tried. Its strands got tangled.

"Slower," Suke-san said.

Dango tried slower. Better. The braid started to form.

Daru came over and watched. "Can I try too?" he said.

Suke-san gave him three strands.

Daru's first attempt was more controlled than Dango's. By the second row, his braid was even.

"You have careful hands," Suke-san said to Daru.

Gabu came and looked. He had tried braiding once, long ago. He picked up three strands.

For a while, all four of them were braiding at the table.

Dango's braid was lumpy but real. Daru's was even and tight. Gabu's was slow and deliberate. Suke-san's was perfect.

"Four braids," Dango said.

"All different," Daru said.

Suke-san looked at the four braids. "You can tell who made each one," it said.

"Is that good?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san. "Craft holds the person who made it. The hands leave a mark."

Dango looked at its lumpy braid. "Even this one?" it said.

"Especially that one," said Suke-san.
