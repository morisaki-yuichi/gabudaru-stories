September 10

Gabu Tells About Winter

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

At dinner, Dango said: "Tell me about winter."

Gabu looked at Dango. "What do you want to know?"

"What it is like. I have not been here for winter. I want to know what to expect."

Gabu thought.

"Winter in this garden," he said, "is very quiet. The plants sleep. The pond sometimes has a thin layer of ice in the mornings — not thick, but it is there. The days are short. By four in the afternoon, it is dark."

Dango listened.

"The birds are mostly gone. The bees are gone. The ants are underground. The garden looks empty."

Dango looked worried.

"But," Gabu said, "inside it is warm. We light the fire. We drink warm things. We read. We tell stories. We look out the window at the cold world and we are safe in here."

Daru nodded. He had been through one winter with Gabu. "The fire is the best part," he said. "And the hot soup. And the way the garden looks in snow."

"Snow," Dango said. "I have never seen snow."

"You will," said Gabu. "Usually once or twice."

Dango looked at the garden outside. It was still autumn now. But somewhere ahead was snow.

"I am a little afraid," Dango said.

"That is normal," Gabu said. "But you will find it is good. Everything that sleeps wakes up again. And the waiting makes spring even better."

Dango held this thought and turned it over carefully, like a stone.
