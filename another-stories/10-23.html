October 23

Suke-san's Shelf Space

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu cleared a space on the shelf.

Below Dango's things and beside Daru's things, there was a small empty space.

"Yours," Gabu said to Suke-san.

Suke-san looked at the shelf. It looked at Dango's row of found things and Daru's dried flower and green glass. It looked at Gabu's journal in the middle.

Then it took out the map-stone from its bundle.

It set it on the shelf.

Just that. One thing.

Dango looked at the map-stone on the shelf. It was the largest thing on any of the shelves. It was also the oldest-looking — worn smooth at the edges from being carried for many years.

"Is that all you want?" Dango said.

"For now," Suke-san said. "The stone holds many places. It is enough."

Dango thought about that. A single object that held an entire life's worth of travel.

"My shelf has many things," Dango said. "You have one."

"Different ways," Suke-san said.

"Which is better?" Dango said.

Suke-san looked at Dango's shelf — the feather, the round stone, the wave-bark, the stripe-stone, the clay turtle, the pine cone, the seed, the jam jar.

"Each thing on your shelf is a memory," Suke-san said. "On mine, all the memories are inside the one stone. Neither is better."

Dango looked at both shelves. "We remember differently," it said.

"Yes," said Suke-san. "That is what makes a home interesting."
