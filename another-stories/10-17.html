October 17

Suke-san and the Garden

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Suke-san walked around the garden slowly.

It looked at everything. It did not ask many questions. It just looked.

Dango followed at a small distance, watching.

Suke-san went to the apple tree and put a hand on the bark. It looked up at the bare branches. It spent a long time at the tree.

"Old," Suke-san said.

"Very old," Gabu said. "I planted it when I was young."

Suke-san nodded.

It went to the pond. It crouched down and looked at the water. It put one finger in.

"Deep?" it said.

"Deep enough," Gabu said.

Suke-san sat by the pond for several minutes.

Then it went to the compost pile and looked at that too.

Dango could not figure out what Suke-san was thinking. Its face did not show excitement like Dango's face, or calm like Gabu's. It just looked, very carefully, at each thing.

Finally, Suke-san stood in the middle of the garden and was still.

"It is a well-made place," it said.

Gabu nodded. "Thank you."

"The pond and the tree together," Suke-san said. "The compost feeding the beds. The wall keeping the cold wind out." It paused. "You understood how things work together."

Gabu looked at Suke-san with something like recognition.

"You know gardens," he said.

"I know things that take time," Suke-san said. "A garden is one of those."

Gabu felt that he had found someone who understood.
