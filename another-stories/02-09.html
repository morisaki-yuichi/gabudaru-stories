February 9

Fog

🐢 Gabu

This morning, the garden is full of fog.

Gabu opens his eyes. He cannot see clearly. Everything is grey and soft. The big tree is just a dark shape. The wall is gone in the grey.

He walks out into the fog. It is strange.

He can see only a few steps ahead. Everything else is hidden.

He walks toward the pond. He knows the way. He has walked it many times. But in the fog, everything looks different.

He almost walks into the apple tree. He did not see it.

"Sorry," he says.

He finds the pond. He can hear it before he sees it. The water makes a small sound.

He sits by the pond. He cannot see the other side. The fog is on the water too.

He thinks: my garden is the same. The tree is there. The wall is there. The flowers are there. I just cannot see them.

He thinks about this for a long time.

He says: "Things are there even when I cannot see them."

He thinks about spring. He cannot see spring yet. But it is there. Coming closer.

In the middle of the morning, the sun comes up higher. The fog starts to thin. He can see more of the garden. The tree. The pond. The wall.

By noon, the fog is gone. The garden is clear. Everything is bright.

He walks around and checks on everything. The flowers are there. The tree is there. The stones are there.

He is glad to see them all.

He thinks: they were there all along. I just could not see them.

He goes to his flat stone.

He is happy.

He sleeps.
