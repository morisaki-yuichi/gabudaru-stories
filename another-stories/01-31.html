January 31

The End of January

🐢 Gabu

Today is the last day of January.

Gabu walks around the whole garden. He does this slowly. He wants to remember everything from this month.

He goes to the south wall. The yellow flower is there. Three now, with a fourth bud. He touches the petals gently with his nose.

He goes to the pond. The water is clear. He can see the bottom in the shallow parts. Small stones on the bottom.

He goes to the big tree. He looks up. The buds on the branches are small but real.

He goes to the leaf pile. The snail is still there, he thinks. He says hello.

He goes to the gate. He looks through the crack. He sees the outside path in the late afternoon light.

He goes to the old stone in the corner. He climbs up and looks at the garden from above.

He thinks about January. It was quiet. It was cold. He did not do very much. But he found a small stone. He found a yellow flower. He heard a strange sound at the gate. He watched two birds. He walked in the snow. He looked at the moon.

A good month.

He climbs down from the old stone.

He walks to his flat stone. He sits on it.

Tomorrow is February. He does not know what will happen in February. But he is ready.

He says: "Thank you, January."

He means it.

He is grateful for his garden. For the cold and the snow and the quiet and the birds. For being here.

He closes his eyes.

He is warm.

He is content.

He sleeps.
