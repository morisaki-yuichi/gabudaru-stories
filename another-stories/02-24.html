February 24

Gabu Is Not Afraid

🐢 Gabu

Today there is a sound that startles Gabu.

A loud bang from outside the wall. He does not know what it is. He goes into his shell for a moment.

He waits. He listens.

Silence.

Then the sound again. Bang. And then another. Bang bang.

He is inside his shell. It is dark and warm in there. He breathes slowly.

He waits.

After a while, the sounds stop.

He puts his head out slowly. The garden is quiet. The trees are the same. The pond is the same. Everything is fine.

He comes all the way out.

He breathes.

He thinks: that was a loud sound. I do not know what it was. But nothing in my garden changed.

He walks to the wall. He listens. He cannot hear anything now.

He says: "I do not know what that was. But it is quiet now."

He walks to his flat stone.

He thinks about being afraid. He thinks: I go into my shell when I am afraid. My shell keeps me safe. That is what it is for.

He is not ashamed of going into his shell. Turtles go into their shells. That is normal.

But he came back out when it was quiet.

He thinks: being brave does not mean not being afraid. It means coming back out.

He looks at his garden. The south wall. The tree. The pond. All fine.

He is all right.

He is not afraid now.

He closes his eyes.

He breathes slowly.

He sleeps.
