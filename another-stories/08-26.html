August 26

The Apples Keep Coming

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Every few days, more apples were ready.

Gabu checked the tree each morning. Dango always came with him.

They had learned the signs together: the color, the soft give, the way the apple came off the branch without pulling.

Dango was getting good at reading apples.

"This one," Dango said one morning, pointing.

Gabu looked. The apple Dango was pointing at had a deep red all the way around. He touched it. It gave gently.

"Yes," Gabu said. "This one."

Dango reached up and took the apple from the branch. It came off easily.

"I read the apple," Dango said.

"You did," said Gabu.

Dango held the apple. It was warm from the morning sun. Round and perfect.

"Can I eat this one?" Dango said.

"You found it," said Gabu. "Yes."

Dango ate the apple slowly, standing under the tree. It ate every part, down to the core.

Then it looked up at the tree. Still many green ones. A few more showing red.

"The tree will be empty soon," Dango said.

"Not for another few weeks," Gabu said. "But yes. The tree gives all it has. And then it rests."

Dango looked at the tree. It had given fruit all summer, silently, in the background. And now it was giving it all up.

"The tree is generous," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "That is what trees are."
