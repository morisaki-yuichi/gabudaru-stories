June 8

A Bad Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Some days just went wrong.

Daru woke up feeling strange. His stomach hurt a little. He was tired even though he had slept well.

He bumped into the garden pot. He dropped his food. He tried to catch a dragonfly and fell in the mud.

Gabu watched all this quietly.

At lunchtime, Daru sat down and put his head in his hands. "This is a bad day," he said.

"Tell me," said Gabu.

"Everything is going wrong," Daru said. He listed all the things. The pot. The food. The mud. His sore stomach.

Gabu listened. He did not say "it will be fine" or "stop worrying." He just listened.

When Daru was done, Gabu said: "Some days are like that."

"What do you do?" Daru asked.

"I try to do one small thing that is good," Gabu said. "Just one thing."

Daru thought. "Like what?"

"Come with me," Gabu said.

He took Daru to the pond. The water was still and clear. Gabu washed the mud off Daru's shell carefully, with cool water.

It felt very good.

"Better?" Gabu said.

Daru nodded. His stomach still hurt a little. But his shell was clean. And Gabu was there.

"A little better," Daru said.

"Good," said Gabu. "One small good thing. That is enough for today."

They sat by the pond together until the afternoon light came.
