August 23

The Last Hot Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Nobody knew it was the last hot day until it was over.

The day was very warm. The pond was warm. The air was thick.

They swam in the afternoon. They floated side by side. Dango floated with its hat on — the grass hat, now faded and soft. It covered its face.

"I am sleeping," Dango said from under the hat.

"You are floating," Daru said.

"I can do both," said Dango.

They stayed in the water a long time. The sun moved slowly across the sky. The light changed from white to gold.

Gabu got out first. He stood on the bank and let himself dry in the warm air.

Daru got out next. He shook the water from his ears.

Dango stayed in until the water got too cool.

That evening, they sat outside until late. The air was warm. The fireflies were out, but fewer than before.

"Are there fewer fireflies?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "They come earlier now. They will be gone by September."

Dango watched them. "Then I should watch them tonight."

They watched the fireflies until the last one blinked out.

In the morning, a cool wind came in from the north. The heat was gone. Just like that, summer changed its face.

Dango looked at the garden in the cool new air.

"Was yesterday the last hot day?" it said.

"I think so," said Gabu.

Dango was quiet. Then it said: "I watched the fireflies until the end. I am glad."
