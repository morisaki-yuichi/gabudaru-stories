November 12

Gabu's Old Book

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu had a box under his bed.

He brought it out one afternoon.

"What is that?" Dango said immediately.

"Old things," Gabu said. He opened it.

Inside were some papers, a flat stone, a dried flower, a small carved wooden turtle.

Dango, Daru, and Suke-san gathered around.

"How old?" Dango said.

"From many years ago," Gabu said. "When I was young."

He took out the carved turtle. It was small and not very skilled — rough edges, not smooth. But recognizable.

"Who made that?" Dango said.

"I did," Gabu said. "When I first came to this garden. I was learning to carve."

Dango held it carefully. "Young Gabu made this," it said.

"Yes."

"Were you very different then?" Dango said.

Gabu thought. "Faster," he said. "More uncertain. I did not know what I was doing yet."

"And now you do?" Dango said.

"More than then," Gabu said. "Less than I will."

Dango turned the small carved turtle in its hands. The carving was not good. But something in it was earnest — trying very hard.

"I like young Gabu," Dango said.

Gabu smiled. "He would have liked you," he said.

Dango put the small turtle back carefully. The box was closed. The old things went back under the bed.

But they had been seen. That was something.
