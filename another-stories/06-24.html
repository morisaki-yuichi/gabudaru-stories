June 24

Daru Sees a Firefly

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

It was dark when Daru saw it.

A small light, near the pond. It blinked once. Then again.

Daru stopped walking. He stared.

The light moved. It floated up slowly, then came back down. It blinked again — soft, yellow-green, like a tiny star that had come to earth.

"Gabu!" Daru said in a loud whisper. "Gabu, come here!"

Gabu came out of the house. He saw Daru standing very still, looking at the pond.

"What is it?"

"A light," Daru said. "A flying light."

Gabu looked. He saw the firefly. He smiled.

"It is a firefly," he said. "They come in late June. They make their own light."

"How?" Daru said. He could not look away.

"No one knows exactly," Gabu said. "It is just something they can do."

More lights appeared. Two, then five, then many. The whole edge of the pond was blinking with tiny slow lights.

Daru sat down on the grass. His mouth was open.

"They are calling to each other," Gabu said softly. "The lights are their words."

Daru watched for a long time without speaking. The fireflies moved over the pond, over the grass, between the tall plants. Their lights blinked on and off in slow, quiet conversation.

"I want to come back tomorrow night," Daru said.

"They will be here," Gabu said. "All summer."

Daru looked at the lights with wonder. There were so many things in the world that he had not known about.
