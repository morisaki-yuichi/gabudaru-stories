September 15

Midmonth Autumn

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

It was the middle of September.

The garden was half one thing and half another. Some leaves were yellow, some still green. Some apples were red, some were brown and soft and falling on their own. The air was cool in the morning and warm in the afternoon.

Gabu said this was the most interesting time of year. "Not one season," he said. "Both at once."

Dango looked at the garden in the midday light. The yellow and green mixed in the apple tree. The red rose hips against the old brown stems. The still pond and the moving leaves.

"It is beautiful," Dango said. "More beautiful than full summer."

"Why?" Daru said.

Dango thought. "Because it is about to be something different," it said. "When things are about to change, they are very bright."

Gabu looked at Dango. That was a wise thing.

"Like the last hot day," Daru said slowly. "We did not know it was the last. But we watched the fireflies very carefully."

"Yes," said Dango. "Because things are most beautiful right before they go."

They sat in the September garden and tried to look very carefully at everything around them. The yellow leaf. The red hip. The still pond. The warm afternoon light.

Gabu wrote in the journal: "Sept 15. Dango says things are most beautiful right before they change. I think about this."

He left it at that. Some things did not need more words.
