March 22

Gabu Looks at the Gate

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu stands in front of the gate for a long time.

He looks at it. The old wood. The iron hinges. The latch.

He touches the latch with his foot.

He thinks about pushing it. He has thought about this many times.

He pushes gently. The gate moves. Just a little. Just a crack.

He can feel the outside air coming through the crack. It smells of wet grass and spring and distance.

He pushes a little more. The gate opens wider.

He can see more now. The path. The grass on both sides. A tree. Far away, a fence.

He stands there with the gate open a crack.

He breathes the outside air.

He thinks: I could walk through.

He waits.

He thinks: not today. But maybe soon.

He pushes the gate gently closed. The latch clicks.

He walks away.

He walks to the middle of the garden.

He looks around. His flat stone. His tree. His pond. His flowers.

He thinks: I do not need to go out. My garden is wonderful.

But.

He thinks: there is something out there too. And one day I will go see it.

He does not know when.

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He thinks about the outside smell.

He sleeps.
