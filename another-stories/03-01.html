March 1

Spring Begins

🐢 Gabu

It is March. The first day of March.

Gabu wakes up and something is different. The air is warmer. The sky is brighter. The light is stronger.

He comes out of his shell and looks around.

The garden looks the same as yesterday, but it feels different. Like the garden took a deep breath in the night and is now wide awake.

He walks to the big tree. The buds are open at the tips. He can see the very first leaves. Tiny and pale green. Just starting.

"Hello," he says. "You are here."

He walks to the south wall. He counts the flowers. Seventeen. And more buds.

He walks to the pond. The frog is awake. More frog sounds from the water. Are there more frogs? He listens. Yes. Two different voices.

He walks to the apple tree. The buds are pink. Pink! Yesterday they were brown and green. Now pink.

He stands in the middle of the garden.

He says: "Spring is here."

He says it loudly. As loudly as he can. Which is not very loud, because he is a turtle. But he means it with his whole body.

He walks everywhere in the garden. Quickly, for him. He wants to see everything.

The climbing plant on the east wall has new leaves. The moss is bright green. The grass near the pond is taller.

He goes back to his flat stone. He is a little out of breath.

"That was fast," he says.

He laughs.

He closes his eyes.

He is so happy it is March.

He sleeps.
