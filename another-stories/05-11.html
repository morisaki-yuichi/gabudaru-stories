May 11

Daru Makes a Mistake

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru
0:00

Today Daru eats the early berries.

Gabu told him not to. He said: wait until summer. He said: early berries make your stomach hurt.

But today Daru sees the first berries on the east wall. Small and red. He smells them.

He eats a few.

They are very sour. He wrinkles his nose. He eats a few more.

In the afternoon, his stomach hurts.

He comes to Gabu.

"I ate the early berries," says Daru.

Gabu says: "I know."

Daru says: "My stomach hurts."

Gabu says: "Yes. I told you."

Daru is quiet.

Gabu says: "Come. Lie here in the sun. Drink some water. It will pass."

Daru lies in the warm sun near the pond.

Gabu sits beside him.

He does not say I told you so. He already said it. Once is enough.

He brings water from the pond in the most efficient way he can, which is not very efficient, because turtles do not carry water. But he shows Daru where to drink.

In the evening, Daru feels better.

He says: "I understand now."

Gabu says: "Now you know for yourself. Not just from being told."

Daru says: "Is that why you let me eat them? You could have stopped me."

Gabu thinks. "You learn better when you find out yourself. I could not carry you every step."

Daru is quiet.

He says: "Thank you for staying with me."

Gabu says: "Of course."

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
