January 5

A Small Stone

🐢 Gabu

It is a quiet January day. The sky is clear and very blue. The air is cold and clean.

Gabu is walking along the path by the wall. He walks slowly, as he always does. He looks at everything.

He sees something small on the ground. It is a stone. Not a big stone. A very small one. About as big as one of his toes.

He looks at it closely. It is a dark grey stone. It has one tiny white line running through it, like a thread.

Gabu picks it up. Well, he tries. His feet are not very good for picking things up. He pushes it with his nose. It rolls a little.

He looks at it for a long time.

"Where did you come from?" he asks the stone.

The stone does not answer, of course. Stones do not talk. But Gabu likes to ask.

He decides to keep it near his flat stone. He pushes it slowly across the garden. It takes a while. He does not mind.

He puts it next to his flat stone. Now there are two stones together. His big flat stone, and this little one.

"You can live here," says Gabu.

He looks at both stones. The big one and the small one. He likes that they are different sizes. He likes that they are together.

In the afternoon, Gabu sits on his flat stone. He looks at the little stone beside him. The white line in it catches the light.

He feels good about this.

When it is dark, Gabu curls up next to the two stones. He is warm.

He closes his eyes and sleeps.
