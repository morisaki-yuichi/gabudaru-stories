December 30

The Second to Last Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

One more day after today.

The year was almost over.

Dango was quiet in a way that was unusual for it.

Daru noticed. "What is it?"

"I am thinking," Dango said.

"About what?"

"About how much I did not know in July," Dango said. "When I arrived."

Daru thought about his own arrival in April. What he had not known then.

"You knew you wanted to stay," Daru said.

"Yes," said Dango. "I knew that."

"The rest, you learned," Daru said.

"Is that how it works?" Dango said. "You come with the wanting, and the rest follows?"

Daru thought. "I think so. Gabu already knew everything about this garden. But he did not know what it would be with us here. He had to learn that too."

Dango looked at Gabu, who was at his usual window seat.

"He learned us," Dango said.

"And we learned him," said Daru.

Suke-san had been listening. It said: "Every friendship is learning the other person."

"Like a new language," Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san. "But faster, when you live together."

Dango thought about the language of Gabu's expressions. Of Daru's quiet. Of Suke-san's stillness.

"I speak you all," Dango said.

Daru smiled. "Yes," he said. "We speak you too."
