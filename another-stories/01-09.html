January 9

The Cold Wind

🐢 Gabu

Today the wind is blowing. It is a cold, sharp wind from the north. The bare branches of the big tree move back and forth. The dry grass bends down and then stands up again.

Gabu walks out and the wind pushes against him. He is small and low to the ground, so the wind does not push him over. But he can feel it.

His eyes are cold. His feet are cold. Even his shell feels cold.

He walks to the side of the big tree. There is less wind here. The tree blocks it. He sits close to the tree trunk.

"Better," says Gabu.

He watches the wind moving through the garden. He cannot see the wind. But he can see everything the wind moves. The grass. The branches. An old leaf rolling across the path.

The leaf rolls past him. Fast. Then it hits the wall and stops.

"Hello, leaf," says Gabu.

The leaf does not say anything. It is just a leaf.

Gabu thinks about wind. He does not know where wind comes from. From somewhere far away. From outside the garden. From the world he has never seen.

He thinks: the wind has been many places. It has come here to my garden.

He opens his mouth a little. He breathes in the wind.

"Hello, wind," he says quietly. "I am Gabu. I live here."

The wind keeps blowing. But Gabu feels a little better about it now.

In the afternoon the wind gets softer. The branches stop moving so much. The garden is calmer.

Gabu walks to his flat stone. He sits on it.

He closes his eyes. He is glad the wind is softer now. He sleeps.
