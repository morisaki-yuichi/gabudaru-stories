July 16

Dango Misses Home

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

One afternoon, Dango was quiet.

This was unusual. Dango was almost never quiet.

Daru noticed. He sat beside Dango under the apple tree.

"What is it?" Daru said.

Dango was looking at the wall. "I was thinking about where I came from," it said.

Daru waited.

"I came from a long way away," Dango said. "I do not remember a home like this. Not a garden. Just... moving."

"Were you always alone?" Daru said.

"Mostly," Dango said. "Sometimes I met others. But I never stayed."

"Why not?"

Dango thought. "Because I did not know if I was welcome," it said.

Daru was quiet for a moment. Then he said: "You are welcome here."

Dango looked at him.

"Gabu said so," Daru said. "And I say so."

"But what if I am too loud?" Dango said. "And ask too many questions? And run when I should sit still?"

"You are learning," Daru said. "I had to learn too. Gabu was patient with me."

Dango looked at the garden. The pond was glittering in the afternoon light. The house was warm. The bread smell was still faintly in the air.

"This feels like a home," Dango said.

"It is," Daru said. "Ours and yours."

Dango leaned against Daru's shell, just a little. Daru did not move away.

They sat together under the apple tree until Gabu called them for dinner.
