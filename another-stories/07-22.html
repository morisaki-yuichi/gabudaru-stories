July 22

Night Swimming

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango had an idea.

"Let us swim in the pond at night," it said.

Daru looked at the dark pond. "At night?"

"Yes. After dinner. When it is dark. The fireflies will be there."

Gabu considered this. The pond was safe. The water was warm. And the night was mild.

"All right," he said.

After dinner, when the sky was dark and the fireflies were out, they went to the pond.

They waded in slowly. The water was warm — warmer than during the day, somehow, or maybe it just felt warmer because the air had cooled. It was dark and still. Their feet made soft sounds.

The fireflies moved around them. Their lights reflected in the water — little green points of light, real above and mirrored below.

Dango floated on its back. The fireflies moved over it. One landed on Dango's nose and blinked twice, then flew away.

"This is the best idea I have had," Dango said.

Daru floated nearby. He looked up. The stars were out. He could see the river of stars from here, reflected in the pond. Stars above, stars below.

Gabu swam slowly in a circle. He had swum in this pond many nights. But it had never been like this — with two others, and fireflies, and the sound of small happy movements.

"Let us do this every summer," Dango said.

"Yes," said Daru.

"Yes," said Gabu.

They swam slowly in the dark until they were all wonderfully tired.
