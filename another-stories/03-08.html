March 8

Something Behind the Wall

🐢 Gabu

Gabu hears something behind the wall today.

He is walking near the north wall when he hears it. A sound. Not a bird. Not wind. A living sound.

He stops. He listens.

The sound again. Something moving in the leaves outside the wall. Something walking.

He walks to the wall. He puts his ear against it. He can hear more clearly now.

Something is moving out there. It sounds larger than a beetle. Larger than a frog.

He walks to the gate. He looks through the crack.

He can see the path. He can see a little of the grass beyond it.

He sees something move in the tall grass at the edge of his view. Just a flash of movement. Then gone.

He waits.

Nothing more.

He thinks: what was that?

He thinks about the creatures outside his wall. He has seen birds come over. He has seen the cat on the wall. He knows there are creatures out there.

But who was that today? The sound was soft and careful. Something that moves quietly.

He waits a long time at the gate.

Nothing more comes.

He walks away from the gate. He walks to the middle of the garden.

He sits down. He thinks.

He thinks: things live out there. Things I have not seen.

He looks at the wall. He looks at the gate.

He is not afraid.

He is curious.

He thinks: one day I will go out and see.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
