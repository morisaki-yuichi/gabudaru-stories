April 6

Gabu Prepares

🐢 Gabu
0:00

Gabu decides to prepare the garden today.

He does not know exactly what he is preparing for. But he has the feeling of getting ready.

He walks to the pond. He finds an old stick near the edge. He moves it to the side. The pond edge looks cleaner.

He walks along the path. He moves some small pebbles off the path so it is clearer.

He goes to his flat stone. He sweeps leaves off it with his foot. The stone is clean and flat and smooth.

He goes to the blue stone and the grey stone. He arranges them a little. Just right.

He walks to the south wall. He looks at the flowers. They are beautiful. He cannot do much for them. They are already perfect.

He walks to the gate. He sniffs the crack again. The living smell is strong today. Close.

He says: "My garden is ready."

He is not sure who he is saying this to.

He walks to the big tree. He sits under it in the shade.

He thinks about having a visitor. What would he do? He would show them the pond. The flowers. The big tree. The old stone in the corner. The far corner with the soft moss. The gate.

He would tell them: this is my garden. I love it. I am glad you are here.

He closes his eyes.

He thinks about saying those words.

He is ready to say them.

He sleeps.
