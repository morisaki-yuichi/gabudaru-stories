December 4

Stories of Snow

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu looked at the sky.

"Snow is possible," he said.

Dango looked at the sky. White and low. "What does snow look like, exactly?"

It had never seen real snow. Not here, not anywhere.

Gabu described it: small crystals of ice that fell like white dust. Silent falling. Everything covered until the world was white.

Daru described it: soft underfoot, until it packed hard. Tracks in it. The garden unrecognizable.

Suke-san described it: heavy on tree branches until they bent. The smell of cold nothing.

Dango tried to imagine all three descriptions at once.

"What does it feel like?" it said.

"Cold," said Daru. "But not sharp. Soft cold."

"Does it hurt?"

"No," said Gabu. "It is gentle."

Dango looked at the white sky. "I want to see it," it said.

"You will," said Gabu. "Maybe this week. Maybe this month. Some years it comes twice."

Dango thought about white everywhere. The pond white. The apple tree white. The path white.

"And our footprints in it," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "That is one of the best things."

"What are the others?" Dango said.

"The silence," said Daru. "Everything sounds different in snow. Quieter. Like the world is listening."

Dango held all these things in its mind and looked at the sky.

Come on, snow, it thought.
