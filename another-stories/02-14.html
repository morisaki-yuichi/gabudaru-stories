February 14

A Gift

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu finds something at his flat stone.

He does not know what it is at first. He walks up to his flat stone in the morning and there is something sitting on it. Something he did not put there.

It is a small piece of bark. Pale brown. And on top of the bark, two small round berries. Red and bright.

He looks at them.

He looks around the garden. Who put this here?

He does not see anyone.

He looks at the berries. They are bright red. Like small lights.

He smells them. Sweet. Very sweet. He has not smelled sweet food in a long time.

He eats one berry.

Oh.

It is wonderful. Sweet and sharp and full of taste. He closes his eyes while he eats it. He tastes every bit.

He eats the second berry.

Also wonderful.

He licks the bark where the berries were.

He sits on his flat stone. He thinks: where did this come from?

He looks at the big tree. A bird? The birds sometimes drop things.

He looks around the garden. He does not see anyone.

He says: "Thank you."

He says it to the whole garden. To the bird if it was a bird. To whoever brought the gift.

He thinks: someone gave me something nice today. I do not know who.

He decides it does not matter who. What matters is: there are two sweet berries. And they were good.

He is grateful.

He closes his eyes.

He still tastes the sweetness.

He sleeps.
