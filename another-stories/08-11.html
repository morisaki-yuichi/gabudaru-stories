August 11

Three Sleeping Spots

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

Gabu had always slept inside, in his sleeping spot against the south wall.

Daru had made a sleeping spot near the big leaf plant — soft and sheltered, with a view of the pond.

Dango had its spot in the corner near the rose bush. When it arrived, Gabu had put down a layer of dry grass and leaves for it. Dango had added to it over the weeks — more leaves, a piece of soft bark, the feather that had fallen from its shelf.

One night, Dango said: "Can we each describe our sleeping spots?"

They did.

Gabu: "My spot is dark and quiet. The wall is warm from the daytime sun. I can hear the night sounds from outside."

Daru: "My spot is near the water. I like to hear the pond in the night. Sometimes I hear the frogs."

Dango: "My spot smells like the rose bush. Even in late summer, there is a faint smell. And the rose hips are right there when I wake up."

They were quiet after.

"A sleeping spot is the most personal thing," Gabu said. "It is where you begin and end each day."

Dango thought about this. Before coming here, it had slept anywhere. Now it had a spot. A real one.

"I can smell the rose bush right now," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. He could smell it too.

They went to their spots. Each one was different. Each one was right.

They slept.
