November 16

The Cold Works Its Way In

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

The cold was coming into the house more now.

Not into the main room, where the fire kept things warm. But in the morning, in the corners, there was a chill.

Suke-san had already fixed the shutter. Now it turned its attention to small gaps around the door.

It used clay mixed with fiber — a paste it made that dried hard. It pressed it into the cracks with careful fingers.

Dango watched.

"Where did you learn this?" it said.

"I have wintered in many places," Suke-san said. "Cold places. You learn to seal the gaps."

"What was the coldest place you wintered?" Dango said.

Suke-san thought. "A stone shelter in the mountains," it said. "No fire. Only my own warmth inside my shell."

Dango looked at Suke-san with wide eyes. "Was it terrible?"

"It was very cold," Suke-san said. "But I was all right. The shell protects."

Gabu came and looked at the sealed gaps. "Better," he said.

Suke-san nodded. It moved to the next gap.

Dango thought about Suke-san in a cold mountain shelter, alone, with nothing but its shell.

"Suke-san," it said.

"Yes?"

"I am glad you are here and not in a cold mountain shelter."

Suke-san kept working, pressing the paste carefully. "So am I," it said. "So am I."
