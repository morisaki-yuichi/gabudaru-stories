December 24

The Long Night

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

The longest nights were still now, even past the solstice.

They stayed up later than usual.

Gabu made something special — a warm drink with dried apple and spices he kept in a small jar. The smell filled the house.

Dango and Daru and Suke-san sat by the fire.

Dango had its wreath in its hands, the one it had made for the door. It touched each of the four elements — the feather, the rope loop, the bark piece, the dried flower.

"Tell me about before," Dango said.

"Before what?" Gabu said.

"Before all this. When you were alone here in December."

Gabu gave them each a cup of the warm drink. He sat down.

"I was all right," he said. "I had my routines. The fire. The journal. The winter preparations. I knew what to do."

"But?" Dango said.

"But the house was quiet," Gabu said. "Very quiet."

He looked around at the four of them.

"Now the house is noisy," he said.

Dango laughed. "I make noise," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Thank you for that."

Dango smiled. It put the wreath back on the door.

"This December is different," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Every December will be different now," Dango said.

Gabu looked at the fire. "Yes," he said. "I hope so."

They sat together in the warm house on the long December night, drinking the spiced apple drink, and the fire kept them all.
