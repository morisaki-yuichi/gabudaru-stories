August 24

Dango Writes a List

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Dango borrowed Gabu's pencil and a piece of paper.

It sat at the table and wrote very carefully.

Daru looked over its shoulder. "What is that?"

"A list," Dango said. "Of things I want to remember about summer."

Daru read the list:

The warmest pond water. The first night with fireflies. The dragonfly on my shell. The apple that was ready. Night swimming. Floating with a hat on. The bread smell. The stone that holds the sun. Watching the ant road with Daru. Gabu's story about the mountain.

Daru looked at the list. "That is a good list," he said.

"I do not want to forget," Dango said.

Daru said: "I have those memories too, but I did not write them."

"You have a good memory," Dango said. "I forget things. I need to write."

Gabu came in and saw the list. He read it. He did not say anything for a moment.

Then he went to the journal and opened it. "You can put it in here," he said.

Dango looked at the journal. "In your journal?"

"Yes," Gabu said. "It is our journal now."

Dango folded the list and put it carefully between two pages. It fit perfectly.

"Gabu's story about the mountain," Daru said softly, reading the list again.

"That was my favourite," Dango said.
