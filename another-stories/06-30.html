June 30

The Last Day of June

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

It was the last day of June.

In the morning, Gabu made a count. Thirty days. Thirty stories.

He thought about June: the first summer heat, the long evenings, the solstice, the storm, the fireflies, the sound from outside the wall.

Daru came and sat beside him.

"What are you thinking?" Daru said.

"About the month," Gabu said. "It went fast."

Daru thought too. He had learned to float in the pond. He had built a shelter. He had heard fireflies talking with their lights. He had seen the garden in the dark. He had heard the strange sound beyond the wall.

"What comes next?" Daru said.

"July," said Gabu. He said it simply. But there was something in his voice.

Daru looked at him. "What happens in July?"

"Summer goes on," Gabu said. "The apples keep growing. The pond stays warm." He paused. "And I think something may change."

"The sound from outside the wall?" Daru said.

Gabu was quiet. "Maybe," he said.

Daru looked at the gate. It was closed now. But he had been outside it. He knew the path. He knew the clearing in the sunlight.

Somewhere out there, something had made a small high sound like a question.

"I hope it comes here," Daru said.

"I think it might," said Gabu.

The sun rose over the garden wall. June was over. July was waiting, warm and full of green.

Something was coming.
