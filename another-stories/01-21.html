January 21

The Gate

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu goes to the gate again.

The gate is in the east wall. It is made of old dark wood, with iron hinges. The iron is a little rusty. The wood is a little soft from the rain and years.

Gabu puts his face close to the gate. He looks through a crack between the boards. He can see something on the other side. A path. Brown earth. The edge of a tree.

He sniffs. He smells the same things as yesterday. Earth. Grass. Something unknown.

He looks through the crack for a long time.

He thinks: what is out there? Fields? Other gardens? A river? A road? He does not know.

He thinks about going out. He thinks: I could push the gate open. The wood is old and soft. I could do it.

But he does not.

He is not ready. Not today.

He thinks: why am I not ready? He does not have an answer. He just knows he is not. The garden is enough for now.

He turns and walks away from the gate. He walks to the middle of the garden.

He looks around. His garden. The big tree. The pond. The flat stone. The wall with its many stones.

"This is enough," he says.

He does not say it sadly. He says it quietly, like a fact.

He walks to his flat stone and sits on it.

He looks at the gate from far away. It does not move.

He thinks: one day, maybe. When I am ready.

He closes his eyes.

The garden is warm around him.

He is content.

He sleeps.
