March 5

A Sunny Morning

🐢 Gabu

The sun is strong today. Really strong. The kind of strong that means it is truly spring and not winter pretending to be spring.

Gabu stands in it with his eyes closed.

His shell gets warm. Not just surface-warm. Deep-warm. The warmth goes all the way in.

He feels his whole body relax.

He breathes slowly. In and out. In and out. Warm, warm, warm.

He opens his eyes. The garden is bright. Everything looks sharp and clear in the strong sun. The flowers are bright. The new leaves are bright. The pond shines like a mirror.

He walks to the pond. He stands at the edge.

He puts one foot in.

Warm. Not cold!

He puts both front feet in.

Still warm. Warmer than he expected.

He walks in. The water is cool but not cold. He feels it on his belly. He walks deeper.

He swims a little. He has not swum all winter.

His legs remember what to do. He moves through the water. He goes to the middle of the pond.

He looks around from the middle of the pond. He can see his whole garden.

He sees the big tree. The apple tree. The wall. The gate. His flat stone.

"Hello, garden," he says.

He swims back to the edge. He gets out. He shakes a little.

He walks to his flat stone. He sits in the sun. The sun dries him quickly.

He is happy.

He has swum in his pond for the first time this year.

He closes his eyes.

The sun is warm.

He sleeps.
