February 4

Gabu Finds Food

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu is looking for food. His stomach is empty. He needs to eat.

He goes to the south wall first. He finds some small plants growing in the cracks. He eats them carefully. They taste a little bitter but he does not mind.

He goes to the corner near the leaf pile. He digs a little with his foot in the soft earth. He finds some old seeds. He eats them.

He goes to the north side of the wall. There is more moss here. He eats some moss. It is cold and damp but it fills his stomach a little.

He walks along the base of the wall. He finds some old mushrooms. Small ones. They grew after the rain. He eats three of them.

He goes to the pond edge. He finds some water plants coming up at the edge. He eats them. They taste clean and good.

He sits by the pond. He is not full, but he is less hungry.

He thinks about food. In summer, there is so much. Sweet berries. Fresh green leaves. Mushrooms everywhere after rain. He can eat as much as he wants.

In winter, he has to look harder. He has to be patient. He has to be happy with small things.

"Small things are enough," he says.

He is not sad about this. It is just the way of winter.

He walks to his flat stone. He sits on it.

The sun comes out. He sits in the sun.

His stomach is warm. His shell is warm. The stone is under his feet.

He is all right.

He closes his eyes.

He rests.

He is content.
