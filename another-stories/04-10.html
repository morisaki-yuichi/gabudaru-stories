April 10

Daru!

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

It happens in the morning.

Gabu is at the pond when he hears it. A sound at the gate. A knocking sound. Gentle. Then again.

He turns. He looks at the gate.

The gate is moving. The latch is lifting. Slowly.

He cannot move. He watches.

The gate opens. Just a crack. Then a little more.

And through the gate comes a turtle.

A small turtle. Smaller than Gabu. A round shell, greenish-brown. Big eyes, looking around with great curiosity. A little nervous, maybe. But mostly curious.

The turtle sees Gabu.

They look at each other.

Neither of them moves.

Then the other turtle says: "Hello. Is this the garden?"

Gabu opens his mouth. He closes it. He opens it again.

"Yes," he says. "This is the garden."

The other turtle looks around. Eyes wide. Taking everything in. The flowers. The pond. The big tree. The blue sky.

"It's beautiful," says the other turtle.

Gabu looks at his garden. He has always known it is beautiful. But hearing it said by someone else, for the first time, makes it feel even more beautiful.

"Yes," says Gabu. "It is."

The other turtle looks at him. "My name is Daru."

"I am Gabu," says Gabu.

They look at each other.

Gabu feels something he has never felt before. Or has not felt for a very long time. A warm, full feeling.

"Come in," says Gabu. "Let me show you."

Daru steps all the way through the gate.

The gate closes behind him.

Gabu walks toward the pond.

"This way," says Gabu.

And they walk together, for the first time, toward the pond.

The garden has two turtles now.
