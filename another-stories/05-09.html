May 9

The Tall Flowers

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

New flowers are coming up today. Taller ones.

Gabu knows these flowers. They come every year. Tall, with big round heads. Orange and yellow and deep red.

They are just beginning. The stems are coming up. No flowers yet. Just long green stems.

Daru looks at them.

"What are these?" he asks.

"Wait," says Gabu. "In a few weeks."

Daru is not very patient when it comes to flowers.

"But what are they now?"

"Stems," says Gabu. "Just stems. The flower is inside. Waiting."

Daru looks at the stem. He touches it gently.

"The flower is in there?" he says.

"Yes," says Gabu. "All of it. The petals, the color, everything. Already there. Just not open yet."

Daru is surprised.

"The whole flower is already in there?"

"Yes."

Daru thinks about this. He looks at the stem with different eyes.

He says: "So it is already a flower. It just looks like a stem."

Gabu thinks. "Yes. Something like that."

Daru touches the stem again. Gently, respectfully.

"Hello, flower," he says.

Gabu smiles.

He looks at the stems too. He thinks about what Daru said. It is already a flower. It just looks like a stem.

He thinks: things are often more than they look.

He walks to his flat stone.

He thinks about this.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
