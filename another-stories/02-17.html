February 17

Looking for Something

🐢 Gabu

Gabu has a feeling today. A feeling like something is waiting for him. Something to find.

He walks around the garden. He looks carefully.

He looks near the pond. He looks under the leaves. He looks by the wall, all the way around. He looks under the big tree.

He does not find anything new.

He goes back to the south wall. He looks at the flowers. He counts them.

Wait.

Seven flowers. Yesterday there were six. A new one opened in the night.

He looks at it. Small and white, with a yellow center.

"Hello," he says. "You are new."

He looks at it for a long time.

He thinks: was this what I was looking for?

He is not sure.

He walks to the gate. He looks through the crack.

Outside: the path. Empty.

He listens. Nothing.

He walks to the pond. He looks in the water.

He sees himself. And he sees something else. A new plant at the bottom of the pond. Green, waving gently.

He looks at it.

"Hello," he says. "When did you come?"

He looks at the plant for a long time.

He thinks: the garden always has something new. I just have to look.

He walks back to his flat stone.

He thinks about everything he found today. A new flower. A new pond plant. The mud from yesterday. The frog.

He has a good garden. There is always something.

He closes his eyes.

He is content.

He sleeps.
