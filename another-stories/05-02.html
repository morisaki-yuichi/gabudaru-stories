May 2

Two Friends in the Rain

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

It rains again today. A warm May rain.

Gabu and Daru walk in the rain together.

The warm rain is different from the cold winter rain. It smells of flowers and warm earth.

They walk to the pond. The rain makes circles everywhere.

"There," says Daru. He points at a spot. "A big circle. A big drop fell."

"There," says Gabu. "Three small circles together."

They look for circles. Big ones, small ones, overlapping ones.

It is a game they made up without deciding to.

"There," says Daru. "A circle inside a circle."

Gabu looks. Yes. A big drop made a big circle and a small drop landed in the middle of it.

"A circle within a circle," says Gabu.

They walk along the path. The rain falls on their shells, drumming softly.

"Do you like rain?" asks Daru.

Gabu thinks. "Sometimes. Not always. In winter I did not like it much. Now it is warm. I like it more."

"I like the smell," says Daru. "Rain on warm earth. And the flowers after."

"Yes," says Gabu. "The flowers after."

They walk under the big tree. The leaves catch the rain. Drops fall from the leaves to the ground in slow, fat drops.

They stand under the tree side by side.

Daru says: "I am glad it rained today."

Gabu says: "Why?"

Daru says: "Because we walked in it together."

Gabu thinks: I have walked in rain many times. Alone. It was fine. But this is better.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
