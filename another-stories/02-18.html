February 18

The High Branch

🐢 Gabu

There is something in the high branches of the big tree.

Gabu can see something up there. Something dark. He looks at it for a long time.

It is a nest. A round, dark nest, made of twigs and dry grass. Sitting in the fork of two high branches.

He has not noticed it before. It was there in winter, hidden among the leaves. Now the branches are bare and he can see it clearly.

He walks around the tree. He looks up from every side. The nest is round and neat. Quite big. Bigger than his head.

He thinks: who made this?

He looks for the bird. He does not see one.

He thinks: a bird made this nest. They worked hard. Twig by twig. Each piece placed just right.

He looks at it for a long time.

He thinks: I have never made anything like that. I have no nest. I have my flat stone, which I did not make.

He thinks about making something. But what? He does not know how to make a nest. He has no hands, just feet.

He looks at his small stone collection next to his flat stone. The flat stone. The grey stone. The blue stone.

He did arrange those. He put them together. That is a kind of making.

He looks at the nest again.

He says: "That is very good work. The bird who made that is very skilled."

He walks to his flat stone.

He looks at his stones.

He moves the blue stone a little. He looks at it from a different angle.

He is pleased.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
