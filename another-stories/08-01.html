August 1

August Begins

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

August arrived like a door opening onto heat.

The first morning was bright and still. The air was thick and warm even before the sun was fully up.

Gabu came out and breathed it in. Summer was at its deepest point. Everything was big and ripe and full.

Daru and Dango came out together, still sleepy.

"It is the hottest yet," Dango said.

"August is always the hottest," Gabu said. "But it is also the richest."

They looked at the garden. The apple tree was full of large apples, still green. The pond was covered in floating leaves. The rose bush had small red hips now where the flowers had been.

"What is richest about it?" Daru said.

Gabu thought. "Everything is at its largest," he said. "The plants have had all spring and summer to grow. The sun has been here the longest. August is summer remembering what it is."

Dango walked slowly around the garden, touching things. The apple. The rose hip. The edge of the pond.

"It feels like something about to be done," Dango said. "Like the last page of a long story."

Gabu looked at Dango. That was a good way to put it.

"Yes," he said. "August is that."

They ate breakfast outside in the warm morning air. The light was golden and long. A bee came and went from the rose hip.

The first day of August lay ahead of them, full and warm and theirs.
