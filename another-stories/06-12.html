June 12

After the Rain

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

After the rain, the garden was full of snails.

They were everywhere. On the stones. On the leaves. On the sides of the flower pots. Each one moved very slowly, leaving a silver line behind it.

Daru was fascinated.

"There are so many," he said.

"The rain brings them out," Gabu said. "They like wet things."

Daru crouched down to look at one. It was small and brown, with a shell that spiraled inward like a tiny staircase.

"Hello," Daru said to it.

The snail did not answer. It kept moving, very slowly, toward a wet leaf.

"They don't talk much," Gabu said.

"That is all right," Daru said. "They are interesting anyway."

He followed one snail for a few minutes, watching it move. It was so slow. Daru was a slow mover too, but this snail was slower. He felt fast compared to it.

"Are they afraid of anything?" Daru asked.

"Not much," Gabu said. "They carry their homes on their backs. So wherever they go, they are always safe."

Daru looked at his own shell. He touched it with one hand.

"We do too," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "We are alike in that way."

They watched the snails together as the garden dried out in the morning sun. The silver lines they left behind glittered for a while, and then slowly faded as the day got warm.
