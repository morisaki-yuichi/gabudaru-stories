May 1

May Morning

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

May begins warm and bright.

Gabu wakes up and stretches all four legs. He looks at Daru, who is already awake near the south wall.

"Good morning!" says Daru.

"Good morning," says Gabu.

They eat breakfast together. The grass is long now and thick and full of flavor. Gabu eats slowly. He tastes each bite.

After breakfast, they go to the pond.

The pond is green and alive. Dragonflies appear today for the first time. Long, bright blue ones. They hover above the water.

Daru tries to catch one. He reaches up with his nose. The dragonfly moves just out of reach.

"Too fast," says Gabu.

Daru does not mind. He watches the dragonfly instead.

"What are they?" he asks.

"Dragonflies," says Gabu. "They live in the pond when they are small. Then they become these."

Daru watches one. "They were in the pond? When they were small?"

"Yes," says Gabu. "I saw the larvae last month."

Daru looks at the pond. Then at the dragonfly.

"The pond made them," he says.

Gabu thinks about this. "Yes. In a way."

They watch the dragonflies all morning.

Gabu thinks: I have watched dragonflies every year. But this year I have someone to watch them with.

He thinks about the year going forward. Summer. Autumn. More together.

He is glad.

He closes his eyes.

He listens to the dragonflies.

He sleeps.
