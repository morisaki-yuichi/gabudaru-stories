August 10

The Rain That Saved the Garden

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

After five hot days, rain came.

It came at night — a long, slow rain. It fell all night and into the morning.

Gabu woke up and heard it. He lay in his sleeping spot and listened. He had been worried. Five dry days in high summer could damage the plants.

In the morning, he went out.

The garden was soaked and green and new-smelling. The plants that had been drooping were standing up straight. The pond was full. The mud between the plants was dark and rich.

Dango came out and spun around with arms wide.

"Rain!" it shouted.

Daru came out and stepped carefully into the wet grass. He bent down and touched the soil. "It drank all of it," he said.

Gabu walked to the apple tree. The apples were still on their branches. He looked at them — they seemed larger again, round and firm.

"The rain came at the right time," he said.

They walked around the garden together. Everything had color now — sharper green, brighter red on the rose hips, deeper brown in the mud.

Dango was still moving in the rain, even though it was mostly over. Just a few last drops fell.

"I love rain," Dango said. "I know I say this every time. But I do."

"I know," said Daru. "Every time I believe you."

Gabu looked at the garden. Fed. Whole. Alive.

He felt the same way Dango felt, but quietly, inside.
