January 15

The Moon at Night

🐢 Gabu

Tonight Gabu wakes up in the middle of the night. He does not know why.

He opens his eyes. The garden is full of light. But it is not the sun. The light is soft and cool. It is the moon.

The moon is very big tonight. It is round and bright. It is white, like a very clean stone in the sky.

Gabu looks at it.

He has seen the moon before, of course. Many times. But tonight it seems especially big. Especially bright.

He walks to the middle of the garden. The moonlight falls on the grass. The pond catches the moonlight and holds it. The water looks like silver.

"Beautiful," says Gabu.

His shadow is on the ground. A small turtle shadow. He looks at it. He moves one foot. His shadow moves too.

He looks at the trees and the wall. Everything has a shadow in the moonlight. Long, cool shadows.

It is very quiet. No birds. No wind. Just the moonlight and the silence.

He walks to the pond. The moon is in the water. He looks at the moon in the water and then at the moon in the sky. Two moons. One real, one reflection.

"Hello, moon," says Gabu.

He sits by the pond for a long time. He does not feel tired. He feels peaceful.

When the moon starts to set, it gets darker. The sky in the east starts to get lighter. Blue, then grey.

Gabu walks back to his flat stone.

He has seen something beautiful tonight.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps until morning, with the memory of the moon in his mind.
