July 4

Looking at Stars

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

One clear night, Gabu woke Daru up.

"Come outside," Gabu said. "Quietly."

Daru came out. The garden was dark and very still. The air was warm even at night.

Gabu was lying on his back on the flat stone near the pond.

"Look up," he said.

Daru looked up.

The sky was full of stars. More stars than Daru had ever counted. They went from one side of the sky to the other, crowded and bright. Some were big and some were tiny. Some were white and some were faintly blue or faintly orange.

In the middle, a wide band of light went across the sky like a river.

"What is that?" Daru said.

"The river of stars," Gabu said. "Many, many stars so close together they look like light."

Daru lay down on the stone beside Gabu. He looked up.

He had never seen this before. He had seen stars before. But not like this — so many, so bright, so wide.

"Are they very far?" he said.

"Very far," said Gabu. "Farther than you can imagine."

Daru tried to imagine. He could not.

They lay on the stone for a long time. The stars moved very slowly. Not enough to really see, but if you watched for a long time, you could feel it.

"I am glad we are here and not up there," Daru said.

"Why?" said Gabu.

"Because you are here," said Daru. "And the garden is here."

Gabu looked at the stars and smiled.
