June 20

The Longest Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today was the longest day of the year.

Gabu had been counting. Twenty days into June. The summer solstice.

He told Daru at breakfast. "Today the sun will be in the sky longer than any other day. After today, the days start getting shorter."

Daru looked up. The sky was already bright and blue.

"I want to watch the whole day," Daru said.

They watched the sun rise. It came up slowly, orange and warm. The garden filled with golden light.

They watched through the morning. The sun moved slowly across the sky.

They watched through the afternoon. The heat grew and then began to soften.

They ate dinner outside and watched the evening come. The sun went red. The shadows grew long and longer.

And still the sun did not set.

"It is taking so long," Daru said. He was not complaining. He was amazed.

"Yes," said Gabu.

They sat in the shelter Daru had built and watched the sky turn from orange to pink to purple. The stars appeared one by one. And at last, very late, the sun was gone.

"That was the whole day," Daru said quietly.

Gabu nodded.

"It was a lot of day," Daru said.

Gabu smiled. "That is what I think every year," he said.

Daru watched the first bright star. He felt small and happy.

"Tomorrow the days get shorter," he said. "But we still have all of summer."

"Yes," said Gabu. "We do."
