May 13

Swimming in the Pond

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Gabu and Daru swim in the pond.

The water is warm now. Really warm. A proper summer warmth starting.

Gabu goes in first. He walks in slowly, feeling the warm water on his feet, then his legs, then his belly.

He swims to the middle.

Daru comes in fast. He likes water. He splashes. He goes under and comes back up.

"Come to the middle," calls Gabu.

Daru swims to the middle. They float side by side.

Above them: blue sky with a few white clouds.

Around them: the garden, all four walls, visible from the middle of the pond.

Below them: the cool dark water.

Daru says: "I love swimming."

"I know," says Gabu. "You are better at it than me."

Daru says: "You are better at sitting still on land. Different skills."

Gabu thinks about this. "Different skills," he agrees.

They float for a while.

A dragonfly hovers above the water. It looks at them.

"Hello, dragonfly," says Daru.

The dragonfly darts away.

Gabu says: "What was it like, swimming where you came from? In the fields?"

Daru says: "There was a small stream. Not a pond. I liked the stream. It moved."

"The pond does not move," says Gabu.

"No," says Daru. "But it has depth. The stream was shallow."

They swim back to the edge.

They get out. They stand in the sun and dry off.

Gabu is happy.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
