November 21

The Shortest Stories

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

One evening, Gabu said: "Tell the shortest story you know."

They thought.

Dango went first: "A turtle was afraid of the dark. Then it was not."

"That is two sentences," Daru said.

"The shortest story is two sentences," Dango said.

Daru thought. He said: "Something was lost. Then it was found."

"Even shorter," Dango said.

Gabu thought for a long time. He said: "The garden waited. Friends came."

They were quiet.

"That is us," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

Suke-san had been listening. Now it spoke.

"Someone was walking," it said. "Then they stopped."

They all looked at Suke-san.

"Is that the whole story?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Suke-san.

"It sounds like the beginning," Dango said.

"It is the beginning," Suke-san said. "The middle is here." It paused. "The end is a long way off."

Dango smiled. "Good," it said. "I do not want the end for a long time."

"The end of any good story is far from the beginning," Gabu said. "That is how you know it is a good story."

They sat with their four very short stories in the firelit room.

Four stories. One house.

One story that was still being told.
