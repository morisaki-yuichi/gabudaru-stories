September 18

Gabu Is Tired

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Some days, Gabu was tired.

On this day, he did not come out early. He stayed in his sleeping spot longer than usual.

Daru and Dango made breakfast without him.

"Is he all right?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Daru. "Gabu has tired days. Everyone does."

Dango looked at the door of the house. "What do we do?"

"Let him rest," said Daru. "And when he comes out, it will be like a normal day. Only later."

Dango thought about this. It wanted to help. But helping someone rest meant not bothering them.

They ate breakfast and were quiet.

They did gentle things in the garden — swept the path, moved some leaves, checked the apple tree. No shouting, no games.

When Gabu came out in the late morning, he blinked in the light.

"You let me sleep," he said.

"Yes," said Daru.

Gabu looked at the tidy path. "Did you sweep?"

"Yes," said Dango.

Gabu sat down on his bench. He was still tired-looking, but calmer. He held his cup of tea that Daru brought him.

"Thank you," he said.

Dango sat beside him. It did not say anything. It did not ask if he was all right. It just sat.

After a while, Gabu said: "This is a good bench."

"Yes," said Dango.

They sat in the morning light and said nothing more.

Sometimes being there was the most helpful thing.
