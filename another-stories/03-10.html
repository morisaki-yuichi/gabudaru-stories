March 10

After the Rain

🐢 Gabu

This morning, after yesterday's big rain, the garden is sparkling.

Every leaf has drops of water on it. The drops catch the morning sun. Each one is a tiny light.

Gabu walks slowly. He does not want to shake the drops off things. He wants to look at them.

He goes to the big tree. The leaves have drops on them. He looks closely at one drop. He can see his garden in it. Tiny, upside down, curved. The tree, the pond, the sky. All inside one drop.

"Amazing," says Gabu.

He goes to the rose bush on the east wall. The rose leaves have drops too. The red petals have drops. Each drop is a small perfect ball.

He goes to the spider web near the low branch of the tree. The web is covered in drops. Each thread of the web has a line of small drops. It looks like a necklace.

He looks at it for a long time.

"The spider will be happy," he says.

He goes to the pond. The pond is higher after the rain. The water is at the very edge of the grass.

The frog is out. Happy.

Gabu sits by the pond. The sun is warm. The drops are drying slowly. Disappearing.

He thinks: soon all the drops will be gone. The sun will take them.

He thinks: I am glad I saw them before they went.

He thinks: beautiful things do not always last. But while they are here, they are real.

He looks at the web one more time. Still sparkling.

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He is glad he went out this morning.

He sleeps.
