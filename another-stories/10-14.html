October 14

The Night Before

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

That night, Gabu did not sleep well.

He lay in his sleeping spot and listened to the quiet. The fire had gone low. Daru and Dango were asleep.

He was thinking.

He had lived in this garden alone for a long time. Then Daru had come, and the garden changed. Then Dango had come, and it changed again.

He had not expected either of them. And both of them had been exactly right.

Now there was something else nearby. Something on the path.

He had learned to trust the garden. It brought what was needed. Not always when you expected. Not always in the form you imagined. But it brought things.

He got up quietly and went to the window.

The night was cold and clear. The gate was closed for the night. The garden was silver in the moonlight.

The vine on the gate had lost most of its leaves now. The branches were dark and twisted. But a few red leaves still clung on.

Gabu looked at the gate for a long time.

Then he went back to his sleeping spot and lay down.

He did not sleep right away. But he was calm.

In the morning, they would open the gate again.

Whatever was on the path would come when it was ready.

Things always did.

He closed his eyes and listened to the quiet house — two sleeping friends, a warm fire-smell, the garden outside.

He felt something coming. Like a smell before rain. Like the moment before a door opens.
