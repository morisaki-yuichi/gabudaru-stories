April 19

Gabu Explains

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru has many things to learn about the garden. Gabu teaches him.

He teaches him: the pond is deepest in the middle. Do not try to touch the bottom there.

He teaches him: the berries on the east wall will be ripe in late summer. Wait for them. Do not eat them early. Early they are sour and make your stomach hurt.

He teaches him: the far corner gets very cold at night, even in summer. If you want to sleep warm, sleep near the big tree.

He teaches him: the frog will come to the pond every warm day. Say hello but give it space. Frogs do not like to be touched.

He teaches him: the gate in the wall can be opened. It is not locked. But there is a whole world out there. Think before you go.

He teaches him: the birds in the tree are friendly. They will not hurt you. But if you go too near the nest, they may call loudly.

He teaches him: the lichen on the wall takes a very long time to grow. It should not be disturbed.

Daru listens to everything. He asks questions.

"What if I accidentally touch the lichen?"

"Try not to," says Gabu. "But if you do, it will grow back. Slowly."

"What if I eat the early berries by mistake?"

"Your stomach will hurt for one day. Then you will be fine. And you will remember not to do it again."

Daru thinks about this.

"You learned all this yourself?" he says.

"Yes," says Gabu. "Over many years."

Daru looks at Gabu.

"I'm glad you are here to teach me," he says.

Gabu is glad too.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
