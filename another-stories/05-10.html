May 10

Gabu's Favorite Rock

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Gabu shows Daru the old stone in the corner.

Not the flat stone. The big rough stone. The one covered in lichen. The one he climbs to see the garden.

He climbs up first. "Come," he says.

Daru looks at the stone. He tries to climb. He slips back.

He tries again. Gabu watches from the top.

"Push with your back feet," says Gabu.

Daru tries. He gets halfway up.

"Keep going," says Gabu.

Daru keeps going. He reaches the top.

He stands on the old stone next to Gabu.

He looks out.

He sees the garden from above. He has never seen it like this.

"Oh," says Daru.

"Yes," says Gabu.

Daru turns slowly. He looks in every direction. He sees the flowers. The tree. The pond. The apple tree. The walls. The gate.

"You can see everything," says Daru.

"Almost everything," says Gabu.

Daru looks over the wall. "And outside too."

"A little," says Gabu.

They stand on the stone together. The afternoon light falls on the garden below.

Daru says: "This is your favorite rock."

"Yes," says Gabu.

Daru says: "I see why."

They stand together for a while.

Then they climb down slowly.

Gabu helps Daru find the right footholds.

At the bottom, Daru says: "Thank you for showing me."

Gabu says: "It is better with someone to share it."

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
