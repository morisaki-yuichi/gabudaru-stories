April 13

The Blue Stone

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru finds the blue stone today.

He is walking near Gabu's flat stone when he sees it. He stops.

"What is this?" says Daru. He leans down close to the stone.

"I found it in February," says Gabu. "Near the pond. I do not know where it came from."

Daru looks at it. He turns it over. He looks at it from every side.

"It is beautiful," says Daru. "I have never seen a blue stone."

"I had not either," says Gabu.

Daru puts his nose right against it. "It smells like water," he says.

Gabu did not know that. He goes close and smells it too.

Yes. A faint smell. Like water. Like the pond on a clear day.

"You are right," says Gabu. "How did I not notice that before?"

Daru looks pleased. "Maybe you need new eyes sometimes."

They both look at the blue stone.

Gabu says: "You can pick it up if you like."

Daru tries. He nudges it with his nose. He pushes it a little. It rolls.

"It rolls very well," says Daru.

They roll it back and forth between them. Not fast. Slowly, gently. Back and forth.

It is a game, Gabu thinks. We are playing a game.

He has never played a game before. Not like this.

He rolls the stone to Daru. Daru rolls it back.

Back and forth, in the afternoon sun.

When they are tired, they put the stone back next to the flat stone.

Gabu looks at his collection.

He thinks: the blue stone brought Daru, in a way. Or Daru brought the blue stone's secret.

He closes his eyes.

He smiles.

He sleeps.
