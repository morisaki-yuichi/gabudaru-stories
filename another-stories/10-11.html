October 11

The Sound Gets Closer

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Two evenings later, the sound came again.

Closer this time.

They were at dinner when Daru said: "Listen."

They all went still.

From outside the wall: a clear high voice. Singing a short phrase. A pause. The same phrase again.

"It is a song," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Who sings while walking in October?" Daru said.

"Someone cheerful," Dango said.

The singing was not far. It was just outside the wall, moving slowly along the path.

Dango went to the gate. "Should I open it?" it said.

Gabu was quiet for a moment. "Leave it for tonight," he said. "But tomorrow morning, we will open the gate and watch the path."

Dango came back from the gate. The singing had moved on and grown faint again.

It stood by the fire and thought.

It had been here when Daru arrived. It knew what it felt like to be on the outside — on the path, looking for a place. For a voice. For an open gate.

"Gabu," it said. "What if they come to the gate?"

"Then we open it," Gabu said.

Dango nodded. That was the right answer.

"I will listen very hard tomorrow," it said.

Daru nodded too. He remembered his own walk down the path. He remembered the sound of voices that had led him here.

The fire crackled. Outside, the wind moved through the bare branches.

Someone was nearby.
