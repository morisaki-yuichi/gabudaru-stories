September 11

The Pond Gets Still

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

The pond changed in September.

In summer, it had been busy — full of dragonflies, fish moving near the surface, frogs jumping in, turtles floating. Always something moving.

Now, in September, it was stiller.

The dragonflies were mostly gone. The fish had gone deeper. Mido the frog came less often. The pond plants had thinned.

Gabu sat beside the pond in the morning.

Daru came and sat beside him.

"It feels like the pond is thinking," Daru said.

Gabu looked at the water. The surface was very still. He could see the sky in it perfectly — the blue and the few white clouds.

"I know what you mean," he said.

Dango came and sat on the other side.

"Where did the fish go?" Dango said.

"Deeper," Gabu said. "The water is cooler. They like the warmer deeper parts."

"Will they come back up?"

"In spring," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the still pond. "The pond is doing what the garden does," it said. "Getting ready to rest."

Gabu nodded. "Yes. Everything is getting ready in its own way."

They sat by the still pond together. The reflection of the clouds moved slowly across it. A single leaf fell from the apple tree and landed on the surface.

It floated.

Dango watched it. It was the only moving thing.

"I am going to miss summer," Dango said.

"I know," said Gabu. "It will come back."

"Next year," Dango said.

"Yes. Next year."

Dango watched the leaf float and decided that next year was close enough to bear.
