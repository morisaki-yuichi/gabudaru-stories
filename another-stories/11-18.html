November 18

Working Together, Quiet

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Some days had no events.

On those days, everyone did their own work.

This was one of those days.

Gabu worked on the journal, writing his observations for the week. The pond ice in the morning. The birds that had come. The way the compost pile was shrinking as it worked.

Daru cleaned and sorted the shelf of tools — the small garden implements, the clay-working tools, the rope and cord.

Suke-san had made a new small project: weaving a mat from dried plant stalks. Its hands moved rhythmically.

Dango was writing on a piece of paper. It wrote big and then small and then big again. It crossed out many things. It was trying to write its own story.

Nobody talked much.

The fire made sound. The wind outside made sound.

Suke-san hummed very softly — not a whole song. Just a note now and then.

In the afternoon, Gabu made tea. He set it on the table without calling anyone.

One by one, they came and took a cup without speaking.

They stood with their cups and looked out the window at the grey November garden.

Then they went back to their work.

Dango looked up from its paper. "Is this what a family is?" it said.

No one had a quick answer.

Then Gabu said: "I think it is one kind."

Dango went back to its paper. It had been trying to write the right word at the top of the page.

It wrote: Family.

It looked at the word. Then it kept writing.
