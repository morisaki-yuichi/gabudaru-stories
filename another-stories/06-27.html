June 27

Gabu Teaches Patience

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

There was a bird that visited the garden.

It was a small brown bird with a yellow belly. It always came to the same spot, near the big stones by the wall. It pecked at the ground, looking for seeds or insects.

Daru wanted to see it up close. He tried to walk toward it. But every time he moved, the bird flew away.

"It is too fast," Daru said.

"You are too loud," Gabu said. He was not being unkind. He was explaining.

"How do I see it up close?" Daru said.

"Sit still," Gabu said. "Here, by this stone. And wait."

Daru sat down. He tried to be still. But he kept shifting. He kept turning his head.

"Be still," Gabu said softly. "Like a stone."

Daru tried again. He made himself very still. His breathing slowed. He watched the ground without moving his eyes.

After a long time, the bird came back.

It landed on the stone. It hopped forward. It was very close now — close enough to see its bright black eye, its yellow belly, the quick way it moved its head.

Daru did not breathe.

The bird pecked at the ground. It found something. It ate it. It hopped closer to Daru, looked at him with one eye.

Then it flew away.

Daru let out his breath slowly.

"I saw it," he said.

"You waited," Gabu said. "That is the only way."

Daru watched the empty stone. He would remember this: if you want something to come to you, you have to be still.
