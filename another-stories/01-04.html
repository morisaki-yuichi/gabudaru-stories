January 4

The Pond

🐢 Gabu

It is a cold January morning. The sun is low in the sky. It gives a little light but not much warmth.

Gabu walks to the pond. He goes slowly because the ground is slippery near the water. He is careful.

The pond is round and still. In summer, dragonflies fly above it. Frogs sit on the stones at the edge. Now it is empty and quiet. Just water and sky.

Gabu looks into the water. He can see clouds in it. He can see the bare branches of the big tree. Everything in the water is upside down. He finds this interesting.

He puts one foot in. The water is very cold. He takes his foot out quickly.

"Too cold," he says.

He sits at the edge of the pond. He watches. A dead leaf falls from somewhere and lands on the water. It floats. It moves slowly to the far edge.

Gabu watches the leaf all the way across. It takes a long time.

He thinks about summer. In summer he goes into the pond. The water is warm. He floats on the top. He likes summer.

But it is January now. He must wait.

"I can wait," says Gabu. He is good at waiting.

He sits by the pond until his feet are cold. Then he walks back to his flat stone. He stands on it. The stone keeps him just a little off the cold ground.

He looks at the pond from far away. It is beautiful, even in winter. It catches the light and holds it.

Gabu is glad he has a pond in his garden. He closes his eyes. He is content.
