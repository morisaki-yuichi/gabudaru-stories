January 17

Gabu Thinks

🐢 Gabu

Today is a grey day. Gabu does not walk much. He sits on his flat stone. He thinks.

He thinks about his shell. His shell is always with him. He does not need to carry it. It is just part of him. But sometimes he thinks about it. He is glad to have it. When he is cold, it helps. When he is afraid, he can go inside.

He thinks about the garden. He has been here for a long time. He does not remember coming here. He was very small when he came. The garden was already here. The big tree was already here. The wall. The pond. The flat stone.

He thinks: was someone here before me? Before my time?

He does not know the answer.

He thinks about the gate in the wall. Somewhere, outside the gate, there is a world. He has never been there. He wonders what it is like.

He thinks about spring. When the flowers come up. When the bees come. When the pond gets warm. When the birds come to the garden. He likes spring.

He thinks about his favorite food. Ripe berries from the vine on the wall. Sweet and soft. He cannot have them now. But he can remember them.

He sits and thinks for a long time.

A bird comes to the big tree. It sits and sings. Gabu listens.

He thinks: I am lucky. I have a garden. I have food. I have a warm stone. I have the bird's song.

He does not have a friend. But he does not think about that today.

He closes his eyes.

He is warm. He is peaceful.

He sleeps on his stone.
