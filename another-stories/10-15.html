October 15

Suke-san!

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Dango was sitting in the gate.

It had been there for an hour in the morning cold, wrapped in an extra leaf, watching the path.

Gabu was inside making breakfast. Daru was by the pond.

Then Dango heard footsteps.

They were slow and regular. Coming around the bend in the path. Getting louder.

Dango sat perfectly still.

Around the bend came a turtle.

It was not small like Dango. It was not medium like Daru. It was older-looking, with a large smooth shell the color of deep water and a face with kind creased eyes. It walked with a slow, careful step. And under its arm, it was carrying a small bundle.

The turtle saw Dango.

They looked at each other.

The turtle stopped.

"Hello," said Dango.

"Hello," said the turtle. Its voice was warm and unhurried. "I heard this was a garden. With friends."

"Yes," said Dango. It stepped back to make room. "Come in."

The turtle looked at the gate. At the garden beyond. At Dango's face.

"My name is Suke-san," the turtle said.

"I am Dango," said Dango. "This is Gabu's garden. He will want to meet you. And Daru."

Suke-san stepped through the gate.

Inside, Dango called: "Gabu! Daru!"

They came. And they saw Suke-san standing in the October garden with a small bundle under one arm and a warm smile on its face.

"Welcome," said Gabu. "You found us."

"I heard singing," Dango said later.

"Yes," said Suke-san. "I sing when I walk. I hope it was not a bother."

"No," said Dango. "It was how we knew you were coming."
