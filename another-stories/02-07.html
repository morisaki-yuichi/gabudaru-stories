February 7

A Butterfly

🐢 Gabu
0:00

Gabu does not expect to see a butterfly today.

It is February. It is cold. But the sun is warm this afternoon and he is sitting on his flat stone with his eyes closed.

When he opens his eyes, there is a butterfly on his shell.

He goes very still.

The butterfly has wings the color of pale gold. They open and close slowly. Open and close.

He looks at it. He does not move.

The butterfly is resting on the warm curve of his shell. He thinks: my shell is warm from the sun. The butterfly likes warm things.

He understands this. He likes warm things too.

They stay like this for a long time. The butterfly on his shell, wings opening and closing. Gabu sitting very still.

He says, very softly: "Hello."

The butterfly opens its wings once more. Then it flies away. Up, over the wall, gone.

Gabu watches it go.

He thinks about the butterfly. Where was it hiding in the cold? Under a leaf? In a crack in the wall? He did not know butterflies came in February.

But this one did.

He thinks: maybe the warm day was too good to stay inside. Maybe the butterfly felt the sun and had to come out.

He understands that too.

He walks to the south wall. He looks at his flowers.

"A butterfly came today," he tells them.

He looks at the garden. The sun is golden in the afternoon. It catches the pond and the stones and the flowers.

He thinks: this is a beautiful day.

He closes his eyes.

He is very happy.

He sleeps.
