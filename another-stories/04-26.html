April 26

Daru Runs

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Daru runs across the garden.

Well, Daru moves faster than a turtle usually moves. He cannot really run. But he moves quite quickly. For a turtle.

Gabu watches.

Daru goes from the flat stone to the pond. Quickly. Then from the pond to the apple tree. Quickly. Then from the apple tree back to the flat stone.

He stops in front of Gabu.

He is a little out of breath. For a turtle.

"I ran," says Daru.

"I saw," says Gabu.

"I wanted to see how fast I could go," says Daru.

Gabu says: "Not very fast."

They both know this. They are turtles.

Daru says: "But faster than yesterday?"

Gabu thinks about it. "Maybe."

Daru looks pleased.

He says: "In the fields before I came here, I had to move quickly sometimes. When it was cold and I needed to find shelter before dark."

"That is why you are faster than me," says Gabu.

"You have always been safe in your garden," says Daru. "You did not need to be fast."

Gabu thinks about this.

He says: "That is true. I have always been safe here."

He is grateful for that. Truly.

He looks at Daru. Daru walked across fields, quickly, alone, to find this garden. To find him.

He says: "Thank you for coming quickly."

Daru looks at him. Then he laughs a little.

"I came as fast as I could," says Daru.

Gabu is glad.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
