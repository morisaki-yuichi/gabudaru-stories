August 14

Daru Teaches Something

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

Daru had something he wanted to teach.

He had been thinking about it for a while. He was not a teacher. Gabu was the teacher. But there was something he knew that the others did not.

He brought Dango to the edge of the pond in the morning.

"What are we doing?" Dango said.

"I am going to show you how to be still," Daru said. "Really still. For a long time."

Dango looked at him. "I know how to be still," it said.

"For a minute," Daru said. "I mean for a long time. The way Gabu can."

Dango looked at Gabu, who was sitting quietly by the house. Very still.

"How long?" Dango said.

"As long as you need," Daru said.

They sat side by side at the edge of the pond. Daru showed Dango: breathe slowly. Let your body settle. Do not fight the urge to move — just notice it and let it pass.

Dango tried.

Five minutes. Ten. The ants moved across the stones. A bird landed nearby and pecked at the ground.

The bird came very close. It looked at Dango with one eye. Dango held still.

The bird pecked at something right in front of Dango's foot. Then flew away.

Dango let out a long, slow breath.

"Fourteen minutes," Daru said. He had been counting.

Dango's eyes were wide. "I did not know I could do that," it said.

"You can do more than you know," said Daru. "We both can."
