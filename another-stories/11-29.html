November 29

Dango Thanks Everyone

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

In the evening, Dango did something unusual.

It stood up.

Everyone looked at it.

"I want to say something," Dango said.

They waited.

Dango was quiet for a moment. It had planned what to say. But now it was harder than expected.

"When I came here," it said, "I was moving all the time. I did not stop because I was afraid if I stopped, I would not be welcome. I would not belong."

It looked at Gabu. "You said I was welcome before you knew me. Just because I was here."

Gabu nodded.

"You taught me patience," Dango said to Daru. "When I could not sit still. You sat beside me and showed me how."

Daru was still.

"You—" Dango looked at Suke-san. "You came later than me. But you made the house feel complete. When you came, I understood what I had been missing when I was walking alone. It was not just a place. It was people."

Suke-san said nothing. But its eyes were very warm.

Dango sat back down.

It was quiet for a moment.

"Thank you," Dango said. "All of you."

Gabu looked at the fire.

Daru cleared his throat, which was not usual for him.

Suke-san said: "Thank you for sitting in the gate."

They sat together in the warm November house, and the fire said what none of them had words for.
