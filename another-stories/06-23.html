June 23

Picking Apples — Not Yet

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru looked at the apple tree.

The apples were green and hard. They were bigger than last month, but still very small.

"When will the apples be ready?" Daru asked.

"Not until late summer," Gabu said. "Maybe September."

Daru looked at the apples. He reached up and touched one. It was firm and cool.

"That is a long time," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

Daru was quiet. Then he said, "What if I am not patient enough?"

Gabu smiled. "Then you will eat a green apple," he said. "And it will taste very sour. And then you will wait."

Daru looked at the apple in his hand. He put it back on the branch carefully.

"I will wait," he said.

"Good," said Gabu.

They sat under the tree. The apples hung above them, small and green and full of future sweetness.

"How do you know when they are ready?" Daru asked.

"They turn red," Gabu said. "Or yellow. And when you touch one, it comes off the branch easily. It does not need to be pulled."

"It lets go," Daru said.

"Yes. When it is ready, it lets go."

Daru thought about this. He liked the idea that the apple itself would know when the time was right. He thought everything should work that way — that things are ready when they let go.

He looked at the small green apples and felt happy thinking about autumn.
