November 23

What the Garden Looks Like in November

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu had been watching the garden all his life.

But this November, he saw it through new eyes — Dango's eyes, a little, and Suke-san's eyes, and Daru's steady eyes.

He wrote in the journal:

"November garden. The apple tree is bare and shows its true shape — the way it bends west. I never noticed that before Dango pointed it out. The rose bush is like dark wire. The pond is a small grey mirror.

Under the soil: the seeds Suke-san planted. Their names unknown. Waiting.

The buried letters from September. Four of them now.

The compost, working quietly, turning this year's dead things into next year's food.

Under the pond's thin ice: three fish, very still.

Under the mud at the pond's edge: Mido, breathing once every few minutes.

This is what November looks like. Empty from above. Full underneath."

He read this over.

Then he added one more line: "Four friends inside, warm."

He showed it to Dango.

Dango read it. It was quiet for a moment.

"I did not know I helped you see the tree better," it said.

"You did," said Gabu.

Dango thought about this. Without knowing, just by being itself — curious, noticing, surprised — it had changed how Gabu saw his own garden.

"The garden sees differently with more eyes," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "More eyes see more."
