December 25

A Gift Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

They gave each other small things.

Not planned — it just happened.

Daru gave Gabu the piece of river glass he had carried since before he came here. The green, sea-worn piece from his shelf.

Gabu looked at it. "I cannot take this," he said. "It is yours. You carried it all the way here."

"That is why I give it now," Daru said. "I do not need to carry it anymore."

Gabu held the glass. It was smooth and cool. Someone had carried this for a long way.

Suke-san gave Dango the braided rope it had first made — the one that had shown them the braiding technique.

Dango held it. "Your first rope here," it said.

"Yes," said Suke-san.

Dango had nothing prepared. But it thought fast. It went to its shelf. It took the clay turtle it had made in August — round and a little clumsy.

It gave it to Suke-san.

Suke-san held the small clay turtle. Its shell was round and the legs were imprecise. But the person who made it was clear in every curve.

"This is you," Suke-san said.

"Yes," said Dango.

They put the gifts in new places — the river glass on Gabu's windowsill. The braided rope around Dango's shelf post. The clay turtle on Suke-san's shelf beside the map-stone.

Gabu looked at all of this.

"Good gifts," he said. "Not new things. True things."
