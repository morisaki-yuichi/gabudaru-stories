July 24

Dango Helps in the Garden

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Gabu was working in the garden.

He was clearing away old leaves from around the base of the plants. The leaves were starting to rot and making the soil too wet.

Dango came and watched.

"Can I help?" it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "You can carry the leaves to the compost pile."

Dango got to work. It picked up armfuls of leaves and carried them across the garden. Back and forth, back and forth.

Daru came and started helping too. He worked along a different row.

The three of them worked together through the morning. Gabu dug and loosened the soil. Daru pulled out small weeds. Dango carried everything to the compost pile.

By lunchtime, the garden beds looked clean and fresh.

Dango stood at the compost pile and looked back at the garden. It had put many things in that pile this morning. The pile was bigger.

"The pile is useful," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Everything goes in. It all breaks down. And then it becomes food for next year's plants."

Dango looked at the pile. "So the old leaves come back as new things," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Nothing is wasted."

Dango thought about this for a while. It liked the idea very much.

"I want to help in the garden every day," it said.

"Then you will," Gabu said. "There is always something to do."

Dango nodded and felt a small warm feeling: the feeling of being useful.
