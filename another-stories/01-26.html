January 26

Longer Days

🐢 Gabu
0:00

Gabu has noticed something. He has been watching for a few weeks now. The days are getting longer.

In December, the sun went down very early. He had to stop walking before he was ready. There was not enough light.

But now the sun stays up a little longer each day. Not much. Just a few minutes. But Gabu notices.

He walks to his flat stone in the afternoon. The sun is still up. He sits and counts slowly. The sun is still up. He walks to the pond. The sun is still up.

"Longer," he says. "Every day a little longer."

He thinks about what this means. It means spring is coming. Not soon. Not today or tomorrow. But it is coming.

Every day a little longer. Every day a little warmer.

He feels something like excitement. He is not good at feeling excited. He is a turtle. Turtles are slow and patient. But he feels a little warm inside, a little stirring.

"Spring," he says.

He walks to the yellow flower. It is still there. It is a little bigger now.

"You know too," he says to the flower. "You know the days are longer."

He walks to the gate and looks through the crack. Outside the gate, the path is in the long afternoon sunlight. He can see it clearly. The path is golden.

He thinks: one day I will go out that gate. When the time is right.

He turns and walks back into his garden.

He is warm.

He is happy.

He closes his eyes and lets the long afternoon sun warm his shell.

He rests.
