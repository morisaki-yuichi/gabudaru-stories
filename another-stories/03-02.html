March 2

New Leaves

🐢 Gabu

Today Gabu watches leaves.

He stands under the big tree. He looks up. Yesterday there were tiny leaves. Today there are more of them. Still very small. Still pale green. But more.

He picks one branch and watches it all morning. Does it move? Does it grow?

He cannot see it growing. But he knows it is.

He walks to every plant in the garden and looks at its leaves.

The climbing plant on the east wall: bright green heart-shaped leaves, some small, some just starting.

The rose bush: red-green leaves, rolled up tight, beginning to unfurl.

The apple tree: pink-green leaves, soft and new.

The herbs near the south wall: grey-green leaves, small and fragrant.

He goes to each one and looks closely. He smells them. New leaves have a clean, green smell.

He smells them all.

He thinks: every leaf is a little different. Different shape. Different shade of green. Different smell.

He thinks about how many leaves will be on the big tree when it is fully green. Hundreds. Maybe thousands. Each one a little different.

He looks up at the sky through the branches. The blue comes through in pieces, framed by the new pale leaves.

It is very beautiful.

He sits under the tree all afternoon. He watches the leaves move in the soft wind.

He thinks: this is one of my favorite things. New leaves in spring.

He is warm. The air is warm. The ground is warm.

He closes his eyes.

The leaves move softly above him.

He sleeps under his tree.
