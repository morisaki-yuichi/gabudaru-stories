January 11

Under the Leaves

🐢 Gabu

In the corner of the garden, near the wall, there is a pile of old leaves. They fell in autumn. They are brown now, and some are soft and wet.

Gabu goes to look at the leaf pile today.

He puts his nose into the leaves. They smell of earth and rain and something old and good. He pushes his way inside a little. The leaves close around him. It is warm in here.

He sits very still. He listens.

Under the leaves: rustling. Something small is there. He waits.

A small beetle comes out from under a leaf. It is shiny and dark. It walks quickly across the top of the leaves. Then it disappears again.

"Hello, beetle," says Gabu.

He finds a snail in the leaves. The snail is in its shell, pulled all the way in. The shell is pale brown with a spiral pattern.

"Are you sleeping?" asks Gabu.

The snail does not move.

"That is all right," says Gabu. "I sometimes want to sleep too."

He sits in the warm leaves for a long time. It is comfortable here. Protected from the wind. The leaves are soft.

He thinks: things hide in winter. Beetles. Snails. Seeds. They are not gone. They are just waiting.

He feels a little like that too. He is in his garden, waiting for warmer days. But he is all right.

In the afternoon, the sun reaches the leaf pile. It warms the leaves a little. Gabu pushes further in.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps in the warm leaves until evening. Then he goes back to his flat stone.

He is comfortable. He is not afraid.
