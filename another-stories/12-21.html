December 21

One Minute More

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

The day after the solstice.

Dango was watching for one extra minute of light.

Of course, one minute was not something you could see. But Dango watched anyway.

"Is it longer today?" it asked at breakfast.

"One minute," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the window. The morning light was coming in.

"I cannot tell," it said.

"You will not be able to tell for weeks," Gabu said. "It is one minute a day at first. Very slow."

Dango thought about one minute a day. Over weeks, that became hours. Over months, that became summer.

"It is slow," Dango said.

"Everything good is slow," said Gabu.

Suke-san had been listening. It said: "I once walked a very long path. Every day I could not see how much closer I was getting. But I kept walking."

"And then you arrived," Dango said.

"Here, yes," said Suke-san.

Dango thought about the one minute of light. Each day, one minute. Invisible. Building.

"The year is walking toward summer," Dango said.

Gabu looked at it. "Yes. That is exactly what it is doing."

"One minute at a time," said Daru.

"One minute at a time," said Dango.

It was not visible yet. But it was real. The year was moving. The light was returning.

One minute, one step, one day at a time.
