August 16

A Story Before Sleep

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

It had become a small custom.

Before sleeping, one of them told a short story. It had started by accident — Dango had told a story one evening and Gabu had said, "Good. We should do this every night." And so they did.

Tonight was Gabu's turn.

He said:

"Once there was a very old mountain. It was so old it had forgotten being young. It had been a mountain for longer than the rivers, longer than the forests.

One day, a seed landed on the mountain. It was a very small seed. The wind had brought it from far away.

The seed grew into a tree. It grew for fifty years. It grew very tall.

When the tree was old, a bird came and made a nest in it. The bird sang every morning.

The mountain had been alone for a very long time. But now it had a tree, and the tree had a bird, and the bird had a song.

The mountain could not sing. But it could feel the vibrations of the song through the tree's roots.

And it thought: this is what it means to have a friend."

Gabu stopped.

It was quiet for a moment.

"Is that the end?" Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"I liked it," Daru said.

Dango thought about the mountain feeling the vibrations of the bird's song through the roots. It thought about how Gabu had been alone for so long.

"Gabu," it said softly.

"Yes?"

"You are the mountain."

Gabu was quiet. Then he said: "Go to sleep, Dango."
