July 31

The Last Day of July

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

The last day of July was hot and sweet.

The garden was at its most full. The rose bush had given its last big flowers, and they were starting to fall. The apple tree had many large round apples, still green but getting bigger. The pond was ringed with tall green plants.

Gabu, Daru, and Dango spent the morning in the pond.

At noon, they ate under the apple tree. Gabu had made cold things — cucumber, light bread, cold herb tea. They ate slowly in the shade.

Dango looked up at the apples hanging above them.

"They look riper," it said.

Gabu looked up too. "They are getting there. Another month, maybe."

"I will be here," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

In the afternoon, they walked the ant road and watched it for a while. They visited Mido at the pond. They sat in the shelter and listened to a bee somewhere in the rose bush, working in the last of the petals.

As the sun went low and gold, Daru said: "July was a good month."

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Dango came in July," Daru said.

Dango, who was lying on its back in the grass, raised one foot. "That was me," it said.

"The best thing in July," said Daru.

Dango put its foot down. It was quiet for a moment. Then it said: "I think so too."

They lay in the golden light of late July, three turtles in a garden, listening to the last bee of the day.
