April 11

Getting to Know Daru

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru spent the night in the garden. When Gabu wakes up in the morning, Daru is there. He slept near the apple tree.

Gabu walks over.

"Good morning," says Gabu.

Daru opens his eyes. "Good morning!" He is already awake and eager.

They eat breakfast together. Gabu shows Daru where the good food is. The new grass near the pond. The moss on the south wall that is tender this time of year.

Daru eats quickly. He is hungry from his journey.

"Where did you come from?" asks Gabu.

"From beyond the fields," says Daru. "Far away. I heard about this garden."

"Who told you?"

"A bird," says Daru. "A small brown bird with an orange chest."

Gabu thinks about his robin. His bird.

"She told me there was a garden," says Daru, "with a green turtle who lived there alone. She said it was a good garden."

Gabu is quiet for a moment.

He says: "It is a good garden."

After breakfast, Gabu shows Daru the whole garden. The big tree. The pond. The far corner. The old stone. The flowers on the south wall.

Daru looks at everything with wide eyes. He asks many questions.

"What kind of tree?" "How deep is the pond?" "How long have you been here?"

Gabu answers each one.

He finds he likes answering the questions. He has known these things alone for so long. Now someone wants to know.

When they finish the tour, they sit side by side on Gabu's flat stone.

Two turtles on one stone.

Gabu looks at the garden.

It looks different with two.

He closes his eyes. He is very happy.

He sleeps.
