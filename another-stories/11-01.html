November 1

November Arrives

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

November arrived like a grey curtain being drawn.

The sky was low and solid white. The trees were completely bare. The garden was brown and still. The pond was very dark.

Dango stepped outside and looked at it all.

"It is serious now," Dango said.

Gabu came and stood beside it. "Yes," he said. "November is the most serious month."

Daru came out. He looked at the sky. "Rain today," he said.

"How do you know?" Dango said.

"The smell," said Daru. "Low and heavy."

He had learned from Gabu.

Suke-san came out last, moving slowly in the cold.

The four of them stood and looked at the November garden.

It was not a sad garden. It was a resting garden. The bones of it were still there — the apple tree's silhouette, the rose bush's dark stems, the pond reflecting the white sky. Everything stripped to its essentials.

"I see the shape of things better now," Suke-san said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "Without the leaves, you see the structure."

"The garden is showing its skeleton," Dango said.

"And the skeleton is strong," Daru said.

They stood in the cold for a while, looking.

Then Gabu said: "Inside. Breakfast. It is cold."

They went in. The fire was already low from the night. Suke-san stoked it. Gabu made porridge. Daru set cups. Dango got in the way and was cheerful about it.

November had arrived. And they were warm.
