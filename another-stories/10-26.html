October 26

Teaching and Learning

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san
0:00

Something was happening in the garden.

Each of them was teaching and learning from the others at the same time.

Gabu had taught Daru to float and be still. Daru had taught Dango to be still longer. Dango had taught Suke-san where everything in the garden was. Suke-san had shown Gabu a way to mend the clay pot crack that was stronger than his own method.

Gabu had taught Dango to read the weather. Dango had taught Daru a new song — not Gabu's song, a different one, silly and fast. Daru had shown Suke-san the ant road. Suke-san had told them how to know which mushrooms were safe from the underside of the cap.

All of this had happened without anyone deciding it would.

Dango said: "We are all teachers and all students."

"Yes," said Gabu.

"What did you learn from me?" Dango said to Gabu.

Gabu thought carefully. "You showed me the garden in the dark," he said. "You called it the night garden. I had not thought of it that way before."

Dango was quiet. It had not known this.

"And from Daru?" it said.

"To write things down," Gabu said. "I kept a journal before Daru came. But watching him write the letter — that reminded me why writing matters."

Dango thought. "And from Suke-san?"

Gabu looked at Suke-san. "Patience is not the same as stillness," he said. "Patience is something deeper."

Suke-san looked at Gabu. "You already knew that," it said.

"I knew it," Gabu said. "But you reminded me."
