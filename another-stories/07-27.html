July 27

A Very Quiet Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango

Some days had no big events.

This was one of those days.

Gabu woke up, ate breakfast, and worked in the garden a little. Then he sat with his journal for an hour.

Daru swam in the pond in the morning. In the afternoon, he watched the ant road for a long time and thought about nothing.

Dango tried to catch a leaf that kept blowing away in the breeze. It did not catch it. It did not mind.

At dinner, Gabu asked: "What did you do today?"

"I swam," said Daru.

"I chased a leaf," said Dango.

"I wrote in the journal," said Gabu.

They ate. Outside, the fireflies started up over the pond. The evening was warm.

"It was a quiet day," Daru said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"I like quiet days," Daru said.

Dango nodded. It had not always liked quiet days. When it was walking alone, quiet days meant loneliness. But here, a quiet day meant something different. It meant everything was all right. It meant there was nothing wrong, nothing to fix, nothing to run from.

A quiet day here was a full day.

"I am learning to like them too," Dango said.

Gabu got up and put the dishes in the water. The fireflies blinked outside the window.

A quiet day in a garden with friends was one of the best things there was.
