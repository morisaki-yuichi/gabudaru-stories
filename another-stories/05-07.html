May 7

Daru Learns Something New

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today a butterfly lands on Daru.

He is sitting still near the south wall when it happens. A yellow butterfly. It lands on his nose.

He is very still.

He can feel the tiny weight of it. Almost nothing. But something.

The butterfly opens and closes its wings.

He breathes very slowly.

The butterfly sits there for a long moment.

Then it flies away.

Daru does not move for another minute. He stays still.

Then slowly he turns his head and looks for Gabu.

Gabu is nearby. Watching.

"It happened," says Daru.

"I saw," says Gabu.

Daru looks very happy.

He says: "I felt it. Just a tiny weight. Like nothing."

"Yes," says Gabu. "Like nothing. But something."

Daru thinks about this.

He says: "Small things feel big when you are paying attention."

Gabu says: "Yes. Exactly."

Daru looks at the south wall flowers. He looks at each flower carefully. He really looks.

He says: "I usually see flowers. But just now I really saw them. Each petal. The colors."

Gabu says: "Being still helps you see more."

Daru nods slowly.

He says: "This is a good thing to learn."

Gabu says: "It is one of the most useful things."

They sit together.

Daru is already better at being still than he was a week ago.

Gabu is pleased.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
