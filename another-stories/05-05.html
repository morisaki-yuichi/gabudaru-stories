May 5

Children's Day

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today is a special day. Gabu does not know the human word for it. But he can feel something.

Outside the wall, there are sounds. Voices. Laughter. Distant music.

He and Daru go to the gate. They listen.

Children's voices. High and clear. Somewhere not far away.

They look through the crack. They can see the path. Beyond the path, a field. In the field, shapes. Small shapes, moving.

"Children," says Daru.

"Yes," says Gabu.

They watch through the crack. The children run. They shout. They laugh.

One child finds something in the grass and holds it up. Gabu cannot see what it is. But the child is happy about it.

"I saw children once before," says Daru. "In the fields, before I came here."

"What were they like?" asks Gabu.

"Fast," says Daru. "Very fast. And loud. But not in a bad way. In a happy way."

Gabu watches a child run across the field. Arms out. Running for the joy of it.

He thinks: that looks happy.

He thinks about children and about turtles. Children are small like him but very fast. And they talk very fast.

He thinks: different creatures are happy in different ways.

The sounds of the children gradually get quieter. They have moved away.

He and Daru walk away from the gate.

"They seemed happy," says Daru.

"Yes," says Gabu. "It is a happy day out there."

He walks to his flat stone.

He closes his eyes.

He smiles.

He sleeps.
