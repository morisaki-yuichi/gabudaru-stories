April 24

Daru Has a Question

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Daru has many questions every day. But today he has one big question.

He comes to Gabu in the morning.

"Gabu," he says. "What was it like before I came? Were you very lonely?"

Gabu sits with this question for a moment.

He says: "Yes. Sometimes. Not all the time. But sometimes."

Daru says: "I am sorry."

Gabu says: "Don't be sorry. I am fine. And now you are here."

Daru says: "But all those months in winter. Alone in the cold."

Gabu thinks about January and February and March. The cold mornings. The quiet days. Walking to the pond alone. Looking at two birds and thinking about what it would be like to have a friend.

"I was all right," he says. "I had my garden. My stones. The frog. The birds. The flowers."

Daru says: "But you wanted someone."

"Yes," says Gabu honestly. "I wanted someone."

Daru is quiet.

Then he says: "I wanted someone too. That is why I walked to this garden. I heard about it and I walked for many days to find it."

Gabu looks at him. "You walked many days?"

"Many days. Through the fields and over the stream and along the path. By myself."

Gabu thinks about this. Daru came to him.

"I am glad you came," says Gabu.

"I am glad too," says Daru.

They sit together in the morning sun.

The question is answered. Not fully. But enough.

Gabu closes his eyes.

He is warm.

He sleeps.
