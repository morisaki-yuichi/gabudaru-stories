December 7

The First Snow

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

It came at night.

Gabu woke up in the quiet and knew immediately. Something was different about the silence. Heavier. Fuller.

He went to the window.

White.

Everything white.

It had fallen while they slept — soft and steady. The garden was transformed. The brown and bare had become a single surface of white. The apple tree's branches each held a line of snow. The pond was white. The path was white.

Gabu went and woke the others.

"Snow," he said.

They came to the windows.

Dango pressed both hands on the glass.

"It came," it said. Its voice was very quiet.

It came.

They went outside together, wrapped in everything warm.

Dango's foot went into the snow. It was ankle-deep. The crunch was very satisfying.

Dango looked at its footprint. Perfect. Deep. Real.

It took another step. Another.

It looked back. A trail of footprints.

"I made footprints in snow," it said.

"Yes," said Daru.

Suke-san was standing still in the middle of the garden, looking at the white everywhere with its calm eyes.

Gabu watched all of them.

The first snow. Dango's first snow.

The garden looked like something new and ancient at the same time.

"It is quiet," Dango said.

"Yes," said Daru. "Like the world is listening."

Dango stood in the white garden and listened with the world.
