November 17

Daru Reads Aloud

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu had a small number of books on his shelf.

Not many — maybe ten. But good ones, read many times.

One evening, Daru asked if he could read aloud.

"Yes," said Gabu.

Daru chose a book — old, about the sea. He sat in the chair and opened it.

He read slowly and clearly. He had a good reading voice — calm and even, like his walking pace.

The others listened.

The book was about a turtle who had crossed a great ocean. It described the ocean at night, with phosphorescence — the water glowing blue-green in the dark, lighting up with every movement.

Dango pressed its hands together. "The ocean glows?"

"Sometimes," Gabu said quietly, not wanting to interrupt.

Daru kept reading.

The turtle in the book crossed the ocean in thirty days. It arrived at an island it had been to before, long ago.

Daru read until the turtle arrived and felt the sand under its feet.

Then he stopped. "I'll stop there," he said. "That seems like a good place."

Dango let out a long breath. "The ocean glows," it said.

"In deep water, sometimes," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the book with great interest. "Are there more chapters?"

"Yes," said Daru.

"Tomorrow?" Dango said.

"If you want," said Daru.

"I want," said Dango.

Suke-san had been very still through the reading. "I have seen the glowing water," it said. Once, simply.

Everyone looked at it. But Suke-san did not add more, and that was all right.
