December 11

Reading Together

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Daru had finished reading the book about the sea.

They had read it together over many evenings. The turtle crossing the ocean. The glowing water. The island at the end.

Now it was over.

"I miss it," Dango said.

"There are other books," Gabu said.

They looked at Gabu's shelf. Ten books. Old and read many times.

"Which one next?" Dango said.

Gabu thought. He chose a thinner book — a collection of small stories, each one short. "For winter evenings," he said. "Short ones."

Daru began reading.

The first story was about a seed that took forty years to bloom. The flower, when it finally came, lasted three days.

Dango said: "That is not fair."

"Is it not?" Gabu said.

"Forty years for three days," Dango said.

"But the three days were the right three days," Suke-san said. "The flower bloomed at the right time."

Dango thought about this.

"So the waiting was not wasted," it said. "The waiting was part of it."

"Yes," said Gabu.

Daru turned the page to the next story.

Outside, December cold.

Inside, fire and voices and the small stories of a winter evening.
