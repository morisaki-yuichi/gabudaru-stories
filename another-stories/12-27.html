December 27

The Journal Is Full

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango🐱 Suke-san

Gabu turned to the last page of the journal.

"The journal is almost full," he said.

Dango came to look. The pages were dense with Gabu's small writing and Daru's writing and Dango's big enthusiastic letters and Suke-san's rare, carefully placed words.

The map Dango had drawn. The list of summer memories. The four grateful things. The wreath drawing of the first snow. The pressed leaves from September. The four brief stories.

"This whole year is in here," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"What happens when it is full?"

Gabu went to his shelf. He got down a new journal — same size, same brown cover. He set it on the table.

"A new one," Dango said.

"Every year," Gabu said.

Dango looked at the shelf. There were many old journals there, side by side. He counted them.

"Thirteen years," it said.

"Yes," said Gabu. "One journal a year, roughly."

Dango thought about thirteen journals. Thirteen years of one turtle alone in a garden. And then this one — the fourteenth — with four voices in it.

"The fourteenth journal is the fullest," Dango said.

Gabu picked it up and looked at the density of the pages.

"Yes," he said. "By far."

He closed it. He put it on the shelf at the end of the row.

Then he opened the new journal to its first page. It was blank and white.

"Next year," Dango said.

"Yes," said Gabu.
