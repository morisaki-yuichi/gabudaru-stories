July 10

Dango and the Pond

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru🐕 Dango
0:00

The first thing Dango wanted to do was see the pond up close.

It walked to the edge and looked in. The three fish were there. Dango's eyes went very wide.

"Fish!" it said.

"Yes," said Daru. "Three fish. They have always been here."

Dango crouched down. The fish came toward its reflection in the water, curious. Dango did not move.

One fish came so close that Dango could see its tiny eye.

"Oh," Dango said. Very softly. "Oh, hello."

The fish turned and swam away.

"Do they talk?" Dango said.

"Not in words," Gabu said. "But they communicate. When one turns, the others follow. That is a kind of talking."

Dango thought about this. It watched the fish move together.

Then Dango looked at the water itself. "Can I go in?"

"The water is warm at the top," Daru said. "And cool deeper down. I will come with you."

They went in together. Dango stepped carefully. The mud at the bottom was soft. Dango made a small surprised sound.

"The floor is soft," it said.

"Yes," said Daru. "You get used to it."

Dango stood in the shallow part. The water came up to its belly. It looked down at its feet in the water.

Then it looked up at Daru with an expression of pure happiness.

"This is the best pond I have ever been in," Dango said.

"Have you been in other ponds?" Daru asked.

Dango thought. "No," it said. "But I am sure this is the best."
