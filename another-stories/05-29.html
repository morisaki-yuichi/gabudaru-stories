May 29

The Old Tree

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru
0:00

Today Gabu and Daru sit under the big tree and Gabu tells Daru about it.

"This tree," says Gabu, "is the oldest thing I know."

Daru looks up.

"How old?" he asks.

"I don't know," says Gabu. "Very old. It was here long before me."

He puts his foot on one of the big roots. "These roots go very deep. Deep into the earth. Deeper than the pond."

Daru touches a root.

"They feel strong," says Daru.

"Yes," says Gabu. "The tree holds itself down with these roots. And it feeds itself through them."

Daru says: "The tree eats through its feet."

Gabu says: "Something like that."

They look up into the leaves. The light comes through in patches. Green light.

Gabu says: "I have sat under this tree in every season. In winter when it was bare. In spring when the leaves came. In summer in the shade. In autumn when the leaves turned."

Daru says: "Does it change?"

Gabu thinks. "It changes every year. But it is always the same tree."

Daru thinks about this.

He says: "Like us. We change. But we are still us."

Gabu says: "Yes. Like us."

They sit under the old tree.

The leaves move. The roots are still.

Gabu feels the roots under the ground beneath his feet.

He closes his eyes.

He feels old and rooted and here.

He sleeps.
