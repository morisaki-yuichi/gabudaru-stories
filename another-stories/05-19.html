May 19

A New Plant

🐢 Gabu🐢 Daru

Today Gabu notices a new plant growing by the gate.

Not the yellow flower from last month. That one has been there a while. This is something else. Something that was not there yesterday.

He walks to it. He looks.

A small vine. Curling around the base of the gate. Bright green. With tiny leaves.

He looks at it closely. Where did it come from?

Daru comes to look too.

"New," says Daru.

"Yes," says Gabu.

Daru sniffs it. "It smells like the gate. And something else. Something from outside."

Gabu sniffs too. Yes. A different smell. Not the garden smell.

"Another seed from outside," says Gabu. "Came under the gate."

Daru looks at the tiny vine. It is curling upward now, toward the gate.

"It wants to climb the gate," says Daru.

Gabu watches.

He thinks: the vine came from outside. Now it is inside my garden. Growing here. Becoming part of the garden.

He thinks: Daru came from outside too. Now he is inside the garden. Part of it.

He says: "Things from outside become part of this garden. The seed. You. The cat who sits on the wall sometimes."

Daru says: "The yellow flower too."

"Yes," says Gabu.

They look at the vine.

Gabu says: "Welcome."

Daru says: "Welcome."

They say it to the vine. And to each other. And to everything that came from outside and stayed.

He closes his eyes.

He smiles.

He sleeps.
