March 7

The Far Corner

🐢 Gabu

Every garden has a far corner. A place that is a little harder to get to. A little less visited.

Gabu's far corner is the northeast corner. Where the north wall and the east wall meet. The two walls make a corner there. It is shaded. A little damp. A little dark.

Gabu goes there today.

He walks along the north wall. He reaches the corner.

It is different here. Cooler than the rest of the garden. Darker. The sun does not reach here for long each day.

The ground is covered with soft, wet moss. Very green. His feet sink into it a little.

He finds many small plants here that he does not find in the sunny parts of the garden. Small dark leaves. Tiny mushrooms coming up through the moss.

He finds a large flat stone, almost hidden by the moss. He looks under it. A woodlouse curls up under there. Then another. Many small things living under that stone.

"Hello," says Gabu.

He finds a snail on the north wall. A fat, dark-shelled snail. Moving slowly upward.

"Hello," says Gabu.

He looks at the corner. He has not been here much. It feels like a different part of the garden. A different world, almost. Quieter. Wetter. Cooler.

He thinks: my garden has many worlds inside it.

He stays in the far corner for a while. He sits on the mossy ground. It is cool and soft.

He thinks: I will come here more often.

He walks back to his flat stone. He feels like he has been on a trip.

He closes his eyes.

He sleeps.
